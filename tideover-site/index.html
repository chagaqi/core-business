The long wait

It isn't one ticket. It's a months-long emotional journey.

You collected the cash today and ship in 60 to 120 days. In that gap a confident buyer slowly turns into an anxious one — and an anxious one turns into a refund request.

  1. DAY 7

    Still excited

    The confirmation-email glow. They tell friends. No questions yet.

  2. DAY 30

    First doubt

    The excitement fades. "Wait — when is this actually coming?"

  3. DAY 60

    Anxious

    No update in weeks. They Google your name with "scam" and start screenshotting.

  4. DAY 89

    Ready to bail

    One unanswered message away from a refund request — or a chargeback.

Chargebacks hit more than revenue

A refund is a lost sale. A chargeback is a dispute on your record — and enough of them put your Stripe or PayPal standing at risk.

WISMO floods the inbox

"Where's my order?" arrives in waves for months, burying the tickets that actually need you.

"Scam" reviews stick

An anxious buyer left in the dark writes the review first and asks questions later. That page outlives the wait.

Questions

Answered plainly.

Do I switch helpdesks or install anything?+
No. Tideover bolts onto the Gorgias, Tidio, or Intercom you already run. Nothing to rip out, no second inbox, no infra change — we work inside your existing setup and handle the presale tickets specifically.
How is this different from the bot already in my helpdesk?+
A generic bot doesn't know an order is a preorder, doesn't know its real production timeline, and answers a Day 80 panic the same as a Day 5 hello. Tideover is built only for the 60–120 day wait, with replies tied to where each order actually is.
Will you make promises you can't keep?+
Never. We use honest confidence bands — "currently tracking for late July," not a hard date. Every novel reply is human-approved before it sends. A false delivery promise is the most damaging thing we could say, so we don't.
What does the free pilot involve?+
For one full presale cycle, we run your presale support by hand inside your helpdesk — timeline-aware, human-approved replies in two daily windows — and build a day-by-day reassurance playbook from your real tickets, measured against your baseline.
What can you prove, and what can't you yet?+
Right now we can show leading indicators — faster first response, fewer WISMO tickets, logged saves. The full refund-reduction delta needs a complete 60–120 day cycle to be real, so we report it after the first cohort finishes. We'd rather tell you that than invent a number.
What does it cost after the pilot?+
The pilot is free. On proof, it's roughly $199–$499/mo, scaling to $799–$999+/mo as volume grows. Any performance fee waits until there's a real case study to stand on.
I'm a crowdfunding creator juggling backers and preorders — does that fit?+
That's exactly who this is for. Backers, late pledges, and new preorders all bring their own long waits, and Tideover handles that mixed presale load without you switching a thing.
