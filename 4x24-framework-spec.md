# The 4×24 Framework — Internal Playbook

**Owner:** Creative Strategy
**Scope:** AI UGC video ad production for DTC functional consumables (mushroom coffee, adaptogen gummies, greens powders, nootropics, functional beverages)
**Tool stack:** Arcads / Creatify (avatar generation) → Captions AI (edit / lipsync / captions) → GPT / Claude (scripting) → Meta Ads Manager (deploy + iterate)
**Cadence:** One full cycle every 2 weeks per client account.

---

## 0. The One-Paragraph Version

Every 2-week cycle ships **4 hypothesis ANGLES × 6 VARIATIONS = 24 deliverables** against the client's Meta account. We launch all 24, read three diagnostic metrics (thumb-stop, hold, CTR) at the right altitude, **kill losers fast, scale winners, and feed the survivors into the next cycle's hypotheses.** Because measured creative fatigue sets in around week 7, a fresh 24-creative batch every 2 weeks means a client account is *never* running on fatigued creative. The tools produce the pixels; **the judgment layer — which angle, hook, format, and CTA for a given AOV and avatar — is the moat.**

---

## 1. The 4 Hypothesis Angles

These are **hypotheses about *why* a stranger stops and buys**, not formats. Each cycle tests all four so we never over-index on one psychological lever. Anchor on these four; refine the *expression* per brand, never the four-way structure.

### Angle 1 — Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)
**Thesis:** Lead with the visceral symptom the avatar feels *today*, twist the knife, then position the product as relief.
**Lead with:** the jitters / the 2-3pm crash / the racing mind at 11pm / the post-meal bloat.
**Emotional job:** recognition ("that's literally me") → tension → relief.
**When to use:**
- **AOV:** Best for **low-to-mid AOV ($25–$45)** where the purchase is impulse-adjacent and the symptom is acute and namable.
- **Avatar:** Strongest when the avatar has a **single sharp recurring pain** with a daily trigger (MycoMornings jitters/crash; ZenChews racing mind). Weaker for vague "general wellness" wants.
- **Default:** This is the **highest-EV opener for a cold account** — symptom-led PAS almost always produces the cycle's cheapest thumb-stop.

### Angle 2 — Founder / Origin Authenticity
**Thesis:** A real human built this to solve their *own* version of the avatar's problem; trust transfers from person to product.
**Lead with:** "I built this because…" / "I was so sick of X that I…" / the moment of frustration that started the brand.
**Emotional job:** trust, anti-corporate credibility, "they get it."
**When to use:**
- **AOV:** Pulls its weight most at **mid-to-high AOV ($45+)** where the buyer needs a reason to trust before spending (DailyGreens24 $59 tub). Trust de-risks the bigger ticket.
- **Avatar:** Best for the **skeptical / been-burned-before** avatar (the health-optimizer who's tried 5 greens powders). Also the avatar who values *intention* and ingredient sourcing.
- **Note:** Highest ceiling for **brand equity + repeat purchase**, not always the cheapest first-click. Run it every cycle anyway — it's the angle that builds the brand, not just the sale.

### Angle 3 — Mechanism / Ingredient Education
**Thesis:** Explain *why it actually works* at the ingredient level so the rational buyer can justify the purchase.
**Lead with:** "Here's why lion's mane does X…" / "the reason L-theanine + ashwagandha hit different…" / "most greens are missing this one thing…"
**Emotional job:** intelligent reassurance, "I'm not being scammed," permission-to-buy for the analytical brain.
**When to use:**
- **AOV:** Scales with AOV — the **more expensive the product, the more the buyer wants the mechanism** ($59 DailyGreens24, nootropics).
- **Avatar:** Made for the **research-y optimizer** (reads labels, knows what adaptogen means). Underperforms for pure impulse/emotional buyers — keep it lean for low-AOV impulse SKUs.
- **Watch:** Easiest angle to make boring. Mechanism MUST be wrapped in a curiosity hook ("the one ingredient 90% of greens skip") or it dies on thumb-stop.

### Angle 4 — Social-Proof / Transformation Testimonial
**Thesis:** Show the *after*. A relatable person 30 days in narrates what changed.
**Lead with:** "30 days in, here's what nobody tells you…" / "I didn't expect *this* to be the thing that changed…"
**Emotional job:** aspiration + proof + FOMO ("if it worked for them…").
**When to use:**
- **AOV:** Works at **every AOV**; the universal closer. Tends to be the **best retargeting/MOF angle** (warm audiences who already know the product).
- **Avatar:** Best when the transformation is *observable and believable* (more steady energy, sleeping through the night, no 3pm crash). Avoid implausible/overclaimed transformations — compliance risk on ingestibles is real.
- **Note:** Strongest **scale-up angle** once a winner is found; the format the algorithm tends to reward with cheap reach.

### Angle Selection Cheat-Sheet

| | Low AOV ($25–40) | Mid AOV ($40–55) | High AOV ($55+) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acute single-symptom avatar** | **PAS** (lead), Transformation | PAS, Transformation, Mechanism | PAS, Mechanism, Founder |
| **Skeptical / been-burned avatar** | Founder, PAS | **Founder** (lead), Mechanism | **Founder** (lead), Mechanism |
| **Research-y optimizer avatar** | Mechanism, PAS | Mechanism, Transformation | **Mechanism** (lead), Founder |
| **Cold account, no data yet** | All 4 — PAS gets the most spend | All 4 even split | All 4, Founder + Mechanism weighted |

> Rule: **always ship all 4 angles every cycle.** The cheat-sheet decides *spend weighting and which angle leads*, not which to drop. Dropping an angle blinds the test.

## 2. The Variation Matrix — 4 Angles → 24 Deliverables

We turn each angle into **6 variations** by moving a small set of **levers**. The discipline: **change ONE primary lever at a time per variation so the read is clean.** A variation that changes three things teaches us nothing about *why* it won.

### The Levers

| Lever | Options | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Hook (first line)** | Pull from the Hook Bank (§3) | Does the *promise/pattern-interrupt* stop the scroll? |
| **B. Opening-3s visual** | (a) face-to-cam cold open · (b) product-in-hand · (c) symptom B-roll (tired at desk / phone in bed) · (d) bold text-card | Does the *first frame* earn the thumb-stop? |
| **C. Format** | `TH` talking-head · `VO` voiceover+b-roll · `LF` lo-fi text-overlay · `SS` split-screen demo · `GS` green-screen-react | Which *production style* the avatar trusts |
| **D. Runtime** | `15` (15s) · `30` (30s) | Hook-only punch vs. full argument |
| **E. CTA style** | `SHOP` direct ("link in bio / shop now") · `SOFT` ("see why people are switching") · `OFFER` ("first-order discount / bundle") · `URG` ("before the batch sells out") | Which ask converts the click |

### The Grid — exactly how 6 variations are built per angle

Each angle gets a **fixed 6-cell template**. Cells 1–4 are the **format sweep** (one hook held roughly constant to isolate format), cells 5–6 are the **hook/CTA sweep** (format held to the angle's best-guess winner to isolate hook + CTA). This balances *format discovery* and *message discovery* inside every angle.

| Var # | Hook (A) | Open-3s (B) | Format (C) | Runtime (D) | CTA (E) | Purpose of the cell |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **V01** | Hook-α | face cold-open | `TH` | `30` | `SHOP` | Baseline / control |
| **V02** | Hook-α | symptom B-roll | `VO` | `30` | `SHOP` | Format: voiceover vs. talking-head |
| **V03** | Hook-α | bold text-card | `LF` | `15` | `SOFT` | Format: lo-fi + short cut |
| **V04** | Hook-α | product-in-hand | `SS` or `GS` | `30` | `OFFER` | Format: demo/react + offer |
| **V05** | Hook-β | face cold-open | *angle's best format* | `15` | `URG` | Hook test: new hook, short |
| **V06** | Hook-γ | best-guess visual | *angle's best format* | `30` | `SOFT`/`OFFER` | Hook test: third hook, full length |

**The math:**

```
4 angles  ×  6 variations per angle  =  24 deliverables / cycle
```

Within the 24 you automatically get a balanced spread:
- **Formats:** every cycle exercises all 5 formats (TH, VO, LF, SS/GS) multiple times → format-level learning. **Production method:** talking-head formats render fast in Arcads/Creatify; character-led / product-forward / testimonial formats are best produced with the **Sora 2 consistent-character workflow** (mint one character per brand-avatar with product embedded at generation time, reuse-and-tag, change only action + dialogue) — see `28-sora2-consistent-character-workflow.md`.
- **Runtimes:** ~50/50 split of 15s and 30s → length learning.
- **Hooks:** 3 distinct hooks per angle × 4 angles = **12 unique hooks tested per cycle** → hook-bank validation.
- **CTAs:** all 4 CTA styles fire each cycle → CTA learning.

> **Why 6, not 5 or 8?** 6 keeps each angle's micro-test statistically legible at a realistic 2-week ingestibles budget (you need enough spend per creative to clear noise — see §4 thresholds). Below ~4 you can't isolate format; above ~6 you starve each creative of spend and read nothing. 6 is the sweet spot that still lands on a clean 24.

## 3. Hook Bank — 15 Plug-and-Play Templates

Proven hook *shapes* for ingestible-wellness. `[ ]` = fill per brand. Map each to its strongest angle, but most flex across two. Keep the first line under ~12 words — it's the thumb-stop.

| # | Hook template | Best angle(s) | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| **H01** | "POV: it's 2:47pm and the [crash / brain fog] just hit again." | PAS | Names the exact trigger moment; instant recognition. |
| **H02** | "If you get [jittery / anxious] from coffee, stop scrolling." | PAS | Direct call-out filters + qualifies the exact buyer. |
| **H03** | "I tried [X] every morning for 30 days. Here's what changed." | Transformation | Time-bound + curiosity gap on the payoff. |
| **H04** | "Nobody talks about the *real* reason you're [bloated / wired] every night." | PAS / Mechanism | Conspiratorial "hidden truth" frame. |
| **H05** | "I built [Brand] because I was sick of [problem]." | Founder | Origin + earned credibility in one line. |
| **H06** | "Most [greens / coffees] are missing the *one* ingredient that actually matters." | Mechanism | Curiosity gap + frames competitors as inferior. |
| **H07** | "Here's why [lion's mane / L-theanine] hits different than caffeine." | Mechanism | Mechanism wrapped in a comparison curiosity. |
| **H08** | "Stop drinking [X] if you want [calm focus / to sleep]." | PAS | Pattern-interrupt negative command. |
| **H09** | "3 things I wish I knew before I [fixed my 3pm crash]." | Mechanism / Transformation | Listicle promise = high hold rate. |
| **H10** | "This is what [racing mind at night] actually does to your body." | PAS / Mechanism | Stakes-raising; agitates before solving. |
| **H11** | "I was *that* person who [doomscrolled till 1am]. Not anymore." | Transformation | Relatable confession → before/after. |
| **H12** | "The [coffee / gummy] my [skeptical husband / doctor friend] actually approved of." | Founder / Social-proof | Borrowed authority + skeptic-converted. |
| **H13** | "Why I switched from [my $6 latte habit] to this." | Transformation / Founder | Switch-story + implicit cost/value framing. |
| **H14** | "Replying to @ — yes, this is the [greens powder] that doesn't taste like [grass]." | Social-proof / Mechanism | Native green-screen-react format; kills the category objection (taste). |
| **H15** | "Read this before you buy *any* [adaptogen gummy]." | Mechanism / Founder | Authority/gatekeeper frame; high save + click. |

**Usage:** Per angle, pick 3 hooks for V01-baseline (α) and the two hook-test cells (β, γ). E.g. ZenChews Angle 1 (PAS): α = **H01**, β = **H08**, γ = **H10**. Rotate the *winning* hook into the next cycle as the new baseline.

## 4. The 2-Week Iteration Loop

### Timeline

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| **D0** | Cycle kickoff: confirm angle weighting (§1 cheat-sheet), assign hooks, lock the 24-grid. |
| **D1–3** | Produce: script (GPT/Claude) → avatar render (Arcads/Creatify) → edit/lipsync/caption (Captions AI) → QA + compliance pass. |
| **D4** | **Launch all 24** into Meta. Structure: see "Account Structure" below. |
| **D5–7** | **Learning phase — do NOT touch.** Let the algorithm exit learning; ignore hour-1 noise. |
| **D8** | **First read + first kills** (thumb-stop / hold gate). |
| **D10** | **Second read + scale decision** (CTR + cost gate). |
| **D11–13** | Scale winners (budget up / duplicate into new ad sets). Draft next cycle from survivors. |
| **D14** | Cycle close + retro. New cycle's D0. |

### Account Structure (so reads are clean)
- **One campaign per cycle**, CBO off where possible at test stage (ABO gives each creative a fair shot), or a dedicated **testing campaign** with per-ad-set budget floors.
- Group by **angle** at the ad-set level so angle-level signal is readable; let creative-level metrics drive kills *within* angle.
- **Minimum spend gate before any kill:** each creative must clear **~$20–30 spend OR ~2,000 impressions** before judgment — below that it's noise on an ingestibles budget.

### Metrics & Thresholds (the three diagnostic gauges)

Read **top-of-funnel diagnostics first** (they isolate *creative* quality), then conversion.

| Metric | What it tells you | Where to find it | **Kill below** | **Scale above** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Thumb-stop rate** (3-sec video plays ÷ impressions) | Did the **hook + first frame** stop the scroll? Tests levers A & B. | Meta: 3-sec plays / impressions | **< 25%** | **> 35%** |
| **Hold rate** (avg % video watched, or 15s-view ÷ 3-sec-view) | Did the **body** hold attention? Tests format + runtime (C, D). | Meta: ThruPlay / video retention | **< 20%** held to ~15s | **> 35%** |
| **Hook rate / CTR (link)** | Did the **promise + CTA** earn the click? Tests A & E. | Meta: link CTR | **< 0.8%** | **> 1.5%** |
| **CPC / CPA / ROAS** (confirmatory, slower) | Did the click convert at viable cost? | Meta + client checkout | CPA > target, ROAS < breakeven after gate spend | ROAS ≥ target with headroom |

> Thresholds are **starting defaults** — recalibrate to the account's own rolling baseline after 2–3 cycles. The *relative* ranking inside a cycle matters more than the absolute number.

### Decision Rules

**KILL** (pause at D8/D10) when, *after clearing the spend gate*, a creative is:
- Below thumb-stop floor (**< 25%**) → the **hook/first-frame failed**. Retire that hook for that avatar.
- Passed thumb-stop but **hold < 20%** → hook over-promised / body or format is wrong. Keep the hook, swap the format next cycle.
- Passed thumb-stop + hold but **CTR < 0.8%** → message-to-CTA mismatch. Keep creative, test a different CTA style.
- CPA above target with no diagnostic strength → hard kill.

**SCALE** (D10–13) when a creative clears all three diagnostic gates *and* CPA/ROAS is viable:
- Increase budget in **≤ ~20–30% daily steps** (avoid resetting learning), or **duplicate the winner into fresh ad sets / new audiences** rather than over-pumping one ad set.
- A "winner" = top ~10–20% of the 24 on the diagnostic stack with viable economics. Expect **~2–5 scalers out of 24** in a healthy cycle.

**ITERATE → next cycle:**
- Promote each **winning angle + hook + format combo** into the next cycle as the new **baseline (V01-equivalent)**, then vary *around* it (new hooks, new opening visuals).
- Losing angles still ship next cycle (never drop an angle) but with **fresh hooks/formats** — a losing *execution* ≠ a losing *angle*.
- Log every result against the naming convention (§5) so winning *patterns* compound across cycles. **The cross-cycle pattern library is the real asset.**

### Fatigue Guardrail
Even a winner decays. Frequency creeping **> ~2.5–3.0** on a previously-strong creative, or a **≥ ~20–30% rise in CPA week-over-week** on a flat creative = fatigue onset. Because a fresh 24-batch lands every 2 weeks, **rotate the fatiguing winner down as the new cycle's scalers ramp up** — the account never coasts to the 7-week fatigue wall.

## 5. Naming Convention

Every deliverable, ad name, and asset file uses one string so any row in Meta or the results sheet is self-describing and joinable.

```
BRAND_ANGLE_HOOK_FORMAT_LEN_vNN
```

| Token | Values | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `BRAND` | `MYCO` (MycoMornings) · `ZEN` (ZenChews) · `DG24` (DailyGreens24) | `MYCO` |
| `ANGLE` | `PAS` · `FND` (founder) · `MECH` (mechanism) · `TRAN` (transformation) | `PAS` |
| `HOOK` | Hook Bank ID `H01`–`H15` | `H01` |
| `FORMAT` | `TH` · `VO` · `LF` · `SS` · `GS` | `TH` |
| `LEN` | `15` · `30` | `30` |
| `vNN` | Iteration/variant number, zero-padded, increments across cycles | `v03` |

**Examples:**
```
MYCO_PAS_H01_TH_30_v01      → MycoMornings, Problem-Agitate-Solve, hook H01, talking-head, 30s, variant 01
ZEN_TRAN_H03_VO_15_v07      → ZenChews, Transformation, hook H03, voiceover+b-roll, 15s, variant 07
DG24_MECH_H06_SS_30_v02     → DailyGreens24, Mechanism, hook H06, split-screen demo, 30s, variant 02
```

**Rules:**
- The **exact same string** is the source filename, the Captions AI project name, AND the Meta ad name. No renaming downstream — that's how results join back to the brief.
- `vNN` is **global per BRAND+ANGLE+HOOK lineage**, not reset per cycle, so you can trace a hook's full history (`v01 → v02 → …`) across cycles.
- Cycle ID lives in the campaign name, not the creative name: `BRAND_CYCLEYYYY-WW` (e.g. `MYCO_2026-W22`). Creative name stays portable across cycles.
- Optional human label after the code for readability: `MYCO_PAS_H01_TH_30_v01__247crash`.

### Results Sheet Columns (the join target)
`creative_id (the name string) | cycle | brand | angle | hook | format | len | spend | impressions | thumb_stop% | hold% | CTR% | CPC | CPA | ROAS | decision (KILL/SCALE/ITERATE) | note`

> One row per creative per cycle. This sheet **is** the moat — it's where the judgment layer turns into a defensible, compounding pattern library across every brand and cycle.
