## 0. THE HARD TRUTH THIS DOC IS BUILT AROUND

**One person cannot run two full-intensity 90-day sprints at 100% each.** Both roadmaps already concede this in writing — doc 37 (Adwield) §0.5 caps V1 at ~6–7 hrs once V2 is live; doc 36 (Tideover) §0 gates its entire human motion behind a Venture-1 trigger and caps itself at ≤3 hrs/day. This doc makes the shared budget explicit and enforceable.

**The real daily ceiling is ~8 focused hours** (doc 36 §0's feasibility number), with **~9 on a strong day and ~6 on a low day.** "Focused" means deep-work output, not chair-time. Everything below is costed against **8**, not a fantasy 12.

**Three non-negotiable invariants that govern every rule in this doc:**
1. **The UGC Graduation Bar (V1 doc 30) is the master domino.** It is [HUMAN]-only, un-offloadable, and unlocks ALL V1 revenue. Until it clears, it gets the protected deep-work AM block and everything else bends around it.
2. **Tideover (V2) does not start its human motion until Adwield (V1) throws off first cash (the doc 36 §0 GATE).** Two un-cashed first-cash sprints never stack. Before the gate, V2 costs Chaga **≤20 min/day** (approvals only); agents do the rest.
3. **Proof-only, both engines.** Never fabricate metrics, case studies, testimonials, reviews, logos, or a track record. The ~$2M/yr home-gym operator story is **real and load-bearing for V2** (fulfillment-wait CX) and is shared there as lived experience — and is **never headlined as paid-ads proof in V1.** Trust is earned by demonstration.

**The balloon rev-share is the floor.** It provides a small base income (30% rev-share) that lets the validation reflex stay intact — no panic-spending before a paying customer. It is not a time sink in this cadence; treat it as background cash, not a venture.

## 1. SPLIT RATIONALE + HOW THE RATIO SHIFTS ACROSS 90 DAYS

The split is governed by one rule: **hours flow to the bottleneck that is closest to cash and least delegable.** Early, that is 100% V1's skill gate. As V1 starts producing cash and its work becomes partly agent-supported (outreach, enrichment, content), capacity frees up — and V2's human motion (which is itself low-frequency) gets switched on. The ratio is a **dial that turns toward V2 only as V1 earns the right to share the calendar.**

### The four phases (anchored to dated milestones, not vibes)

| Phase | Window (dated) | Trigger that opens it | V1 share | V2 share | V2 mode |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P0 — Skill-Gate Starve** | Day 0–14 (Sun Jun 14 → Sun Jun 28) | Today | **~85%** (≈4.5–6 hrs incl. protected AM drills) | **~15% / ≤20–90 min** | **Mode A — agent-only** (scout, pain-mine, draft, domain warmup); ≤20 min/day human approvals |
| **P1 — Ignite V1 Cash** | ~Day 14–35 (Sun Jun 28 → ~Sun Jul 19) | S0 graduation clears | **~80%** (≈5.5–6.5 hrs: renders + DMs + first calls + first delivery) | **~20% / ≤45 min** | **Mode A continues** — agents keep staging; warmup matures; NO human outreach yet |
| **P2 — Stagger V2 In** | ~Day 35–60 (~Sun Jul 19 → Thu Aug 13) | **V1 first cash banked ($3–5K)** OR Day-45 backstop (Wed Jul 29) with ≥3 free hrs/day | **~65%** (≈5 hrs) | **~35% / ≤2.5 hrs** | **Mode B opens** — V2 S0→S1: partner hunt, interviews, concierge onboarding of **1 live partner** |
| **P3 — Two Engines Live** | ~Day 60–90 (Thu Aug 13 → Sat Sep 12) | V1 at ~$6K MRR / retainers stacking | **~55–60%** (≈4.5–5 hrs) | **~40–45% / ≤3 hrs** | **Mode B steady** — V2 S1/S2 concierge + first leading-indicator win; S3a scaffold async via dev agent |

**How to read the shift.** The dial starts pinned at V1 (skill gate can't be shared with anything) and turns toward V2 in two discrete clicks: **first when the gate clears** (drilling stops, freeing the deep AM block — but that capacity goes into V1 *selling*, not V2 yet), and **then when V1 first cash lands** (which both proves V1's engine works AND is the literal doc-36 GATE that unlocks V2's human motion). By P3, V1 is increasingly **agent-supported** (Scout-Personalizer enriches, Dispatcher drafts, Pipeline-Keeper stamps) so its 55–60% buys more than it did at P1 — that efficiency gain is exactly what funds V2's rise.

**Why never 50/50, even at the end.** V1 stays the larger share all 90 days because (a) it is the **cash engine** carrying personal burn (~$3,000 CAD/mo), and (b) its remaining human work — **sales calls and client-delivery QC — cannot be offloaded** and scales with each retainer signed. V2's human work is genuinely lower-frequency (a few weekly blocks + twice-daily SLA windows), so a 40–45% slice is enough to move it. **If the gate or first cash slips, every V2 phase slips with it — V2 is the shock absorber, never V1.**

**The collision rule, stated once and obeyed everywhere:** on any day both engines demand hands, the block that gets cut is **V1 sample-render or V1 content (the FLEX buckets), or a V2 agent-only task — NEVER V1 client-delivery, NEVER a booked V1 call, NEVER a live V2 ticket inside its SLA window.**

## 6. DECISION RULES — SHIFTING WEIGHT BETWEEN THE VENTURES

**The ratio is event-driven, not calendar-driven.** Each rule below names the milestone that flips the dial.

**Rules that pour MORE into V1 (default bias — V1 is the cash engine):**
- **Still in P0 (gate not cleared):** V1 takes ~85%. No exceptions — the gate unlocks everything. V2 stays agent-only.
- **V1 first cash not yet banked by Day 35:** keep V2 in Mode A. **Do NOT open V2's human motion** — two un-cashed sprints never stack (doc 36 §0). V2 slides; V1 does not.
- **V1 reply rate <5% or 0 positive DMs after the top-10 land + 5 business days:** this is a V1 *quality* emergency — pour hours into the hook/sample rebuild loop, pull from V2 entirely until V1's funnel is unstuck.
- **A V1 pilot is mid-cycle with a Day-14 read approaching:** delivery floor is sacred; V2's week compresses to SLA windows + its single weekly block.

**Rules that pour MORE into V2 (only after V1 has earned it):**
- **THE RATIO-FLIP MILESTONE — V1 first cash banked ($3–5K collected):** this single event both proves V1's engine and **fires the doc-36 GATE.** Turn the dial from ~80/20 to ~65/35 and start V2 Mode B (S0 partner hunt). This is the one milestone that matters most.
- **V1 at ~$6K MRR / retainers stacking and stable:** V1 is now partly self-running on agents + recurring delivery rhythm. Turn the dial toward ~55–60 / 40–45 and let V2 reach S2 (prove the delta) and S3a scaffold.
- **V1 hits its capacity ceiling (~2 brands solo+V2) and you're tempted to sign brand #3:** do NOT. Freeze V1 selling, and **redirect the selling energy into V2** instead — V2's marginal hour is now worth more than an over-capacity V1 brand that recreates the agency-betrayal scar.
- **Day-45 backstop (Wed Jul 29):** if V1 is *delivering* and you have ≥3 sustainable free hrs/day, you MAY start V2 Mode B even if first cash is still landing. If V1 is NOT producing cash by Day 45, V2 stays Mode A and its gate slides.

**KILL / PAUSE triggers (per venture — pulling the plug protects the other engine):**
- **V1 PAUSE-SELLING:** production hrs exceed the two-venture ceiling → stop selling, start the Post/Editor VA hire (funded from first retainers). Never sign past ~2 brands while V2 also consumes hours, until the VA is trained.
- **V2 KILL/PIVOT:** 0–1 partners committed by GATE+14 → pause the build, run 10 more interviews; if still cold, **pivot the beachhead** (to long-wait DTC or made-to-order luxury) before sinking any build time.
- **V2 BUILD-FREEZE:** 0 documented leading-indicator wins by GATE+55 → **do not build/encode the app (S3b)**; the wedge is unproven, reassess.
- **V2 COST-KILL:** fully-loaded AI cost >$3.00/resolved ticket sustained → re-engineer prompts/model tier before scaling.
- **WHOLE-SYSTEM PAUSE (row 13):** combined live-obligation load >6 hrs/day for 3+ consecutive days → freeze ALL new commitments (no new V1 retainer, no new V2 live partner) until the 7-day average drops back under 8.
