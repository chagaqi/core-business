This intersection is the unfair zone. Lane A (Ecom x Agentic) is where competitors either know AI OR know ecom — rarely both. Chaga has both.

## The 2-engine framing

The Architect's classic move is **two engines running in parallel**:

1. **Fast-cash engine** — high-margin productized service that prints cash in 30-60 days, funds the system build
2. **Compounding engine** — recurring-revenue SaaS that scales without Chaga's time, becomes the asset

**Recommended portfolio:**

| Phase | Engine 1 (Fast Cash) | Engine 2 (Compounding) |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1-2 | First100 OR Local Makers | (none — focus all on Engine 1) |
| Month 2-4 | First100 / Local Makers running on partial autopilot | Build Presale CS Agent MVP |
| Month 4-6 | Engine 1 with operator hire | Presale launching on Shopify App Store |
| Month 6+ | Engine 1 cashflowing, operator running | Presale + ChurnDetective in parallel |

This way Chaga hits $20K/mo from Engine 1 inside 90 days while building the asset that becomes the long-term machine.

## The hard prioritization question

If we can only build ONE engine and we want to hit $20K/mo in 90 days:

- **Pure fast-cash play:** First100 (3 clients = there) or Local Makers (operationally heavier but uses Chaga's deep moat)
- **Pure asset play:** Presale CS Agent (60-90 days to first paying customer, compounds forever)
- **Hybrid:** ChurnDetective is fast-ish to revenue and recurring from day one

**Recommendation:** Do the 2-engine play. The shortlist deep-dive should answer "which two pair best."
