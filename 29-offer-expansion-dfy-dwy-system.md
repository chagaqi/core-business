# Offer Expansion — DFY / DWY / System / Subscription

### Own-Pipeline Production, a Proof-Gated Ladder, and Why Most of This Is Deliberately *Not Now*
**Founder: Chaga Chai · Status: EXPANSION DECISIONS (extends LOCKED Offer v2.0; does not replace it) · Date: 2026-05-30**

> **Read this first.** Two skeptical audits tore the first draft of this expansion apart, and they were right. The original was a 6,000-word, 9-rung roadmap with flywheel diagrams and Phase-3 pricing — written by a founder with **zero clients, zero case studies, and an unvalidated production method.** The act of designing rungs 5–9 in that much detail *was itself the focus leak* the document claimed to prevent. The gate language let the founder *feel* disciplined while building the distraction.
>
> So this version inverts the ratio. **Phase 1 (NOW) is concrete and operational. Phases 2 and 3 are compressed to a short, gated stub that records the decisions already made — including which lines are killed or demoted — but builds out nothing.** You will know the real shape of the second product after 3 clients teach you, not before. The single most important word remains **SEQUENCING**, and taking it seriously means most of what follows is intentionally one paragraph long.

---

## 0. Thesis (one paragraph)

Chaga is moving production off Arcads/Creatify onto a raw pipeline (Sora 2 + kie.ai + GPT/Claude) he controls. The honest case for that move is **control and speed-to-meta — not cost** (on credit cost alone, at 5-brand volume with ~30–50% first-pass yield, the raw rig is a wash-to-slight-loss vs Arcads' flat quota; the real price is paid in founder-hours). That speed-and-control edge *eventually* supports more ways to monetize the same pipeline — teaching in-house-curious brands (DWY), and a community. But **nothing past DFY ships until DFY has produced 3 documented founding-partner case studies AND the pipeline clears a measurable reliability bar.** Teaching "how to do it" before proving "it works" — and while looking cosmetically identical to every fake AI-ads guru — is the proof-only cardinal sin. **Phase 1 is the whole game right now. Everything else is a gated stub.**

## 1. PHASE 1 — NOW: DFY + Proof + the Pipeline Build (the only live work)

This is the locked offer, unchanged, plus the pipeline build. **It is the entire job for the next 90 days.** Every hour spent on Phase 2/3 detail is an hour not spent landing pilot #1.

### 1.1 The locked DFY ladder (front end — does not move)
$0 made-for-you sample → $0 founding-partner pilot → **$497** 10-video pack → **$1.5–2.5K/mo** mid → **$2.5–3.5K/mo** standard retainer (core revenue) → **$5K+/mo** premium (+ media buying, earned after creative wins). 5-brand cap. Month-to-month. Guarantee: beat control hold-rate while CPA flat, or next cycle free.

### 1.2 The 90-day mission (from the credibility playbook)
Render the made-for-them samples → publish 3–5 teardowns → run one self-funded real test → send the cold emails → land **3 founding-partner pilots** → capture the deltas (control→winner CTR + hold-rate, CPA flat). **That's it. That's the whole game.**

### 1.3 Build and battle-test the own pipeline ON real client work
The pipeline build is a Phase-1 deliverable because it is the prerequisite for *everything* downstream — and because it must earn its reliability on real work before it could ever be taught. Honest framing of the move:

**The economic case, corrected — lead with speed/control, NOT cost:**

| Win | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Speed-to-meta (the real #1)** | When a new style wins on Meta (e.g. claymation-style AI ads *right now*), GUI tools take **weeks** to support it; running raw, Chaga ships it in **days**. This is the genuine, defensible edge. |
| **Control / no throttling** | Direct model access: full prompt-scaffold control, no per-seat quota, no waiting on a vendor to expose a feature. Real. |
| **Cost per unit** | **DEMOTED — wash-to-slight-loss at scale, not a clean win.** Sora 2 first-pass acceptance for a label-accurate, sync-clean, hands-clean clip is ~**30–50%**, so true cost is render-cost ÷ yield ≈ **$3–20 per *usable* clip**. At ~240 usable clips/mo (5 brands × 24 × 2 cycles) that is roughly *neutral* vs Arcads' flat upper-tier quota (~$300–500/mo). Stop selling "cheaper per unit" as the headline; it may be false at scale and it's the easiest claim to be wrong about. |

**The founder-hours line item the first draft hid — this is the binding constraint:**
At 30–50% yield, producing ~240 usable clips means generating **~480–800 raw clips/mo**, each needing a QC look (mouth-sync, hands, label warp, claim language). At ~2 min/clip that is **16–27 hrs/mo of pure QC**, before scripting, angle selection, character minting, kie.ai batch babysitting, launching, tagging, and maintenance. Realistic total at the 5-brand cap: **~30–50 founder-hours/month.** That is a near-full-time load for one person, and it is the reason DFY revenue is hard-capped at ~$15–17.5K/mo. **This number is the gate on every later phase** (see §3).

**The maintenance tax is real and recurring.** `28` is single-source, medium-confidence, with open validation items (label fidelity across reuse, drift threshold, real kie.ai throughput, disclosure handling). These are not optional — they are the cost of owning the rig, and they recur every time the meta shifts and a *new* method must be re-validated for character-consistency and compliance before it ships.

### 1.4 Proof-only credibility (unchanged)
Portfolio reel, 3–5 teardowns, one self-funded real number, build-in-public, the FTC-safe filter on every claim. **Zero fabrication.**

### 1.5 What you do NOT do in Phase 1
**Do not mention DWY, the system, the course, or the subscription anywhere public.** No DWY landing page. No "half the price" content. No course waitlist. You may *privately* document the pipeline as a free byproduct of doing the work — but you sell nothing and announce nothing. The public footprint stays 100% DFY + proof.

## 2. PHASES 2 & 3 — GATED STUB (decisions locked, build-out forbidden until Gate 1)

This is deliberately short. The detailed roadmap was the disease. What follows is **the set of decisions already made** so they're not re-litigated later — not a built product. **Revisit only after Gate 1 clears, when 3 real clients have told you the actual demand, the actual method reliability, and the actual second product.**

**Phase 2 (post-Gate-1): DWY, kept dark.** Teach the proven, documented pipeline to the *in-house-curious* segment — brands already paying Arcads/Creatify or a markup agency. Likely a white-glove install + bounded coaching window. **Locked constraints:**
- **DWY stays dark — referral / word-of-mouth / 1:1 only — until DFY is at the cap with a waitlist.** No public DWY marketing while you're still landing DFY clients: a $20–100K/mo brand founder is one thread away from realizing "I could run this myself," and public DWY content *teaches your own DFY prospects to disqualify themselves.* The two-track discipline governs messaging *inside* your funnel; it does not stop a public footprint from poisoning the DFY top-of-funnel.
- **DWY is NOT near-pure-margin.** It carries a real, uncosted support tail — every student hits label warp, drift, kie.ai failures, FTC-claim questions, and emails you. Bound it *in the contract before launch*: fixed coaching window, capped office hours, "we support the documented SOP, not custom edge cases." Otherwise the install becomes an unpaid lifetime helpdesk.

**Phase 2 (post-Gate-1): the productized course — flagged as suspect, possibly never public.** A public "how to run AI UGC ads" course is *cosmetically indistinguishable from the flooded-inbox guru* even with 3 case studies, because 3 case studies is exactly what every fake guru claims — the market can't tell your real 3 from their fake 30. Teaching is a **different trust category** than DFY, and DFY deltas don't transfer into "credible educator." **Decision: default to NO public course.** If anything, package the system only as the DWY deliverable for people who already bought DFY or saw the pilot ledger. Re-decide after Gate 1.

**Phase 3 (post-Gate-2): recurring — the standalone subscription is KILLED as drawn.** The "$50–150/mo prompt-updates / Always-Current" SKU does not survive audit and is **cut.** "Being current" still ships as a copyable artifact that leaks the same day; churn for a copyable monthly drop is realistically 60–80% in 3 months (one-time sales wearing a subscription label); and the "we're always first" claim rests on a knowledge engine whose only track record is rejecting one fabricated example — that's a compliance filter working once, not evidence it spots winning metas early. **Decision: fold "current-meta feed" into the existing $97/mo Skool community.** Retention comes from a living peer group + a credible curator, not a prompt file. The community is the recurring product; the meta-feed is a benefit inside it.

**Phase 3 (post-Gate-2): white-glove upsell.** Self-servers/community members who decide they'd rather have it done route back into DFY (Rungs 2–4). Real *only* as a trickle off whatever funnel exists — which is why the community, not a churny subscription, must be that funnel.

## 3. The Gates (locked, measurable — activity is not enough)

**→ GATE 1 — must ALL be true before ANY Phase-2 work (and before the word "course," "DWY," or "subscription" appears publicly):**
1. **3 documented founding-partner case studies** in hand (control→winner deltas, CPA flat). The proof threshold.
2. **Pipeline reliability bar — MEASURABLE, not "written up nicely":** reproducible first-pass *usable-clip yield ≥ a stated % (set it from real data, e.g. ≥40%) across ≥3 brands*, with the `28` validation items closed (label fidelity across reuse, drift threshold, throughput, disclosure). You cannot teach a method your own notes admit is unvalidated.
3. **Founder-capacity check (the binding constraint):** DFY is *below* the 5-brand cap, OR the QC/production load is partially delegated or automated. **One human cannot run 5 DFY brands + deliver DWY + produce a course simultaneously** — and under Phase-1 incentives, the thing that gives is the case studies, i.e. the proof the whole sequence depends on. If the calendar isn't free, Phase 2 does not open, regardless of proof.

**→ GATE 2 — must be true before any recurring/community-feed or upsell push:**
1. **DWY has paying customers and ≥1–2 "they ran it and it worked" outcomes** — proof the *taught* system works in someone else's hands.
2. **Knowledge-engine ACCURACY track record, not activity:** a tracked hit-rate — "spotted meta X on date D; it was winning on Meta by D+N" — across Phase-1/2 work. "Ships a cadence of updates" is not enough; the recurring promise is *being early and right*, and that must be demonstrated before anyone pays for it.

**Why this order is non-negotiable:** each phase's product is backed by the proof the previous phase generated. Skip a gate and you're selling an unproven promise — the proof-only cardinal sin. The gates protect both the *proof* and the *founder's calendar*; the calendar breaks first.

## 4. Segment Messaging — the "half-the-price" line is quarantined to 1:1, never public

"You could run this yourself for half the price" is a strong DWY hook and **pure poison** to a DFY prospect (who pays specifically to *not* do it). The first draft tried to quarantine it to a "DWY landing page." **That's insufficient: at <1,000 followers it's all one founder's public footprint — a DFY prospect googles you and finds both.**

**Locked rule:**
- The half-price hook is a **1:1 sales-conversation line ONLY**, spoken to someone who has *already self-identified as in-house-curious on a call.* **Never written, never public, never indexed.**
- Route before you price: open every call with *"Do you want this run for you, or do you want to run it in-house?"* — one track, then pricing language.
- **Labor-honesty caveat (proof-only extends to DWY buyers too):** the claim is "half the price *in cash* — you take on the production hours" (the same ~30–50 hrs/mo). Without that caveat the DWY student does the math at month 2, finds the labor tax, and churns angry. That's a proof-only violation against the *DWY* buyer.

**The three segments (for the eventual two-track discipline, not for building now):** DFY buyer (pays to offload — never hears "half price"); DWY / in-house-curious (1:1 half-price hook + labor caveat); community member (living peer group + meta-feed + curation — the only recurring product).

## 5. Consistency with Proof-Only + the Niche

- **Phase 1 is *only* DFY + proof.** The expansion sits entirely behind a gate that *is* the proof threshold. The launch motion is untouched.
- **DFY stays locked to functional consumables, forever.** "The mushroom-coffee guy" beats "an AI ads guy" for the DFY ICP. Only the *system mechanics* (how to mint a consistent character, batch via kie.ai) are vertical-agnostic — and even those launch niche-first, broaden only if they demonstrably generalize, and only to a *different buyer on a different track.* DFY never broadens.
- **Teaching is a different trust category** and is treated as a proof-only *risk*, not a free win: default to no public course; DWY dark and referral-only; recurring = community, not a copyable artifact; the "always first" claim needs an accuracy track record before it's spoken.
- **Zero fabrication, carried forward.** The FTC-safe filter gates speed — a newly-spotted method is never shipped fast if it's non-compliant or unvalidated for the new style.
