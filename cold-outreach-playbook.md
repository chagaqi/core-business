# Cold Outreach, the way you'd actually want to be reached

*A relationship-first system for B2B prosumer outreach. For founders selling into teams, agencies, partnerships, and the people you want in your corner for the next ten years.*

**Filed under:** Cold DM · Networking · Partnerships · Customer discovery

## The whole game

Move someone from **"Who are you?"** → **"Oh my gosh, take my money."**

That's it. Every step below is just shrinking the distance between those two reactions.

> **Date your product. Marry your niche.**
> You're going to build this network anyway, so build it like someone who plans to stay.

---

## The mindset (most people get this backwards)

The slow way *is* the fast way once you zoom out. The wrong instinct is to treat outreach as a transaction. The right move is to treat it as networking.

| The wrong frame — transactional, turn & burn | The right frame — relational, network away |
|---|---|
| "What can I get out of this person?" | "How do I become worth knowing?" |
| Blast the same pitch to everyone | Pick people you genuinely find interesting |
| Ask for the meeting in message one | Give value long before you ask |
| Measure success in replies this week | Measure success in relationships built |
| Disappear the second they say no | Stay useful whether or not they buy |

## The Method

### 1. Drill your ICP until it's undeniable
*Know exactly who you're for, and why they'd care.*

Before you message anyone, get crystal clear on who your person is and what makes them stop scrolling. The whole job is shrinking the distance between *"who are you?"* and *"oh my gosh, take my money."*

If your offer and your ideal customer are fuzzy in your own head, every message you send will be fuzzy too. Sharpen the ICP first. Everything downstream gets easier.

### 2. Dial your profile so you're instantly credible
*When they look back at you, "who are you?" answers itself.*

The moment you engage, they're going to look at **your** profile. That glance decides everything. Your headline, your bio, your recent posts all have to say "this person is worth my time" in under three seconds.

Tune the whole profile to your ICP. They should see themselves in it, see proof you're credible, and see the value you bring before you've said a word to them directly.

### 3. Flirt on the internet
*Connect, engage, give give give, then ask.*

Send the connection request with **no message attached.** A naked request gets accepted far more than one carrying a pitch. Once they connect, start showing up. Like their content. Leave real comments. Be visible in a way that earns goodwill.

This is the **jab, jab, jab, right hook.** Give roughly eight pieces of value before you ever ask for anything. Then, when you reach out, it's warm. They've already seen your name a dozen times.

The sequence:

1. Connect, no message
2. Like their posts
3. Comment with substance
4. Be consistently visible
5. Then send the warm DM

### The value ladder

**Eight units of value before one ask.** Each rung is goodwill in the bank. By the time you throw the punch, it doesn't feel like a punch at all.

`like → reply → reshare → tip → intro → resource → insight → cheer → THE ASK`

jab · jab · jab · jab · jab · jab · jab · jab → **right hook**

### 4. The payoff compounds
*Is it the fastest? No. Is it the most durable? Absolutely.*

This isn't the fastest path. It's the one that's still paying out years later. Do this consistently and you build a network you can pick up the phone and call long after the first conversation.

Real proof: this exact playbook built a medtech agency to **seven figures** on cold DMs. Years later, out of the industry entirely, those same relationships could still close a million-dollar program with one well-timed message to the right person. That's the compounding effect of a network built on goodwill instead of extraction.

> "My whole career has been made on cold DMs. The trick was never the message. It was deciding to build the relationship before I needed anything from it."

## The Full Playbook

### Profile optimization checklist
*Be worth knowing in 3 seconds.*

- [ ] **Headline speaks to your ICP, not your title.** Lead with the outcome you create for them, not "Founder & CEO."
- [ ] **Profile photo is warm and human.** Real face, clear eyes, looks like someone you'd want to talk to.
- [ ] **Banner reinforces what you do.** Free real estate. Use it to state the value or show the proof.
- [ ] **Bio mirrors the niche.** They should see themselves and their problem in your first two lines.
- [ ] **Recent posts show credibility.** A glance at your feed should prove you know this space cold.
- [ ] **Featured link points somewhere useful.** A piece of value, not just a sales page.

### The scripts

**Step 1 — The connection request**
Send it with **no note.** A blank request gets accepted more often than one carrying a pitch. Save your words for after they've connected.

**Step 5 — The warm DM (after 8 units of value)**
> "Hey **[First name]**, I've really been enjoying your content. You can probably tell, I've been liking a lot of it. The stuff on **[specific topic]** especially hit. I'm building **[one line on what you're building]** and I think there's real overlap. Would love to jam on it sometime if you're open. No agenda, just think we'd have a good conversation."

**Why it works**
It names a real thing you noticed. It's honest about the goodwill you've been building ("you can probably tell"). It leads with curiosity, not a calendar link. And it gives them an easy, low-pressure yes.
