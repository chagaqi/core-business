# Sales Playbook — LOCKED OFFER

### AI-Native UGC Ad Creative Service for DTC Functional Consumables
**Founder: Chaga Chai · Derived from 12-offer-locked.md + 13-positioning-acp.md · 2026-05-29**

> **How to use this doc.** Operator-to-operator, never pushy. The call's first job is to find their monthly ad spend, because spend decides the branch (retainer vs pack-first). The second job is to find out which Track they're in (Refuge / Leverage) so you pitch the right hero. Volume is always the method, never the headline. Never lead with "AI." Never quote round numbers. On credibility: claim NO ad case studies and NO paid-ads history — there are none, and faking them is exactly what the inbox is flooded with. Trust is earned by demonstration: the free made-for-them sample, the rendered portfolio + teardowns, a real number off your own small test, the founding-partner pilots, and the guarantee. Confident, never apologetic — newness is *why the free sample and risk reversal exist*. At launch there are zero case studies; use `[CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER: ...]` and the founding-partner framing, never fabricated numbers.

## PART 1 — Discovery Call Script (15 minutes)

**Goal of the call:** qualify on spend, find the wound or the bottleneck, route to the right Track, and either (a) quote a retainer live or (b) put the $497 pack in their hand. You are diagnosing, not pitching. If they aren't a fit, say so — that's the whole brand.

**Pre-call rule (non-negotiable):** for a priority prospect, the sample of *their* product is already made and already in their inbox before this call exists. The call is the conversation *after* they've seen their own product as a UGC ad. You are never cold on this call.

### 0:00–1:30 — Opening (set the frame, lower the guard)

> "Thanks for the time. I'll keep this to 15 minutes and I'll be straight with you — I'm not going to pitch you for 15 minutes. I take 5 functional-consumables brands total, so half my job on this call is figuring out whether you're even one I should take. If you're not a fit I'll tell you and probably point you somewhere better.
>
> You already saw the sample I made of [THEIR PRODUCT]. Before I tell you anything about how I work — what's actually going on in the account right now? What made you take this call?"

*Why:* The cap is the opener because it inverts the agency dynamic — you're screening *them*. Letting them talk first surfaces the wound/bottleneck in their words, which tells you the Track before you've committed to a pitch.

### 1:30–4:00 — Qualifying question #1: SPEND (the first job, the branch)

Ask it plainly, early, before anything else:

> "What are you spending a month across Meta and TikTok right now — rough is fine?"

**The branch (decide silently, don't announce it):**

| Their answer | Tier | This call becomes | Hero offer you'll land on |
|---|---|---|---|
| **$20–100K/mo** | Tier B (primary) | **Retainer pitch.** Pack is optional foot-in-door if they're cautious. | Mid/standard retainer, quoted live. |
| **$5–20K/mo** | Tier A (downsell) | **Pack-first.** Do not pitch a retainer they can't absorb. | $497 10-video pack → retainer later once it's working. |
| **$100K+/mo, 90+/wk testing** | Out of fit (standalone) | **Honest disqualify / one-angle-family framing.** | Refer out, or one angle-family of leverage only — say so. |
| **Under $5K/mo or pre-launch** | Out of fit | Kindly out. Point them to fix offer/LP/PMF first. | None — be honest, it protects the brand. |

If $20–100K: *"Good — that's exactly the tier I'm built for."* Move to pain-dig, then retainer pitch.
If $5–20K: *"Got it — you're a little earlier than my retainer tier, so I'd start you somewhere lower-risk. I'll come back to that."* Move to pain-dig, then pack.
If $100K+/90+wk: *"Honest answer — at 90 tests a week I'm not your creative department, I'm one angle-family of leverage at most. I'll say that to your face rather than oversell. Want me to be that one lever, or should I point you to someone built for your scale?"*

### 4:00–6:00 — Qualifying question #2: VERTICAL + DECISION + CLAIMS

> "Quick three: Is it functional consumables — coffee, gummies, beverage, greens, nootropics, chocolate? Are you the one who says yes on creative spend? And do you have claims you can actually substantiate, or are you flying close to the FTC line?"

*Why:* Vertical is locked and non-negotiable — beauty/fashion is an explicit no. Founder-as-decision-maker is required. The claims question both qualifies and previews the compliance gate (a real part of the product in this vertical, not a footnote).

### 6:00–9:30 — Pain-dig (find the wound OR the bottleneck → this names the Track)

Ask these and shut up. Their answers route the pitch.

1. *"How many creative tests are you actually getting into the account in a month right now?"* (Listen for "~4 and hoping" → capacity-starved.)
2. *"When a winner dies on you — does it die slow around the 6–7 week mark, or does it just fall off a cliff?"* (The 7-week fatigue clock is the bridge both camps nod at.)
3. *"Who's making creative today — you at 1am, a freelancer, an agency, your two ops people?"*
4. *"Did mid-February last year do anything to the account?"* (The Andromeda tell. If they light up here → Track 1 / Archetype A.)
5. *"If you could wave a wand — is the problem that you don't know what to test, or that you can't physically produce and launch enough to find out?"* (The fork: strategy vs throughput. Almost always throughput.)

**Route off their answers:**

- **They grieve mid-Feb, "winners died for no reason," tried every targeting fix, got burned by an agency** → **Track 1 "Refuge" / Archetype A.** Enter on the wound. Pivot fast.
- **They're winning, want more leverage, "we know volume × variation works, we just can't produce it," two ops people are the bottleneck** → **Track 2 "Leverage" / Archetype B.** Zero grief.
- **Solo or 2–3 person team, already juggling agencies/vendors, wants to hand off and not babysit** → **Track 2 "Leverage" / Archetype C — the dream client.** Lead with offloaded ownership.

### 9:30–12:30 — The Pitch (framed by Track — pick ONE, do not blur)

**If Track 1 — Refuge (Archetype A):** *Enter on recognition, pivot fast to the lever. Do not marinate.*

> "Here's what I think happened, and tell me if I'm wrong. The game changed under you around mid-February — same creative, same audience, CPMs doubled, no explanation. You've already done the targeting fixes, the budget restructures, the consolidation. None of it worked because none of it was the problem. The auction now rewards whoever can keep putting fresh, specific creative in front of the customer — and you physically can't produce at that rate alone. You're not short on instinct. You're short on hands.
>
> So here's the lever you couldn't pull solo. Every two weeks you greenlight 4 angles and the claims you can back. I run those into 24 production-grade variations, launch and tag them in your account in your naming convention, read them at day 14, kill the losers, double the winners, and reload the next 4. Volume isn't the point — it's just how the winner gets found before the 7-week wall kills it. You stop praying one of 4 tests works and start finding winners on a schedule. And you're not the one writing scripts at 1am to make it happen."

**If Track 2 — Leverage (Archetypes B / C):** *No grief. Capacity math + workflow integration.*

> "You don't have a strategy problem — your throughput per dollar is the problem. Every new angle-family means more files to brief, name, upload, structure into ad sets, and reconcile against your data, and that workflow tax is why creative ships slower than your team can actually test. You don't need another strategist with opinions about your account.
>
> What I do is take one angle-family fully off your plate, end-to-end: concept → 24 production-grade variations → iteration → launched and tagged in *your* taxonomy and ad-set structure, read against *your* dashboard, not a screenshot I send you. Every two weeks. Your two ops people get that throughput back without you hiring a third. You approve angles and claims; the family runs itself in your account. I cap at 5 brands, so the SLA never degrades when I sign the next one."

### 12:30–14:00 — The Close (guarantee + roster cap, then a single ask)

Present the guarantee verbatim — risk reversal *is* the perceived-likelihood lever:

> "Here's the deal. Within 30 days my creative beats your current best ad on hook-rate and hold-rate — the 3-second and thumbstop — *and* your CPA holds flat or better, measured against your own control, in your own account, with the head-to-head data on the table. If it doesn't, your next 24-variation cycle is free. It's month-to-month, cancel anytime, and you keep every video either way, win or lose."

Then the cap (honest scarcity — only use real numbers):

> "Last thing — the cap is real. I take 5 functional-consumables brands, period. It's published. It's the reason you can't ever become the deprioritized account on a list. I've got [FILL: N] founding slots left, and founding partners get terms I won't repeat later — more on that if we get there."

**The single ask — branch it:**

- **Tier B / retainer:** *"Want me to walk you through what a retainer would actually look like for your spend and SKU mix? Takes about three minutes."* → go to **Part 3, retainer quote.**
- **Tier A / pack:** *"I'd start you on the 10-video pack — it's $497, one-time, lowest-risk way to see the work in your account. Want me to send it over?"* → go to **Part 3, pack anchor.**

**Non-pushy out (always offer it):** *"And if this isn't the right time, no pressure — I'd rather you come back when you're ready than sign someone who's half-in."*

## PART 3 — Pricing-Conversation Script (quoting live)

> Locked rules: lead with the $497 pack publicly; quote retainers live, never public; never put "$200–500/video" and "$497 for 10" on the same surface without the reconciliation framing; never discount the retainer to close early; the $5K+ premium (with media buying) is earned after creative wins, never sold cold.

### Step 1 — Anchor off the $497 pack (always start here)

> "Public price, the one anyone can buy, is the 10-video pack — $497, one-time. Here's the honest part: that's roughly fifty bucks a video, and it's deliberately underpriced. One comparable UGC video, all-in, runs $200–500 in real value. The pack isn't where the economics live — it's the cheapest, lowest-risk way for you to see the work land in your own account. Its only job is to start a real relationship without you betting much."

*This frames everything below it as the real economics, not a markup.*

### Step 2 — Quote the retainer live (Tier B only, $20–100K/mo)

Tie it to their spend and SKU mix out loud so it's clearly bespoke, not a rate card:

> "For where you're at — [their spend]/mo, [N] SKUs — the retainer is where this actually works. Two tiers fit most brands your size:
>
> - **Mid:** 20 videos a month plus a bi-weekly strategy call — runs **$1.5–2.5K/mo**, depending on SKU mix and angle complexity.
> - **Standard:** same cadence plus full reporting and the live iteration loop — killing losers, doubling winners read in your dashboard — **$2.5–3.5K/mo**.
>
> Both are month-to-month, cancel anytime, you keep every video, and both carry the guarantee. Most brands at your spend start on standard because the iteration loop is where the winner actually gets found — but if you want to dip in, mid does the job."

Anchor against their own P&L, not against competitors:

> "For context — a brand at a $90 AOV doing 3× ROAS at your spend pays a $2.5K retainer out of a single profitable day. I'm not asking you to find new budget; I'm asking you to point a sliver of what you already spend at finding the next winner faster."

If they hesitate at the retainer → step down, don't discount:

> "No problem — start on the $497 pack, see it in your account, and we revisit the retainer when the work's earned it. I'd rather you upgrade because it worked than sign now because I pushed."

**Premium ($5K+, media buying):** do not raise unsolicited cold. Only if they ask about you running buying too: *"That exists — it's $5K-plus and it includes media buying — but I gate it behind demonstrated creative wins. Let's prove the creative beats your control first; then it's an easy conversation."*

### Step 3 — The founding-partner deal (first 3–5 brands only)

> "Because I'm early, I'm taking a small number of founding partners — and I'm honest about the trade. You're one of the first deltas I put on the board, so in exchange for the right to publish your before/after numbers and a one-line testimonial, you get founding terms: [FILL: founding terms — e.g., locked rate / extra cycle / the $0 instrumented pilot before any paid commitment]. The instrumented pilot is 5 videos, $0, built to capture a real before/after on hold-rate and CPA so we both see whether this works before money changes hands.
>
> I've got [FILL: N] founding slots left out of 5. After they're gone, the terms reset to standard. I'm telling you that as a fact, not as a squeeze."

*Founding-partner framing does the double duty of manufacturing the missing case studies and turning free/discounted work into a contractual proof output. Only ever use real, current slot counts.*

## Appendix — Pre-Call Checklist & Hard Prerequisites

- [ ] **Sample already sent.** No priority-prospect call happens without their own product rendered and in their inbox first.
- [ ] **Proof-only credibility.** Never claim ad case studies or a paid-ads history (there are none). Trust carries on the made-for-them sample, the teardown portfolio, a real self-test number, the founding-partner deltas, and the guarantee. The home-gym business appears at most once, lightly, as "a real business of my own" — never as ad-proof, never headlined.
- [ ] **Real slot count.** Only ever state the true current number of founding/roster slots. Honest scarcity only.
- [ ] **No fabricated proof.** Every case-study reference stays a `[CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER]` until a real pilot delta exists.
- [ ] **Track decided before the pitch.** A skeptic who's winning (B/C) must never be handed Archetype A's grief.
- [ ] **Spend found first.** The branch (retainer vs pack-first) is decided off spend, asked in the first 4 minutes.
