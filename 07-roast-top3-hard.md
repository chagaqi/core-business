# Hard Roast — Official Top 3

**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Temperature:** No Mercy
**Format:** Full council session per idea — 4-6 sentences per member, named competitors, real numbers, founder-profile-specific, Path to BUILD / Pivot / Pass per idea, then synthesis comparison.

The three:
1. Customer Interaction SaaS (lean modern alternative to Podium / Birdeye)
2. AI-Native Performance Ads Agency

# IDEA 1 — Customer Interaction SaaS

## The Idea
A modern, API-native SaaS competing with Podium and Birdeye on price + agility. Three core features: instant review requests after every job, unified inbox (SMS, Google, Facebook, Instagram), webchat-to-SMS persistence. Wedge: lean 20-30 person team running modern APIs vs. legacy 800-person sales-and-engineering bloat. Target: HVAC, plumbing, dental, auto shops at $49-$199/mo, $149 average.

## The Customer (Eddie, owns a 12-truck HVAC company in Tampa)
"I pay Podium $299 a month and I hate that I pay it but I need it. My techs leave a job, the customer doesn't get a review request for 4 hours, and by then they're back at work and forget. If your thing fires the SMS the second the job is closed in ServiceTitan AND I can answer the customer back from one screen instead of bouncing between Google business and Facebook DMs, I'll switch — but you need a 30-day money-back guarantee because I've been burned by 'cheaper Podium' three times. And don't try to upsell me on website builders. I have a website. I want one thing done right."

**One-liner:** *"Half the price, same job, no contract, 30-day guarantee. Otherwise you're just another startup that'll be dead in 18 months."*

## The Competitor (Podium's SMB head)
"You're not the first 'lean Podium killer' — we've seen six of you in the last three years. Most died because the SMB CAC is brutal. Trades buyers don't read tech blogs and they don't browse Twitter — they're in BNI groups, Facebook contractor groups, and trade associations we sponsor. Our distribution moat is bottoms-up: we have HVAC association partnerships, contractor influencer rev shares, and a 200-person SDR team cold-calling every license database in the country. Domain knowledge doesn't beat distribution. You'll get to maybe 100 customers via your friend network and then plateau hard at the cold-acquisition wall."

**One-liner:** *"You're going to learn what 'CAC is the killer' actually means around customer 80."*

## The Investor
"The math here is sharp. $149 average × 135 customers = $20K MRR in 90 days if you can stay alive on Chaga's $5K. The category is real — $32.5B CX management market by 2029, and Podium is bloated. Your unit economics work because modern API aggregators (Merge, Twilio, Lob) and AI mean you can run lean with 1-3 people for the first 24 months. But realistic CAC for HVAC owners is $400-800 fully loaded (Facebook ads + sales time), payback is 4-6 months at $149 ARPU. Bootstrap-viable but cash-flow tight. Realistic ceiling: $3-8M ARR before Podium or GoHighLevel notices you and either crushes or acquires you."

**One-liner:** *"The math works. The distribution kills you. Solve distribution before you ship a feature."*

## The Mentor
"Reading your profile again: 'You think in capital efficiency, revenue-per-employee, margins — these are Operator obsessions.' This idea IS that game. Podium has 800 people doing what you could do with 5 + AI. That's a 100x revenue-per-employee gap. The trades-network unlock changes everything I said last session — you have actual ground truth from friends and projects. BUT this is a sales business disguised as a SaaS. You'll be on calls with HVAC owners every day for 6 months. Are you sure you want that life on a Tuesday in 9 months? And — be honest — does this thrill you, or are you choosing it because it scored highest? The Architect builds machines they want to live inside."

**One-liner:** *"You can win this. The question is whether you'll want to be in the room with this customer for 5 years."*

## Path to BUILD

This becomes a clear BUILD if:
1. **You can name 10 trades-owner friends or warm intros TODAY** who'd give you 30 minutes for a discovery call (mentor's distribution test)
2. **You confirm via 5 of those calls that they currently pay $200+/mo for review tools** and would pay $99 for a working alternative (customer's price-test)
3. **You pick ONE vertical to start** (HVAC, plumbing, dental, auto, or roofing — not all) so you can build vertical-specific templates and association partnerships (competitor's distribution-fight)
4. **You set a hard rule: zero feature work until 50 paying customers** — you compete on price, simplicity, and speed-to-onboard, not features (investor's lean-team thesis)

## Pivot Opportunities

- **Vertical hyper-focus:** Drop the multi-trade pitch. Become "Reviews + Inbox for HVAC contractors under 25 trucks." HVAC associations, HVAC podcast sponsorships, HVAC Facebook groups. Distribution gets simpler, brand sharper, expansion comes later.
- **Distribution partnership:** Skip cold acquisition. Partner with Jobber, Housecall Pro, or ServiceTitan as a bundled add-on. They take 30%, you ride their existing 100K+ contractor distribution. Lower margin, much faster scale.
- **Ecom-adjacent pivot:** If the trades sales calls drain you, pivot to DTC review automation for Shopify brands — your *actual* domain. Birdeye-for-Shopify is open. Trades + DTC are different worlds; pick the one you'll wake up excited about.

## Pass Conditions

Walk away if:
- After 10 discovery calls, fewer than 4 trades owners say "yes I'd pay $99 for this" with specifics about pain
- You can't find a distribution wedge that doesn't require a Podium-scale SDR team
- You realize 6 weeks in that talking to HVAC owners all day is making you miserable (Tuesday test failed)

## The One Question
**Can you book 10 discovery calls with trades owners (friends or warm intros) in the next 7 days?** If yes, this is your fastest credible path to $20K MRR. If no, the distribution wall is closer than the council made it sound.

# IDEA 2 — AI-Native Performance Ads Agency

## The Idea
An agency for DTC and ecom brands ($20K-$500K/mo Meta and TikTok spend) producing AI-generated UGC-style video ad creatives at 10-20x the velocity of traditional UGC shops. Foot-in-door pitch: "Give us 10% of your spend. We match or beat your existing CPA. If we don't, you owe us nothing." Long-term play: partner with a creative strategist (rev share) to layer brand storytelling over the AI engine, doubling perceived value per engagement.

## The Customer (Megan, runs growth at a $4M ARR skincare DTC brand)
"My CPA on Meta is climbing $3 every quarter and I'm out of creative angles. Traditional UGC takes 3 weeks and one creator at a time. If you can ship 20 hooks across 20 'creators' in 72 hours and the CPA holds, I'll give you a $5K creative-only retainer immediately. But — and this is the dealbreaker — I sell skincare. People care about authentic faces. If you send me Synthesia avatars that look like soulless AI mannequins, my brand voice dies and my CAC triples. Show me your last 5 winners with real CPA data before I let you touch a dollar of my budget."

**One-liner:** *"Volume is the pitch, authenticity is the dealbreaker. Show me CPA receipts or you're noise."*

## The Competitor (a real existing AI UGC agency in 2026)
"There are now ~40 agencies pitching AI UGC. Arcads released self-serve at $99/mo, so brands' first question is 'why do I pay you a retainer when I can run Arcads myself?' The agencies that survived 2025 did one of three things: (1) built a proprietary model trained on their winners, (2) hired actual creative strategists who could outpunch the generic prompts, or (3) niched to one vertical and built a swipe library nobody else has. If you're 'foot-in-the-door + generic Arcads + Chaga's sales charm,' you'll get to $15K MRR and stall — which is the agency graveyard."

**One-liner:** *"You're entering an arbitrage that was already arbitraged. Your differentiation IS the strategist. Find them or fold."*

## The Investor
"This is a service business with a tool that isn't yours. Tool moat = Arcads / HeyGen / Captions AI — and they sell to your customers directly. Your defensibility lives in (a) the strategist partner, (b) vertical-specific swipe library you accumulate, and (c) the speed-and-iteration playbook you build. Unit economics: 3 retainer clients at $5K-$10K/mo = $15-30K MRR in 90 days, very plausible. But ceiling is real — agencies in this category hit $1-2M ARR and either scale to a 30-person agency (operator hell) or sell creative + acquire as a productized SaaS. Foot-in-door pitch is genuinely good — your sales skill is the unfair advantage."

**One-liner:** *"Cash flow this thing to fund the real asset. Don't mistake it FOR the asset."*

## The Mentor
"Two things from your profile: (1) you said you 'tend to avoid equity partnerships based on past pain.' This idea fundamentally requires one — the strategist partner. You need to confront that pattern or this idea breaks. (2) Your archetype says 'when cash is tight you'll go high-touch and manual, but your instinct is always toward productization. Honor that instinct earlier.' That means: even as you sell this as an agency, design from day one for the moment you turn it into a productized creative-ops SaaS. Don't fall in love with the retainer. The retainer funds the product. And — gut check — does this excite you the way Presale did, or does it just feel safe because you have the playbook?"

**One-liner:** *"The agency funds the asset. Build the asset from day one or the agency owns you."*

## Path to BUILD

This becomes a clear BUILD if:
1. **You identify and have first conversation with the strategist partner candidate in the next 14 days** — not a hire, a peer with creative-director chops who would equity-share or rev-share. Without them, council says don't start.
2. **You stand up the production rig (Arcads + Captions + custom prompts) and ship a real winner pack** — 20 variations on a fake product — in week 1. Proof of velocity before you pitch a client.
3. **You pick a vertical wedge** — DTC supplements, beauty (carefully), consumer tech, productivity apps, or pet — and build a swipe library specific to it. Generic = dead.
4. **You land 1 pilot in week 3** — leverage your network for the foot-in-door pitch. 10% of spend, 30-day, performance-aligned. One pilot → case study → outbound.

## Pivot Opportunities

- **Vertical AI creative studio:** Stop pitching as "ad agency." Position as "AI creative studio for [vertical]." Charge per creative pack ($2-5K) instead of retainer. Higher velocity, more predictable revenue, easier to productize.
- **AI Ads + Presale CS combo:** Both target the same DTC buyer. Bundle: "We'll save your CAC on the front-end (AI Ads) and reduce your refund rate on the back-end (Presale CS Agent)." This is the 2-engine play with the same outbound motion.
- **Course-aligned consultancy:** If you took an AI UGC course, the instructor likely has a community. Position yourself as their preferred implementation partner. Distribution for free.

## Pass Conditions

Walk away if:
- You can't find the strategist partner in 14 days (the dependency is real — solo you're commoditized)
- Your first 5 pitches get the response "we already do this in-house with Arcads" (the arbitrage closed earlier than expected)
- Meta or TikTok publishes new policy flagging synthetic UGC at scale (existential platform risk)

## The One Question
**Who is the strategist partner candidate, and can you have a "would you do this with me" conversation with them by next Friday?** This is the single make-or-break. Don't pitch a client until that conversation happens.

# IDEA 3 — Presale Customer Service Agent

## The Idea
A Shopify app that handles support specifically for brands running presales / preorders / made-to-order / drops. Domain-specific empathy, production-timeline knowledge, reassurance language. Plugs into Gorgias / Tidio / Intercom as a specialist layer, not a replacement. Pricing: $499-$1,500/mo base + % of refunds prevented. Distribution: Shopify App Store + outbound to BackerKit alumni.

## The Customer (Jake, runs a hardware crowdfunder graduating to Shopify, $1.2M raised, shipping in 5 months)
"My BackerKit support inbox is on fire. Every day I get 40 'where is my order' DMs and my answer is the same: 'manufacturing is on schedule, ETA September, here's the latest update.' But if I copy-paste that I sound like a robot, and if I ignore them they cancel and chargeback. If you can give me an agent that responds with ACTUAL empathy — names their order, references their backing tier, knows what day of the timeline they're in — and prevents even half of the refunds I'm seeing right now, I'd pay $1,500/mo for the wait window without blinking. But this needs to be plug-and-play with my Gorgias setup. If I have to migrate, I'm out."

**One-liner:** *"Give me empathy at scale, save my refund rate, integrate with my Gorgias. Take my $1,500/mo."*

## The Competitor (Gorgias product manager)
"You're right that we don't focus on presale — it's 3% of our customer base. The honest reason is that our enterprise customers aren't running drops or crowdfunders. But the second your wedge proves out and we see usage data, we'll either build a 'preorder mode' (4-engineer team, 90-day ship) or acquire you. Your 18-month window is real but smaller than you think — and our existing distribution means we don't need to outbuild you, we just need to ship a 70% solution to our 50,000 stores. The defensible position is being the API layer that all CS bots read from (the Knownly idea you considered) — but that's a harder business."

**One-liner:** *"You have 18 months before we ship preorder mode and reset the math."*

## The Investor
"Pure vertical SaaS. TAM is genuinely small — maybe 30-80K Shopify stores running active preorder workflows (BackerKit alumni + drop brands + made-to-order luxury + crowdfunder graduates). At 5% capture and $499/mo, that's $750K-$2M ARR in the base case. At $1,500/mo with the refund-prevention upsell, plausibly $3-6M ARR. Bootstrappable, attractive cash flow, but venture-uninteresting unless you expand horizontally (which kills the niche moat). This is a 'great cash-flowing business' shape, not a 'venture rocket' shape. Path to $20K MRR: 40 stores at $499 = $20K. Realistic in 90-120 days through BackerKit + Shopify App Store + outbound to crowdfunder alumni lists."

**One-liner:** *"Boring beautiful cash-flowing SaaS. Not venture-attractive. Maybe that's exactly what The Architect wants."*

## The Mentor
"Looking at your profile: 'You tend to think in 5-year arcs while still executing in 90-day sprints.' Presale CS is the perfect Architect business — every customer makes it smarter, every order enriches the empathy templates, every brand that ships becomes a case study. It compounds. The trades-network thing was real, but THIS is closer to your actual scar tissue: you imported from China and watched your customers wait. You ARE the buyer. The Mentor caveat: this is a longer time-to-revenue than the Customer Interaction SaaS or AI Ads. 90-120 days vs 60 days. If your $20K MRR clock is HARD at 90 days and you have no other income runway, this could starve you out before it pays. But if you can survive the slower ramp, it's the most defensible business in your portfolio."

**One-liner:** *"This is the business you'll still love in year 5. The other two might be the business that pays the bills in year 1."*

## Path to BUILD

This becomes a clear BUILD if:
1. **You get 3 BackerKit alumni or active presale Shopify stores to commit to a 30-day manual pilot in the next 14 days** — you run their support inbox yourself with Claude / GPT-4 as the engine. Validates demand AND captures the empathy playbook
2. **You document every emotional pattern, every timeline question, every successful save** during the pilot — this IS the product
3. **You hire (or trade equity for) a Shopify App developer** — most likely candidate is a freelancer for $3-5K, OR a strategic equity slice for an indie Shopify App developer who wants to co-found
4. **You ship the App Store listing by day 60** with the empathy templates baked in + Gorgias / Tidio integration
5. **You hit 10 paying customers by day 90** at $499 average = $5K MRR. Then it compounds.

## Pivot Opportunities

- **BackerKit partnership:** Skip the Shopify App Store grind. Pitch BackerKit directly as their official "graduation support layer" for crowdfunders moving to Shopify. They have the distribution you need.
- **Refund-prevention SaaS, vertical-agnostic:** If presale is too narrow, expand the wedge to "refund prevention for all ecommerce" — but you lose the empathy-as-product moat. Riskier.
- **Run as a service first:** If the dev cost is too high, run it as a managed service ($1,500/mo, you-do-the-work) for 6-12 months until you have enough revenue to fund the App build. You become the product temporarily — which violates the archetype but funds the asset.

## Pass Conditions

Walk away if:
- Fewer than 3 of your 10 outreach attempts to BackerKit alumni respond positively in week 1 (demand signal is weak)
- The Shopify App build economics don't work under your $5K budget AND you can't find a dev partner
- The 90-day clock is hard and you have no other income — this one takes longer than the other two

## The One Question
**Can you get 3 active presale Shopify stores (BackerKit alumni or drop brands) to commit to a 30-day manual pilot — paid or unpaid — in the next 14 days?** If yes, the playbook validates and the App build follows. If no, the demand was your assumption, not reality.

# SYNTHESIS — The Council's Comparison

## Speed-to-$20K MRR
| Idea | Realistic Timeline | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| AI Ads Agency | 60-75 days (3-5 retainer clients) | High if strategist partner locks |
| Customer Interaction SaaS | 75-90 days (50-135 paying customers via trades network) | Medium-high; distribution is the gating risk |
| Presale CS Agent | 90-120 days (40 paying customers at $499) | High demand confidence, slower ramp |

## Build-a-Machine Score (Architect-fit)
| Idea | Machine Potential | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Presale CS Agent | 9 | Pure SaaS, every customer makes it smarter, compounds forever |
| Customer Interaction SaaS | 8 | Lean SaaS but you're in the buyer's living room (sales-heavy) for 12+ months |
| AI Ads Agency | 5 | Agency = trading time. Only becomes a machine if you productize the creative stack. |

## Founder-Excitement Test (the unspoken variable)
The council can't score this — only you can. The Mentor's parting line on each:
- **Customer Interaction SaaS:** "Are you sure you want to be in the room with this customer for 5 years?"
- **AI Ads Agency:** "Does this excite you, or does it feel safe because you have the playbook?"
- **Presale CS Agent:** "This is the business you'll still love in year 5."

## The Council's Final Recommendation

**Single bet:** Presale CS Agent. Slowest to revenue, highest defensibility, most aligned with the 5-year Architect arc. The mentor's "year 5" line is the tell.

**Two-engine bet:** AI Ads Agency + Presale CS Agent. Same DTC buyer = your outreach engine doubles in value. AI Ads funds the slower Presale build. The strategist partner unlocks AI Ads; the dev partner unlocks Presale. Both partnerships happen in parallel.

**If 90 days is HARD-hard and you'll starve without revenue:** Customer Interaction SaaS. Fastest credible cash with founder-fit unlocked, BUT you'll spend 12 months on the phone with HVAC owners, which the Mentor flags as a Tuesday-test risk.
