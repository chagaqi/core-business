# 1. Free Functional Consumables Ad Teardown — *the flagship magnet*

## The concept (and why it's the flagship)

This is the inbound mirror of the cold-outreach sample. Where outreach hands a priority prospect a *rendered ad of their product*, the Teardown hands a self-selected inbound lead a **5–7 minute personalized Loom** where Chaga pulls up their live Meta/TikTok ads (or their site + reviews if ads aren't visible) and breaks down — as an operator who's run the account, not a critic — what's carrying the account, what's about to hit the 7-week wall, and the 4 angles he'd test next.

It is the single best MOFU asset in the business because it *is* the service in miniature: it demonstrates judgment (which angle leads for their AOV), it's specific to their brand (kills "you don't know my brand"), and it ends with a natural, earned ask — not a pitch.

**It also pre-qualifies.** The intake form makes a brand tell us their spend before we spend 40 minutes on a Loom. Below $20K/mo routes to the downsell or the Library; $20–100K/mo routes to a hand-built teardown.

## The promise (what the opt-in commits to)

> A real operator who ran his own functional-consumables P&L records you a 5–7 minute video teardown of your current Meta/TikTok creative: which ad is actually carrying the account, which winner is about to die on the 7-week fatigue clock, and the 4 angles I'd put into testing next for *your* AOV and SKU mix. No deck, no generic "best practices" PDF — your account, my read, in your inbox.

**Guardrails on the promise (keep it honest, keep it deliverable):**
- "5–7 minutes" is a ceiling we hit, not a floor we pad to. A tight 5 beats a rambling 9.
- We promise a *read and 4 angles*, not produced creative. The produced sample is what the outbound motion gives a priority prospect — don't blur the two or the free sample loses its scarcity.
- Turnaround promise: **within 3 business days.** State it; honor it. (Time Delay is already a solved lever — don't break it here.)
- Hard cap on volume so quality holds: we say "I record a handful of these a week" — never imply unlimited, because that signals it's automated, i.e. slop.

## The intake (Typeform) — qualify spend + brand + goals

> **Design rule:** one question per screen, conversational, operator-voice. The form *itself* is a brand impression — no corporate "What is your company size?" filler. Question 5 (spend) is the qualifier; everything routes off it. Question 9 (ad library link) is what makes the teardown buildable without a back-and-forth.

**Intake form copy:**

1. **Welcome screen.**
   > You're about to get a real teardown — me, on a Loom, going through your actual ads like I would my own account. Six questions so I can do it right. Takes 90 seconds.
   *(Button: "Let's go")*

2. **What's the brand?** *(short text — brand name)*

3. **Drop your site link.** *(URL — so I can pull your product, your AOV signal, and your reviews)*

4. **Which lane are you in?** *(multiple choice — single select)*
   - Mushroom coffee / functional coffee
   - Adaptogen gummies
   - Functional beverages (RTD, tonics, sodas)
   - Greens / superfood powders
   - Nootropics / nootropic stacks
   - Mushroom chocolates
   - Other functional consumable *(reveals short text)*

5. **Roughly what are you spending on Meta + TikTok ads a month, combined?** *(multiple choice — single select — THE QUALIFIER)*
   - Under $20K/mo
   - $20K–$50K/mo
   - $50K–$100K/mo
   - $100K+/mo
   *(No "prefer not to say." If they won't tell me spend, they're not ready for a teardown — the form ends politely.)*

6. **Your rough AOV and target CPA?** *(short text — "I read the whole teardown through your unit economics, not generic best practices.")*

7. **What's the one thing breaking right now?** *(multiple choice — single select, routes the emotional framing of the Loom)*
   - A winner that used to print money just stopped working *(→ Track 1 / Archetype A framing)*
   - We can't physically produce creative fast enough to keep testing *(→ Track 2 / capacity framing)*
   - We're testing plenty but nothing new is breaking out
   - CPMs/CPAs climbing and we don't know if it's creative or the account
   - Honestly, things are fine — I just want a second operator's eyes

8. **If we found a winner, what would you do with it?** *(short text — surfaces their real goal: scale, lower CAC, hit a raise number, free up the team. This is the line I close the Loom on.)*

9. **Paste your Meta Ad Library link** (or 1–2 specific ads you want me to look at). *(URL / long text — "Public, takes you 20 seconds: facebook.com/ads/library → search your page. This is what lets me tear down your real ads, not guess from your homepage.")*

10. **Best email for the Loom?** *(email — the opt-in capture)*

11. **End screen.**
    > Got it. I record a handful of these a week, so yours lands in your inbox within 3 business days. While you wait — here are 50 functional-consumables ads I've already broken down. *(Link to Magnet 2.)*

**Routing logic (built into the Typeform):**
- **Under $20K/mo →** redirect to a soft end screen: "You're a little under where the teardown pays off, but the Ad Library (link) is built for exactly your stage — and when you cross $20K, come back." (Captures email, drops them in nurture, doesn't burn a hand-built Loom on an off-ICP lead.)
- **$100K+/mo →** still record it (referral/credibility target) but the Loom and follow-up use the honest capacity line: "at your scale I'm one angle-family of leverage, not your whole creative department."
- **$20–100K/mo →** the ICP. Hand-built teardown, full follow-up sequence.

## The fulfillment format — the 5–7 min personalized Loom

- **Medium:** Loom, screen + face cam (face cam non-negotiable — this is a *person who ran a store* talking to them, the whole proof is that it's not automated).
- **Screen:** their actual Meta Ad Library / the ads they flagged, their site, their reviews. Pulled up live. No slides.
- **Length discipline:** 5–7 min. If their account needs more, that's the call, not the Loom.
- **Delivery:** sent from Chaga's email, subject line `Teardown: [Brand] — what I'd test next`, one or two plain sentences in the body, the soft ask under the video.
- **Naming/tracking:** every Loom tagged in a simple CRM with their spend tier + the "what's breaking" answer, so follow-up is segmented A vs B/C from the first touch.

## LOOM SCRIPT TEMPLATE (reusable) — "the teardown that earns the call"

> **Structure: Recognize → Diagnose → Prescribe (4 angles) → Earn the ask.** ~6 minutes. Voice = operator who's run the account at 1am, not a critic scoring their homework. The whole thing has to feel like a favor, not a funnel. The pivot rule from the offer applies: if they're Track 1 (winner died), *enter* on recognition then move fast to capacity; if Track 2, skip grief entirely and open on throughput.

```
[0:00–0:30] OPEN — name them, set the frame, no preamble
"Hey [First name] — Chaga here. You asked for a teardown of [Brand], so
this is me actually going through your account the way I would if it were
mine. I'm not going to wave credentials at you — I'd rather just show you
I get your ads. So I'm reading this through your P&L and your buyer, not a
best-practices checklist. Let's get into it."

[0:30–1:45] RECOGNIZE — show them you see what's actually working
Pull up their ads. Name the one carrying the account, specifically.
"This one — the [describe hook] — this is your workhorse. I can tell
from [the variations you've spun off it / how long it's been live] that
it's carrying a lot of the account right now."
→ TRACK 1 (winner-died answer): "And my guess is it doesn't hit like it
   used to. That's not you losing your touch — that creative's just past
   the wall. Hold that thought."
→ TRACK 2 (capacity answer): skip the wound. "Strategy here is sound.
   What I'm watching is throughput — you've got [N] live and they're all
   variations of two ideas. That's a production-hands problem, not a
   strategy problem."

[1:45–3:00] DIAGNOSE — the 7-week bridge, made concrete to THEIR account
"Here's the thing every account in this category runs into: creative
fatigues around the 7-week mark. Your winner here looks like it's been
live [~X weeks] — so it's either at the wall or coming up on it fast.
The defense is having the next winner already in the auction before this
one dies. And right now I count about [N] genuinely distinct angles
you're testing — everything else is a variation of those. That's the
gap: not your strategy, your *coverage*."
(Tie it to their unit economics from intake: "At a [$X] AOV and [$Y]
target CPA, you can't afford to coast on one winner.")

[3:00–5:00] PRESCRIBE — THE PAYOFF: the 4 angles I'd test next
This is the section that proves judgment. Be specific to their SKU,
their reviews, their AOV. Pull a real customer review on screen.
"So if this were my account, here are the 4 angles I'd put into testing
next cycle:
  1. [Angle from a recurring theme in THEIR reviews — e.g. the
     jitters-free / no-crash angle for mushroom coffee]
  2. [Angle from an objection their AOV implies — price-justification /
     cost-per-serving math]
  3. [A format angle their current ads aren't running — e.g. founder-
     direct, or problem-aware vs solution-aware]
  4. [A claim-gated angle — 'and this one only if you can substantiate
     [claim], which is exactly the kind of thing I screen before
     anything renders, so you don't catch a Meta strike']
For a $90-AOV SKU I'd lead with [#X], not [#Y] — and that call is the
whole game. ChatGPT will write you all four scripts. It won't tell you
which one to put your spend behind for *your* economics."
(Plant the judgment seed — the moat, stated, not claimed.)

[5:00–6:15] EARN THE ASK — soft, optional, two paths
"That's the read. You can take these 4 angles and run them yourself —
genuinely, no strings, that's a real plan.
The only reason it usually doesn't happen is the same reason you asked
for this in the first place: producing enough variations on all 4 to
actually find the winner is a lot of hands you don't have spare.
That's the part I do — I run 4 angles into 24 variations every 2 weeks,
launched and tagged in your own account, and I cap at 5 brands so nobody
gets deprioritized.
Two ways to go deeper if you want:
 - I've got a one-time 10-video pack for $497 — basically, I produce one
   of these angles so you can see the quality on your own product before
   anything bigger.
 - Or grab 15 minutes on my calendar [link] and I'll walk you through
   what a full cycle on [Brand] would actually look like — and I'll show
   you two more teardowns I've done so you're not betting on a stranger.
Either way — hope the 4 angles are useful. Go win." 
```

**Close-on-their-goal override:** if intake Q8 gave a strong goal (e.g. "free up my 2 ops people," "hit our raise number"), replace the generic "Go win" with a callback to it: *"You said you want to stop being the production bottleneck for your two ops people — that's exactly the thing I take off the plate. Go win."*

**On-voice guardrails for every Loom:** name a real number from their account on screen; never say "revolutionary/game-changing"; name the consumption-B-roll failure mode if they ask why their AI tests looked like slop; never promise whole-account ROAS — the guarantee is hold-rate + CPA-flat, and the teardown promises *angles*, not results.

# 2. The Functional Consumables Ad Library — 50 breakdowns

## The concept

A swipe file with operator commentary: **50 real functional-consumables video ads, each broken down** for the angle it's running, the hook mechanism, why it works (or where it's about to fatigue), and what a brand should steal from it. It's authority and a wide-net list builder — the asset a founder bookmarks and a media buyer forwards to their founder. It establishes "the functional-consumables ad guy" before any sales conversation, which is the entire positioning play ("the mushroom-coffee guy beats an AI-ads guy").

It is deliberately **not gated behind a call and not personalized** — it's the cheap top of funnel that earns the email, then routes everyone toward the personalized Teardown (Magnet 1).

## Structure (50 breakdowns)

**Format:** Notion site or a clean PDF. Each of the 50 entries is one consistent card:

| Field | What goes in it |
|---|---|
| **The ad** | Screenshot or embedded clip (from Meta Ad Library — public) |
| **Brand + lane** | e.g. "Mushroom coffee, ~$X AOV category" |
| **Angle** | The one hypothesis it's testing (e.g. "no-crash / jitter-free") |
| **Hook mechanism** | First 3 seconds — what it does to win the hold-rate |
| **Why it works** | The operator read — 2–3 sentences, P&L-aware |
| **Fatigue / risk flag** | "Running 14+ weeks — past the wall" or "claim risk: implies a disease benefit" |
| **Steal this** | One concrete tactic a reader can apply this week |

**Organize into the 6 lanes** (mushroom coffee, adaptogen gummies, functional beverages, greens/superfood, nootropics, mushroom chocolates) so a founder jumps straight to their category — which doubles as proof of vertical depth.

**Open with a one-page "how to read an ad like an operator"** primer that teaches the angle/hook/hold-rate lens — so the library trains them to value Chaga's judgment, which is what the Teardown sells.

## How to build it cheaply

- **Source 100% from the Meta Ad Library** (public, free). Filter to functional-consumables pages spending actively. Zero licensing cost.
- **Batch the breakdowns** — Chaga writes the operator read (the part that can't be outsourced; it's the whole value). A template + the 7-field card keeps each entry to ~15 minutes. 50 entries ≈ 2–3 focused days.
- **Build in Notion**, publish as a public-but-email-gated page (or export a PDF). No design budget; clean type, consistent cards.
- **Evergreen + compounding:** every fresh ad Chaga reviews for the business becomes a new card. The library grows as a byproduct of the work — and "updated to 60+" is a re-engagement email later.
- **Honesty note:** these are *other brands'* ads with public commentary — never imply Chaga made them. The library is analysis, not a portfolio. (The portfolio gap is real until the founding-partner case studies land; don't paper over it here.)

# 3. AI UGC Setup Guide — Arcads/Creatify + script prompts

## The concept

A short, genuinely useful guide that hands a founder the *exact tool stack and prompts* to make an AI UGC ad themselves with Arcads or Creatify. It looks like we're giving away the service — and that's the strategy. Anyone who tries it runs straight into the real bottleneck (it takes hours, the first outputs look like slop without brand context, and they still don't know *which* angle to put spend behind). The guide makes that wall visible, then nurture positions Chaga as the hands and the judgment that get them past it.

This is the **counter-narrative magnet**: it pre-handles "AI ads are slop" and "I can do this free" by *showing* the reader where DIY breaks, honestly — which builds more trust than asserting we're better.

## Outline of the guide

**Title:** *The AI UGC Setup Guide: How to Make Your First Functional-Consumables Ad with Arcads or Creatify (and where it stops working).*

1. **The honest intro.** "This is the real stack I use. I'm giving it to you because the tool was never the hard part — and you should see that for yourself." Sets the frame: tool ≠ moat.

2. **The tool stack.** Arcads vs Creatify — what each is good at, ~$110/mo reality, where the avatars hold up (product-forward) and where they break (the **consumption "drink-and-react" B-roll** — name the failure mode openly, the way the offer does). Tell them to route around face-on-sip with hands-and-pour and voiceover.

3. **Brand context in, or slop out.** The single biggest DIY mistake: generating with no brand context. Exactly what to feed the tool — your reviews, your voice, your top objections, your AOV — so the output stops being generic. *(This is the u/uitracer9818 lesson, taught straight.)*

4. **The script prompt templates.** 3–4 copy-paste prompts for the core functional-consumables angles (no-crash/energy, problem-aware, founder-direct, cost-per-serving justification). These are real and they work — generosity is the point.

5. **The claim-compliance checklist.** A short FTC/Meta screen — no implied disease claims, no unsubstantiated "clinically proven," substantiation-gate every health claim. Genuinely protects their account, and quietly demonstrates the judgment layer that DIY won't give them.

6. **The QC pass.** The slop-bar checklist: mouth-sync, hands, render quality, brand-safety, claim language — what to kill before it touches spend.

7. **Where this stops scaling (the honest close).** "You can make *one* good ad this way. Here's what happens when you need 24 every 2 weeks: angle selection, production hours, QC, launch, tagging, reading results — that's not a tool problem, it's a hands-and-judgment problem. That's the part I do." Links to the Teardown (Magnet 1) and the $497 pack.

## Where it sits + how it's used

- **Funnel position:** **MOFU.** Two jobs: (1) a *secondary opt-in magnet* for the more technical/skeptical buyer who wants to "just try it themselves" — capture them rather than lose them; (2) a **nurture trust-builder** — dripped to Teardown and Library subscribers as the email that disarms "AI is slop / I can DIY" before the pack offer.
- **Build cost:** near-zero — it's Chaga's real workflow written down once. Evergreen.

- **Subhead:** Make your first functional-consumables UGC ad yourself: the tools, the brand-context inputs that keep it from looking like slop, copy-paste script prompts, and the claim-compliance checklist that keeps your account safe. Free, and genuinely the real workflow.
