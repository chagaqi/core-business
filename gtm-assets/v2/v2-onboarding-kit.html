Tideover — Concierge Pilot Onboarding Kit (v2)

What this is. Everything that turns a "yes, I'll pilot" into a live concierge partner. It picks up where the teardown call ends. Merge fields write {{like_this}} and are the only blanks.

Who runs it. Chaga, personally. Agents draft, Chaga approves.

Proof-only (enforced below). No fabricated refund stats, case studies, testimonials, ratings, or logos. The refund-reduction case study doesn't exist yet; a presale cycle runs 60 to 120 days, so leading-indicator and founding-partner framing goes where proof would. The operator background is a war story. The revenue figure is warm-conversation-only and never appears in a sent asset.

What's inside. A free pilot agreement (3 variants), the intake questionnaire, the access checklist, the SLA explainer, a 5-touch email sequence, and Chaga's run-of-show. Use it the moment a partner says yes.

2. BRAND & TIMELINE INTAKE QUESTIONNAIRE

The diagnostic that produces the inputs for your day-stage script library. Send as a Google Form, Notion form, or fillable doc right after the agreement is signed. We're learning your world, not auditing you. About 12 to 15 minutes. A 6-question fast-start follows for hot partners.

Section 1 — Brand voice & customer

  1. In one sentence, what do you make and who buys it?
  2. Paste 2 to 3 real replies your team has sent customers. This is how we learn your actual voice. Screenshots are fine.
  3. How would you describe your tone? Pick all that fit or write your own: warm, playful, premium, no-nonsense, nerdy-technical, earnest, cheeky, understated, {{other}}.
  4. Words, emojis, or phrases you love or ban? ("we never say 'unfortunately'," "sign off as 'the {{brand}} crew'," "no exclamation points").
  5. What does your buyer care about most while they wait? Quality, being first, the story, just getting the thing. This tells us what reassurance lands.

Section 2 — The presale timeline (the core of the product)

  1. Walk us through your real production timeline, start to finish. Use honest confidence bands, not hard promises, because that's exactly how we'll talk to your customers. Fill what applies:
    StageWhat's happeningWhen (days from order, as a band)
    Order placed → payment clearedDay 0

    Tooling / sampling{{e.g. days 10–30}}
    Production run{{e.g. days 30–70}}
    QC / inspection{{e.g. days 70–80}}

  1. What's the single most common reason this slips, and by how long? We never hide slips. We get ahead of them, and this is how.
  2. What do you currently tell customers their ship window is? The number on the product page or confirmation email.
  3. Multiple customer groups on different timelines right now? Common for crowdfunding graduates: Kickstarter backers vs. BackerKit late-pledges vs. new Shopify preorders. List each group and its timeline. This is the #1 source of "support can't tell who is who," so detail matters.
  4. Region split? US ships first, EU three to four weeks later. List regions and windows.
  5. Deposit or partial-payment orders? If yes: how much up front, when's the balance charged, and how do customers usually ask about it?

Section 3 — The support reality today

  1. Roughly how many presale messages a week right now? A guess is fine.
  2. Top 3 questions you get over and over during the wait? Paste the actual wording: "any update?", "did my order go through?", "where IS my order."
  3. Your worst presale support story? A chargeback, a "scam" accusation, a flood you couldn't keep up with. This tells us where the real damage is.
  4. Have presale waits cost you chargebacks, disputes, or refund requests? A rough sense of how often is plenty. We're not recording a refund baseline as a claim, just understanding the pain.
  5. What helpdesk and preorder app do you use? Gorgias, Tidio, Intercom, plain email, {{other}}. Preorder app: PreProduct, Pre-Order Now, Timesact, {{other}}.
  6. Who answers these messages now, and how fast? You, a VA, a small team, nobody for hours.

Section 4 — Logistics & guardrails

  1. What are you 100% NOT okay with us saying to a customer? Never promise a hard date, never offer a discount, never mention the factory, never say "delayed."
  2. When can we offer something concrete (priority dispatch, a small make-good, a hold) without checking first, and where's the line where we must ask you?
  3. Who's our contact for "we need a human answer fast," and on what channel? {{name}}, {{Slack/SMS/email}}.
  4. What does a win look like for this pilot? Faster responses, a quieter inbox, fewer disputes. We baseline and measure toward exactly this.

Done. We turn this into your day-stage scripts within 48 hours and walk you through them before a single reply goes out.

4. THE TWICE-DAILY SLA EXPLAINER (partner-facing)

A clean one-pager the partner keeps. Sets the AM/PM cadence as a feature and explains what happens between windows. Send with the welcome email.

How your presale support runs during the pilot

The short version. I work your presale inbox in two focused windows every business day, one morning and one afternoon. In each window, every anxious buyer gets an accurate, human-approved reply. Between windows, a small set of pre-approved holding notes keeps things warm so nobody sits in silence.

Why two windows instead of "instant"? What saves a presale buyer isn't speed, it's the right reassurance at the right stage of the wait. A rushed "thanks for your patience!" at day 89 is what makes someone hit dispute. And I'd rather promise a cadence I hit every day than a "24/7" I can't.

Your daily windows

  • Morning: {{am_window}} {{timezone}}. I clear everything that came in overnight.
  • Afternoon: {{pm_window}} {{timezone}}. I clear everything since the morning.
  • Between windows: only the pre-approved holding messages send automatically. No novel reply goes out without a human in the loop.

What jumps to the front for a same-window human reply:

  • A dispute or chargeback threat ("last chance before I charge back").
  • A very upset customer or a "scam" accusation.
  • Anything time-sensitive and concrete, like a wrong address before dispatch.

These get flagged the moment they arrive and answered by a human in the current or next window, never parked behind routine WISMO.

What this protects. Your customers get calm, accurate answers, and I stay sharp enough to write replies that actually work instead of firefighting 200 identical "any update?" emails in real time.

Weekends and holidays. Holding messages still go out. Full windows resume the next business day. Running a launch or a known crunch? Tell me and we add a window for that stretch.

Pre-approved holding templates (auto-send between windows)

The only messages that go out without a live human, and only after you sign off on this exact set. They make zero new promises and never invent a date. They confirm receipt, restate the ETA band you already gave, and set the next-window expectation.

1 — Generic receipt + ETA band

Hi {{first_name}} — got your message, thank you. Quick note so you're not left wondering: your order is in your expected window of {{eta_band}}, and I'll have a full update for you by {{next_window}}. Talk soon. — {{brand}} Support

2 — "any update?" / WISMO acknowledgment

Hey {{first_name}} — thanks for checking in. Your order is still tracking to {{eta_band}}. I'm pulling your exact current production stage and will reply in detail by {{next_window}}. — {{brand}} Support

3 — High-anxiety soft-hold

Hi {{first_name}} — I hear you, and I don't want to rush an answer to something this important. I've flagged your message for a proper reply by {{next_window}} with your exact status and next step. You're not being ignored. — {{brand}} Support

Guardrails baked in: no hard ship date, no discount or refund offer, no "delayed" language, no factory mention. Each holding message only restates the band the customer was already promised and points to the next window.
