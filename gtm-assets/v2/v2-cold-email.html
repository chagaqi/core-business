Tideover — Cold Email System

Venture 2 · Design-partner acquisition · Founder: Chaga Chai
Built to outreach-playbook §4.1 (cold email) and §5.2 (Tideover), structure-matched to the proven scripts in real-scripts-library.md. Beachhead: Kickstarter / Indiegogo / BackerKit graduates moving onto Shopify, then made-to-order and drop brands.

What this is. Cold outbound to book the five founding-partner pilots this cohort. Separate from the nurture sequence, which warms people who already raised a hand. These go to named crowdfunding graduates and made-to-order brands who've never heard of Tideover.

The spine (§5.2). Every email opens on one true, currently-live fact about their presale wait, then hands over a finished made-for-them artifact (the teardown or the day-stage breakdown), permission-gated. Strength leads every time: the live observation and the artifact come first; the operator war story carries the credibility, late. Tideover is a concierge service proving a playbook, not a finished product, so this is hands-on, not software, but that lands as a clause, never the opener. The ask is small: a reply or 15 minutes, never the retainer.

The structure parents (real-scripts-library.md). Every template below mirrors a published operator script. Each one names its parent in the rationale note. The send-ready copy stays clean; the citation lives in the note layer.

The one upgrade — ARTIFACT-FIRST. Where the real operators open with a claim ("I have some ideas," "I help businesses fix that," "I have an idea that can get your next 100 customers"), we open with the finished made-for-them thing: the free teardown or the day-stage breakdown we already built for their exact case. The artifact replaces the claim. That is the only thing we run differently from the parents, and it is called out at each section.

Proof-only (brand kit §8, non-negotiable). No refund-reduction case study exists yet. A cycle takes 60 to 120 days; the proof is being made now, in these pilots. No fabricated stats, testimonials, ratings, or logos. Any external number stays the source's, cited to its name, never restated as Tideover's own result (HubSpot's "triple the run rate," anyone's revenue, all theirs). The "$2M" figure is warm-call only. In cold email the operator background is a war story: "I ran fulfillment, in-house shipping, and the where's-my-order comms for a physical-goods brand for years." Scarcity is real: five founding-partner pilots this cohort, and I run 1 to 2 at a time, because the work is done by hand.

0. How to use this

Send from Chaga's aged personal inbox, not the fresh tideover.app domain (still in ~21-day warmup; reserved for next-cohort sends once warm). No verified email but a handle found? Platform DM instead (connection-note rule below).

Two fields make or break every send:

  1. {{presaleSignal}} — one true, specific, currently-live observation. Generic kills the send.
  2. {{firstName}} / {{brand}} — clean and natural, never a scraped campaign title.

A tool pulls the raw signal; a human writes the hook. Never let AI write {{presaleSignal}} or any per-prospect line. Auto-generated personalization now converts worse than none (per outreach-proof.md tactic 3, 8 sources). Hand-build both fields per priority lead (§3.6).

Cadence (§4.1, outreach-proof.md tactic 4): Day 1 → Day 5 → Day 10 → Day 15 → Day 20 breakup. Five touches over a ~20-day window. In-thread bumps, blank subject after the first (Sam Nelson Agoge reply-bump mechanic). The Day-5 follow-up is the single most valuable message in the sequence (the first follow-up carries the most replies), so it's the most value-dense touch, never "just bumping this." Each later touch carries new info too. Stop the second they reply. Any reply moves to a real conversation. The retainer is never quoted in cold email. Hold at 3 to 5 touches; never pad past 6 or compress to a few-day burst.

Permission-gate the artifact (per outreach-proof.md tactics 5 + 10). The free teardown and the Playbook are the value. The higher-converting move is to ask "want me to send it?" first and deliver on the yes, not to embed the link in the touch (the 150-brand video campaign and Fazio's "no link = ~3x" both land here). Every send below leads with the permission-ask. An embedded-link variant is kept only as an explicit A/B; permission-first is the favored default.
