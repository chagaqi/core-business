Venture 2 — Tideover (tideover.app). Founder: Chaga Chai. Beachhead: Kickstarter / Indiegogo / BackerKit creators moving onto Shopify, 60-to-120-day fulfillment windows, big backer lists. "To tide someone over": get anxious presale buyers through the wait, calm and aboard, until their order ships.

Voice: warm, calm, steady. A helpful operator who's been in your inbox.

This file is structure-matched to the Real Scripts Library (real-scripts-library.md). Every template below mirrors a proven, published operator script (or the playbook's artifact-first arc). The send-ready copy is clean; each section carries a one-line Structure note in the rationale layer naming the real parent it mirrors and the link. The one upgrade — ARTIFACT-FIRST. Where the real operators open with a claim (Welsh's "love what you're doing," Ruiz's "are you looking to," Mahrle's "I'm building the sales team"), Tideover opens with a finished made-for-them thing: the free 15-min teardown of their own presale inbox. HOW TO USE THIS

  1. Match one visible signal. Open with the variant whose trigger is on their page right now. A human reads the page and writes the signal line. Claim a trigger you can't see, or let AI guess one, and you burn the lead.
  2. LinkedIn: no note, or one ultra-specific note under 180 chars (Section 4), opener on accept. Connected, send the opener.
  3. X: reply or quote a real post first when you can (Section 5), then DM.
  4. Permission-gate the artifact (default). Don't drop the teardown booking link or the Playbook in touch 1. Offer it ("want me to send it?") and deliver on the yes. The embedded-link opener is an explicit A/B only; permission-first is the favored default. (per outreach-proof.md: permission-gate, Tactics 5 + 10.)
  5. One link, one CTA. When the link does go out, it's [BookingLink], after the yes. Never in a connection note.
  6. Three to five touches over ~20 days, then stop. The first follow-up is the single most valuable message; make it the most value-dense. Log sent_date and reply. On a reply, route to Section 6.

Each opener has a LinkedIn version (full) and an X/short version. For IG, send the X version. All lead with their situation, carry the war story lightly, end on a permission-ask soft offer.

SECTION 2 — FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCES

Cadence (per outreach-proof.md Tactic 4, 7 sources): 3 to 5 touches total over a ~20-day window (the opener is touch 1). Space them ~3, ~4, ~5 business days apart. Touch 2 is the most valuable message after the opener, so it's the most value-dense: lead with the single best tactic, no ask. New angle every time, never "just bumping this." No reply after the last touch, mark no-reply and move on. Never compress to a few-day burst, never drift past 5. Each touch has a value-add and a pattern-interrupt option. Rotate so a community doesn't see the same bump twice. For X, compress to the core line and hold the [BookingLink] until they engage.

Structure note — parent: Josh Braun, "three follow-ups that replace 'just checking in'" ([VERBATIM] — https://joshbraun.com/how-to-write-follow-up-emails/). Braun's rule: every bump is a deposit (data, praise, or resource), never a bare "any update?", and none reference the prior message. Touch 2A/3A below are Braun's value-bumps (a tactic, a resource), Touch 2B/3B his lighter genuine-question bumps. The break-up (Touch 4) mirrors Sam Nelson, Agoge breakup #1 ([VERBATIM] — https://blog.contactout.io/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/30MPC-x-Sam-Nelsons-Agoge-Sequence.pdf) and Braun's shame-free ghost re-engage ([VERBATIM] — https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_heres-a-cold-email-follow-up-message-i-received-activity-7085234104606019584--Egj): acknowledge a real thing on their plate, then a single low-friction ask, zero guilt. Artifact-first upgrade: Braun's bumps hand over an article or a data slideshow; ours hand over a made-for-them tactic and the teardown of their own inbox, so the deposit is built for them, not borrowed.

TOUCH 2 — the first follow-up, most value-dense (~3 days after opener)
