## PROOF RULES (read before any call)

No refund case study exists yet. A presale cycle is 60 to 120 days, so the proof can't exist until a first cohort completes. Every script sells on measured leading indicators and lived operator experience, never an invented result. Break these and you're the chatbot-demo spam these buyers already hate.

1. **No invented numbers.** No refund stat, chargeback %, case study, testimonial, rating, or logo. Nothing you can't show on a screen-share.
2. **"$2M" is warm-conversation only.** Cold, you're "a former physical-goods operator who ran fulfillment, in-house shipping, and the customer comms during long order waits." Name the figure once they're leaning in, and only because you can back it on request.
3. **Where a refund case study would go, use leading indicators.** Faster first response, fewer where-is-my-order tickets, more deflection during the pilot. The refund number comes after a complete cycle. Say so out loud.
4. **Any third-party stat is cited to its source,** never restated as Tideover's own result. (The library's weak/self-reported numbers, like HubSpot's 57%/21% or any operator's revenue, are theirs, never ours.)
5. **You screen them as hard as they screen you.** Concierge means 1 to 5 partners total. The scarcity is real. If they're not a fit, say so.
6. **Never lead with "AI."** Lead with the wound: the inbox flood, the chargebacks, the day-30 "is this a scam" email.

**The frame:** *Your presale buyers don't get cold feet. They get left in the dark. Generic helpdesks don't even know which orders are presales, or what to say at day 7 versus day 89. I lived this running fulfillment for a physical-goods brand. I want to run your presale support by hand for one cycle, free, to prove a specialist beats a generic bot.*

# PART 1 — DISCOVERY

> **Goal:** confirm the pain is real, capture the buyer's verbatim language at each day-stage, learn their stack, qualify them. You're diagnosing, not pitching. The pilot pitch fires only if the pain is live and they qualify. If not, disqualify kindly on the call.
>
> **Length:** booked for 15, runs to 25 if hot. They talk ~70%, per Miner's 80/20 rule. Ask, shut up, write down their exact words.
>
> **Pre-call:** you found them mid-fulfillment, so you're never cold on the problem. Open by naming the window.
>
> *Structure: Hormozi CLOSER discovery sequence (Clarify why they took the call → Label/mirror the goal → Overview past attempts), with Miner NEPQ's Problem-Awareness → Consequence ladder and Voss's calibrated questions threaded through the dig. Parents: [Hormozi CLOSER](https://www.jointhefollowup.com/p/alex-hormozis-closer-sales-framework), [Miner NEPQ cheat-sheet](https://irp.cdn-website.com/3b74b76a/files/uploaded/NEPQ+SALES+SECRETS+CHEAT+SHEET.pdf), [Voss calibrated questions](https://www.masterclass.com/articles/how-to-use-calibrated-questions-to-negotiate-strategically). **Artifact-first upgrade:** the real discovery frameworks open the call with the rep's agenda ("here's what made you take this call"); we open already holding a made-for-them teardown of their wait, so the call is a walkthrough of their own tickets, not a cold dig.*

## 1A — Crowdfunding graduate (primary beachhead)

### Open (drop the guard)

> "Thanks for the time, I'll hold it to the 15 we booked. I ran fulfillment and the where's-my-order comms for a physical-goods brand, and right now I'm studying one thing: how crowdfunding creators survive the support wave during the 60-to-120-day wait after the money's in. You're mid-fulfillment on [campaign], so you're living it. Here's how this'll go: I ask, you talk, I mostly shut up. By the end we'll both know whether what I do fits your situation, and if it does I'll tell you the one thing I'm offering and you can give me a straight yes, no, or not-yet. Sound fair?"

*Structure: Hormozi CLOSER "Clarify" open (why they're here, what the call is for) plus Nelson's decision pre-frame in the agenda. Parents: [Hormozi CLOSER](https://www.jointhefollowup.com/p/alex-hormozis-closer-sales-framework); decision pre-frame per outreach-proof.md Tactic 7 (Josh Nelson, the cheapest anti-ghosting fix). **Artifact-first upgrade:** the operator openers state a credential ("I run X, I have ideas"); we name their live situation and an operator past, then hand the call to their own tickets. The "by the end we'll both know" line is the decision pre-frame. Confirm the decision-maker is on the call before the full dig.*

### Map the situation

1. *"Walk me through where you are. You funded on [Kickstarter], you've got backers waiting, and now you're selling presales on Shopify too. Who's all waiting on you right now?"*
   → The three-group mess: KS backers, BackerKit late-pledges, new Shopify presales, all on different timelines.
2. *"What ship window are you telling people, and how confident are you in that date?"*
   → The gap between promised and real is where the chargebacks live.
3. *"Rough headcount waiting right now?"*
   → Your qualifier. 1,000+ in the active window means a measurable pilot. Write the number down.

*Structure: Hormozi CLOSER "Overview" (map where they are and what they've tried) + Voss's "What are we trying to accomplish here?" frame. Parents: [Hormozi CLOSER](https://www.jointhefollowup.com/p/alex-hormozis-closer-sales-framework), [Voss](https://www.masterclass.com/articles/how-to-use-calibrated-questions-to-negotiate-strategically). No artifact change here, this is pure diagnosis.*

### The pain dig (this is the whole call)

Ask slowly. After each, say nothing until they fully stop. Capture exact phrasing.

4. *"When someone emails during the wait, what does it actually say? Give me the literal wording you see over and over."*
   → Mining verbatim WISMO language. Expect "any update?", "are you guys real".
5. *"At what point does the mood flip? Day 7 they're excited. Somewhere it turns angry. When?"*
   → Pin the flip point. Usually around day 30, when the novelty dies and the date feels far.
6. *"Has anyone called you a scam or filed a dispute, even though you put the ship date everywhere?"*
   → Almost always yes. The highest-intensity wound. Let them vent and capture the dispute language.
7. *"Who's answering these right now, and how many hours a week is it eating?"*
   → Quantify the manual cost. Listen for "copy-pasting the same reply all day."
8. *"When you open the order in your helpdesk, can you even see it's a presale and when it ships? Or are you clicking into three places?"*
   → The incumbent-blindness wound. Shopify, then the order, then the preorder app.
9. *"Next cohort's bigger than this one. If the support side stays exactly like it is now, what does that look like for you in 90 days, more disputes, more hours, more Stripe stress?"*
   → The consequence question. Make *them* voice the cost of doing nothing. Don't supply the number, let the silence pull it out. This is what makes the pilot feel necessary instead of nice-to-have.

*Structure: Miner NEPQ Problem-Awareness questions (Q4 to Q8: "walk me through the biggest challenge," "how long has this been going on," "what's the impact") closing on the NEPQ Consequence question (Q9: "what happens if nothing changes in the next 6-12 months"). Parent: [Miner NEPQ cheat-sheet](https://irp.cdn-website.com/3b74b76a/files/uploaded/NEPQ+SALES+SECRETS+CHEAT+SHEET.pdf), and Voss's "What is the biggest challenge you face?" for Q4. Q9 is the consequence move flagged in outreach-proof.md Tactic 7. No artifact yet, the teardown is what made the call happen but the dig stays buyer-voiced.*

### Stack and willingness

10. *"What's your stack, and which preorder app?"* → Routes the integration decision.
11. *"What does the dispute situation cost you? Not just fees, the Stripe-account stress."*
    → "This is killing my Stripe account" is often a bigger lever than labor.
12. *"If a tool kept these buyers calm the whole wait without you writing 50 emails a day, nice-to-have or I'd-pay-for-that-today? Be honest, I'd rather hear no."*
    → "I'd pay today" versus "maybe" tells you everything.

*Structure: Miner NEPQ Commitment question ("If we could solve [problem], would that be worth it for you?") for Q12, with Prospeo's SMMA value-probe ("what's a new customer worth," "what happens if nothing changes") behind Q11. Parents: [Miner NEPQ](https://irp.cdn-website.com/3b74b76a/files/uploaded/NEPQ+SALES+SECRETS+CHEAT+SHEET.pdf), [Prospeo SMMA discovery](https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script). Q12's "I'd rather hear no" is Voss's "no"-oriented question, giving them control. No fabricated WTP numbers, theirs only.*

### Close (route to pilot or disqualify)

**Live pain + qualifies (1,000+ waiting, real tickets, owns the call):**
> "This is exactly what I've been mapping, and you've got it worse than most because of the three-groups thing. I already built something for you that lands right on this. Want me to walk you through it?" → Part 2.

**Real pain, tiny volume or off-beachhead:**
> "Genuinely useful, thank you. You're a little early for what I'm building, so I won't try to sell you. I wrote a Presale Anxiety Playbook for exactly your situation. Want me to send it? If your next campaign's bigger, hit me back."

*Structure: Prospeo SMMA close (bridge the gap, then a single soft interest-based CTA) plus Nemo/Abraham's "would you be interested?" ask shape. Parents: [Prospeo SMMA gap close](https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script), [Nick Abraham "would you be interested in speaking"](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham_coldemail-prospecting-b2bsales-activity-7119728772051832833-dVJm). **Artifact-first upgrade:** Prospeo closes with "I've got a few ideas on how to close that gap, can we grab 15 minutes," a promise of ideas. We close with "I already built something for you, want me to walk you through it?" The artifact already exists. Permission-gate it ("want me to walk you through it?" / "want me to send it?") per outreach-proof.md Tactics 5 + 10 before you drop the link.*

## 1B — Other segments (luxury made-to-order, Gorgias power user)

Run the 1A spine. Swap the open and lead with the segment's sharpest wound.

**Made-to-order luxury (expansion).** Open: *"I'm studying how made-to-order brands handle the gap between card-charged and piece-delivered."* The wedge is brand voice, not labor. A generic bot answering a four-figure buyer is a brand violation, and one bad waiting experience costs the repeat and the referral. Reframe value from cost-saving to LTV protection. Close: *"Made-to-order is the cleanest version of this. I built you a sample of how I'd answer your day-60 buyer. Want to see it?"*

**Gorgias power user (incumbent-gap wedge).** Use when they're already on a real helpdesk. Position as the layer their helpdesk doesn't have, not "switch off Gorgias." The highest-intent question: *"Did you ever file a feature request asking Gorgias to surface preorder ETA in the sidebar? How'd that go?"* If yes, they've already articulated your gap. Also probe split-pay presales: *"Does support know how much is left to charge without digging through the app?"* Close: *"It's great at the general case and bad at presales, because that's not what it's for. I drafted what the right presale reply looks like in your voice. Want me to send it over?"*

*Structure: Prospeo SMMA segment-specific discovery ("most [niche] owners deal with one of three things, which hits closest"), with the Gorgias-feature-request probe modeled on Miner's problem-awareness dig. Parents: [Prospeo SMMA](https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script), [Miner NEPQ](https://irp.cdn-website.com/3b74b76a/files/uploaded/NEPQ+SALES+SECRETS+CHEAT+SHEET.pdf). **Artifact-first upgrade:** both closes swap Prospeo's "want to hear how it works?" for "I built/drafted it for you, want to see it?", a permission-gated artifact over a pitch of ideas.*

Ours opens with the pilot as the artifact-in-motion (the free hand-run cycle on their real tickets), and the loadout/proof is shown not claimed.*

# PART 3 — OBJECTION BANK

> **Every response: clarify first, then answer.** Per outreach-proof.md Tactic 9 (Miner Clarify → Discuss → Diffuse; Nelson Acknowledge → Isolate → Handle): never fire the rebuttal at the objection as stated. Bolt a one-line clarify in front ("when you say X, what specifically do you mean?") so you answer the real objection, not the one you assumed. Each scripted answer below opens with its clarify in *[brackets]*, ask it and shut up before the rest. Then: acknowledge, reframe, honest evidence, re-ask. Never argue. Never fabricate proof. When you don't have a number, say so. Lean on: it's free, it's reversible, it's run by a real operator, the scarcity is real.
>
> *Structure: every counter mirrors Miner's "When you say [X], what specifically would you be [X] about?" clarify-first move, then answers. Parents: [Miner NEPQ objections](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), [Prospeo "I'll think about it" / price clarify](https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script), and for the trust answers, Braun's name-the-brush-off-out-loud move ([Braun "send me an email"](https://joshbraun.com/send-me-an-email/)). **Artifact-first upgrade:** where a closer would re-pitch, several answers point back to the already-built teardown / free pilot as the evidence ("judge it on the free pilot on your real tickets"), the artifact does the rebutting.*

### The #1 wall — "we already have a helpdesk"

**"We already have Gorgias / Intercom / Zendesk."**
> *[Clarify:]* "Makes sense. When you say you've got it covered, do the presale tickets actually get a different answer at day 7 versus day 80, or do they get the same shipping reply as everything else?"
> "Good, keep it. I'm not a helpdesk and I'm not asking you to switch. Gorgias is built for the general case and good at it. It's blind to presales. It can't tell your agent this order ships in August, here's what to say at day 45. I'm the layer that sits on top and makes the presale tickets right. Plenty of Gorgias users filed feature requests for exactly this and got nowhere. Every other ticket stays where it is, nothing to migrate."

**"Tidio's bot already answers where-is-my-order."**
> "For shipped orders. The problem is presales. The order hasn't started production, so a standard shipping answer is wrong, and a wrong answer at day 30 turns a calm buyer into a dispute. Mine knows the difference between 'shipped, here's tracking' and 'in production, ships August, here's why you can trust that.' That distinction is the whole thing."

**"Our preorder app handles this."**
> "Those are great at selling the presale: taking the order, the deposit, the inventory logic. They stop the second the order's placed. The 90 days of 'is this still happening' still lands in your inbox by hand. I own that part. I read the timeline your preorder app sets and I do the talking."

**"We built our own macros / Shopify Flow for this."**
> "Respect, that's the operator move, and it's why you're a good fit. Macros are static. They say the same thing at day 7 and day 89, and they don't know where a specific order is in production. The whole wound is that buyers at different days need different reassurance. I make it day-aware and order-specific without you maintaining a macro library. The pilot's free, so worst case you keep your macros."

*Structure: Miner clarify-first on the lead objection, then Nelson's isolate-and-handle. The "bolt-on, keep what you have" reframe is the tideover-brand-kit §10 #1 objection-handler. Parents: [Miner objections](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), [Nelson isolate move](https://sevenfigureagency.com/master-cold-outreach/) (via outreach-proof.md Tactic 9).*

### "AI feels impersonal"

**"AI is impersonal, our brand voice matters."**
> *[Clarify:]* "Fair, voice is the whole thing in presale. When you picture it going wrong, is it the wording sounding robotic, or is it answering the wrong thing for where the order actually is?"
> "Agreed, which is why during the pilot I approve every real reply. The AI drafts, I check the voice and the timeline, you sign off. It's more personal than the copy-paste paragraph most brands send 200 times, and it's trained on your voice and your real timeline, not a template."

**"What if the AI promises a wrong ship date?"**
> "The thing I'm most paranoid about, because a false promise in presale is worse than in normal support. So I hard-constrain it. It only quotes the real timeline band you give me. Never a made-up hard date. And every novel reply is human-approved in the pilot. If we don't know, it says 'in production, here's the honest window, I'll update you.'"

**"Our customers want a real human."**
> "And they get one. Me, during the pilot. This isn't deflect-to-a-bot-and-disappear. It's a human answering faster because the tool surfaces the right timeline instantly. The human stays. What goes is the drowning in the same question 200 times, so they're sharp on the hard ones."

*Structure: Miner clarify-first, then McKenna's pre-empt-the-feared-objection move (name the worry the buyer half-holds, refute with deeper value). Parents: [Miner clarify](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), [McKenna SMYKM element 6 pre-emption](https://www.apollo.io/magazine/the-7-elements-of-a-perfect-cold-email) (via outreach-proof.md Tactic 9).*

### "We'll do it cheaper / ourselves"

**"We'll just hire a VA."**
> *[Clarify:]* "Could work. What would you want the VA to do that's not getting done now, just clear the volume, or actually know the right thing to say at each stage?"
> "Plenty do, and here's what happens. The VA over-promises a date to make someone happy, and that's a dispute in three weeks. A VA is a person with no presale system. The pilot's free, so test whether the system beats the VA before you pay either of us. The issue was never the labor. Nobody had the right answer at the right moment."

**"We'll just over-communicate. More emails, a bigger FAQ, an announcement bar."**
> "You should, and broadcast cuts peak tickets some. But it's one-way. It can't answer this buyer about their specific order at day 52. People still email because they want to hear it about their order. Keep the FAQ. I take the inbox."

**"We'll build this ourselves once we have time."**
> "You could. It's not the LLM that's hard, that's commoditized. The hard part is the day-by-day playbook: what calms a buyer at day 7 versus day 60, what wording prevents a dispute. That's months of living it. I've lived it, and in the pilot I hand you the scripts free. Build it later if you want, you'll have a head start."

*Structure: Miner clarify-first into Cole Gordon's "the stated objection is a belief gap" diagnosis (the real belief here is "labor is the problem," reframe to "the right answer at the right moment is the problem"). Parents: [Miner clarify](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), Cole Gordon belief-ladder (via outreach-proof.md Tactic 9). **Artifact-first upgrade:** "in the pilot I hand you the scripts free" points to the artifact as the proof, not a claim.*

### Budget / timing

**"We don't have budget."**
> *[Clarify:]* "Totally fair. Is it no budget for this at all right now, or no budget until you've seen it actually work?"
> "Then you're a perfect pilot partner, the pilot's free. No card, no contract. I only talk price after it works on your real tickets, and even then it starts well under a support hire. Want in?"

**"Now's not a good time, we're slammed."**
> "That's the symptom I fix. 'Slammed' during a presale is the where-is-my-order flood. The pilot takes work off your plate. The lift on your side is granting access and approving drafts twice a day."

**"Come back after we ship this cohort."**
> "The next cohort's already loading, and the point is to be in the wait window where the pain lives. If I come back after you ship, the data's gone and so is the pain. Let me run the back half of this one free. If it's not helping, you stop. If it is, you're dialed for the next cohort."

**"Send me pricing first."**
> "There isn't any for you yet, the pilot's free, and I won't quote a monthly until we've proven it on your tickets, because I'd be guessing and so would you. What I can send is the one-page pilot terms. Fair?"

*Structure: Miner clarify-first on the budget objection ("is it no budget, or no budget until it works?"), then price-after-value defer. Parents: [Miner objections](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), [Prospeo price-defer "give you a real number" only after value](https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script) (via outreach-proof.md Tactics 8 + 9). The "send me pricing first" answer mirrors Prospeo's "depends what we'd build, let's do 15 minutes so I can give you a real number." No fabricated price-of-inaction numbers.*

### Trust / proof

**"Do you have case studies? Who else uses this?"**
> *[Clarify:]* "Reasonable to ask. Is it proof of the result you're after, or proof I won't disappear on you, those are different worries and I'll answer the real one."
> "Honest answer, no finished case study yet, and I'd be lying if I said otherwise. A presale cycle is 60 to 120 days, so the refund proof can't exist until a first cohort completes. That's why I'm running free founding-partner pilots, to create that proof on real brands like you. What I can show is the leading stuff I measure live: response time, ticket volume, deflection. I'd rather be the one person in your inbox who doesn't fake numbers."

**"How do I know this reduces refunds?"**
> "You don't yet, and neither do I. Anyone quoting a guaranteed % before running your cycle is making it up. What we prove in the pilot are the leading indicators that drive refunds down: faster first response, fewer repeat tickets, more questions handled before they escalate. The refund number comes after your cohort completes, and I'll publish whatever it actually is."

**"You're a one-person thing. What if you disappear?"**
> "Fair with anything early. Two protections. You keep every script I write, so even if I vanished tomorrow you've got the playbook. And there's no lock-in. I'm betting my reputation on these first few partners, so you'll get more attention from me than from a 50-person SaaS support queue."

**"What's your background? Why trust you on this?"**
> "I ran fulfillment, in-house shipping, and the customer comms during long order waits for a physical-goods brand. I've sat in the inbox answering where-is-my-order for the hundredth time and watched a calm customer turn into a dispute. Not a tech founder who read about the problem. That's why I can write the reassurance scripts better than a generic tool." [Warm, substantiable moment only: "It was around a $2M/year operation."]

**"We tried an AI bot and it was bad."**
> *[Clarify:]* "I believe it. When you say it was bad, what specifically went wrong, did it give a confidently wrong answer, did it sound robotic, or did it just deflect people in a loop?"
> "I believe you, most are bad, because they're generic bots with no idea what a presale even is. That's why I'm specialist-only and concierge-first. A human approves everything in the pilot, and it only handles the presale wait, the one place a generic bot is most wrong. Judge this by the free pilot on your real tickets, not the bad bot."

*Structure: Miner clarify-first, then Braun's "name the real worry out loud" defuse (the case-study clarify splits "result proof" from "won't-disappear proof," exactly Braun's move). Parents: [Miner clarify](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), [Braun send-me-an-email defuse](https://joshbraun.com/send-me-an-email/). **Artifact-first upgrade + PROOF-ONLY:** the track-record honesty ("no finished case study yet") is led with strength reversed into the reason for the free founding pilot, never a "smaller shop" apology. The $2M figure is warm-only. Every answer ends pointing at the free pilot on their own tickets as the artifact-evidence.*

### Quick-fire

- *"Too good to be free."* → "It's free because I need the proof more than your money right now. You're doing me the favor."
- *"We're too small."* → "1,000+ in the wait window means you're exactly the volume I can measure on."
- *"We're too big / too custom."* → "Then I learn the most from you, and you get a specialist hand on your hardest cohort. Either way I plug into what you run."
- *"Email me, I'll look later."* → "Will do, one page, so 'later' is two minutes, not homework."

*Structure: Braun's low-friction one-liner bumps (cost the prospect nothing, flip the frame). Parent: [Braun follow-up bumps](https://joshbraun.com/how-to-write-follow-up-emails/). "Email me, I'll look later" mirrors Braun's "one page so later is two minutes" reframe.*

# PART 4 — THE PAID-CONVERSION ASK

> **Hard gate:** never make a paid ask without a documented leading-indicator win AND an experience testimonial. Missing either, don't ask. Extend the concierge and ask the week after the win lands.
>
> **Pricing:** land low, expand on proof. Founding-pilot intro = $199 to $299/mo at conversion; productized Starter = $299 to $499/mo. The $799+ proactive tier and any performance fee are earned later, only once a real refund case study exists. Never lead with a performance fee in the 90-day window. Attribution isn't provable yet.
>
> *Structure: Hormozi/Gordon "price after desire" close (reveal the number only after they've verbalized the outcome's worth) + Nelson's assumptive next-action close ("I'll send the link, you're partner #N"). Parents: Hormozi CLOSER price-late move and Cole Gordon's commit-phase, both via outreach-proof.md Tactic 8; [Josh Nelson assumptive close](https://sevenfigureagency.com/master-cold-outreach/). **Artifact-first upgrade:** the ask opens with the before-and-after on THEIR tickets shared on screen (the artifact they already lived), then the number, never a number into a vacuum.*

## 4A — Standard ask (1 documented win + testimonial in hand)

> "We've been at this [N] weeks. Let me show you the before-and-after, then ask you something straight.
>
> [SHARE SCREEN, the leading-indicator table.] When we started, your first-response time on presale tickets was [X], and where-is-my-order was [Y] per hundred orders. Now first response is [X2], a [Z]% cut, and the repeat questions are down to [Y2] because we handle the routine stuff before it hits you. And you told me the buyers feel '[their words].' That's measured, on your tickets.
>
> The honest part: the full refund number still needs your cohort to finish the wait, so I'm not pretending I've proven that. But everything that drives refunds down is moving the right way.
>
> So here's the ask. I've taken on [1-2] founding partners and I can't run concierge by hand forever, that's why I'm productizing it. I'd love to keep going with you as a paying founding partner: the founding intro is $199 to $299/month, and the productized Starter is $299 to $499/month. Either one locks that price even after I raise it once the case studies land. You keep everything we built, I keep improving it on your account, and you're first in line for the proactive day-7/30/60 outreach when it ships. Want to lock it in?"

**If yes** → "I'll send a payment link today, [tier]. Nothing changes operationally. You're partner #[N]."

**If "let me think about price"** → 4C, then: *"The founding rate's the thing I'd hate for you to miss, so I'll hold it [7 days]. Same service runs in the meantime."*

*Structure: Gordon "get the yes on the logic before the number" + the screen-shared measured win as the value anchor, closing on Nelson's assumptive action ("I'll send the link, you're partner #N"). Parents: Cole Gordon commit-phase and Hormozi price-late (outreach-proof.md Tactic 8), [Nelson assumptive close](https://sevenfigureagency.com/master-cold-outreach/). PROOF-ONLY: refund number explicitly NOT claimed, only the measured leading indicators on their own tickets.*

## 4B — Soft ask (win exists, they want to wait for refund proof)

> "Fair to want the refund number before you commit big, I'd want it too. So let's not do big. The leading indicators are real and you've felt the difference. I'd suggest the starter founding rate, $199/month, basically 'keep what's working while we both wait for the cohort to complete.' If the refund delta comes in strong, you're already in at the founding price before I move everyone up. If it doesn't, you've spent a fraction of what one prevented chargeback covers. Want to do that?"

*Structure: Hormozi/Gordon belief-gap close (meet the real hesitation, lower the commitment, keep the price logic intact). Parent: outreach-proof.md Tactic 8 price-after-desire consensus. The "fraction of one prevented chargeback" is a framing of their own economics, not a claimed Tideover result.*

## 4C — Price objections (paid stage)

**"$X/month is more than I wanted to spend."**
> *[Clarify first, a price objection is usually a belief gap:]* "When you say more than you wanted, is it more than the result's worth to you, or more than you've got allocated this month? Different answers."
> "Let's anchor it. One chargeback runs you the fee plus the lost product plus the Stripe-account risk. The founding intro is $199 to $299, and the productized Starter is $299 to $499. Either is less than a fraction of a part-time VA. And it's the lowest this price will ever be. You're not paying for a tool, you're keeping a result you can already see."

**"Can we do a lower price?"**
> "The founding rate is the lower price, already well under where this lands once the refund case studies exist. What I can do is start you at $199 instead of the full tier, so the number's smaller and you scale as you see more. I won't go under founding, though. Partly principle, partly because cheap-and-desperate isn't the brand."

**"Let me wait until you have the refund case study."**
> "You can, but here's the trade. The case study comes from partners like you running the full cycle. Wait on the sidelines and you lose the founding rate, and you're not in the data, so when the refund number lands it's somebody else's brand in the story. Staying in at $199 keeps the result and the price and puts your cohort in the proof. The wait costs you more than the $199."

**"We need to see ROI first."**
> "Which is why the pilot was free. You've already seen the leading-indicator ROI on your own tickets: [X]% faster response, [Y]% fewer repeat tickets. The paid step keeps that running and adds the proactive outreach. You're buying continuation of something measured, not faith."

*Structure: Miner price-clarify ("how do you mean, too expensive?") into Hormozi's anchor-to-their-P&L (one chargeback's true cost), never discount below founding. Parents: [Miner price-objection clarify](https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/), Hormozi/Gordon anchor-and-never-discount (outreach-proof.md Tactic 8). PROOF-ONLY: the [X]%/[Y]% are placeholders for the partner's own measured deltas, never invented.*

# PART 5 — RESULTS-REVIEW SCRIPTS

> **Purpose:** the recurring touch where you show what's working, capture their reaction (testimonial seed), and at the milestone version set up the paid ask. Only show what's measured. Cite any external benchmark to its source. Never present an estimate as a result.
>
> *Structure: Braun's "every follow-up is a deposit, never a bare update" review cadence (lead with new data/value, then a light ask), with the milestone version using Hormozi's price-after-the-win timing. Parents: [Braun follow-ups that replace "just checking in"](https://joshbraun.com/how-to-write-follow-up-emails/), Hormozi price-late (outreach-proof.md Tactic 8). **Artifact-first note:** every review IS the artifact in motion, the measured before/after on their own tickets shown on screen.*

## 5A — Weekly review (15 min, every week during the pilot)

> "Quick weekly check-in. Two minutes of data, then mostly I want your read. First-response time on presale tickets: [X] to [X2]. Where-is-my-order volume: [Y] per hundred, [up/down] from [Y_prev]. Deflection, the routine stuff handled without you touching it, is at [Z]%. I logged [N] saves this week, moments where someone was heading toward a dispute and the reply turned them around. Here are two. [show ticket links]"

**Qualitative capture (where testimonials are born):**
1. *"Does the inbox feel different this week? Lighter, same, worse?"*
2. *"Heard anything from a buyer, or seen a review, that felt different?"*
3. *"Anything I'm getting wrong on your voice or timeline? Be picky."*
4. *"If you had to tell another founder what changed in one sentence, what would you say?"* ← **write this verbatim, it's your future testimonial.**

**The honest caveat (every week, it builds the trust that closes the sale):**
> "Reminder on what these are. Leading indicators, real and measured, and what drives refunds down. The actual refund number still needs your cohort to finish. I'll show you that the second it's real, whatever it says."

**Close:**
> "Good week. One small ask. That sentence about the inbox feeling lighter, mind if I quote you? Just the experience, no numbers you haven't seen. It helps me bring on the next partner honestly."

*Structure: Braun's value-bump follow-up (data hook first: "we analyzed 937 cold calls, want to see?"), here the data is their own pilot numbers; testimonial ask modeled on Mahrle's "feedback from someone like you" low-cost ask. Parents: [Braun data-hook bump](https://joshbraun.com/how-to-write-follow-up-emails/), [Mahrle feedback ask](https://www.getmagical.com/templates/jed-mahrles-top-7-email-templates). PROOF-ONLY: refund caveat stated every week; testimonial is the experience only, no numbers they haven't seen.*

## 5B — Milestone review (win documented → sets up the paid ask)

> "This one's different. I think we hit the thing I was hoping to prove, and I want to walk you through it properly, because it changes what I can offer you.
>
> Here's the clean before-and-after, baseline and method on the side so you can check my work, no cherry-picking. [Walk the FRT cut as primary, WISMO-per-100 and deflection as supporting.] A real, baselined improvement on your tickets over equal windows. This is the win I came here to earn, and you're the brand it happened on.
>
> The refund case study still needs the cohort to finish, and I'll publish whatever it really is. Given a measured win and you've felt the difference, I'd love to talk about keeping this going as a founding partner." → Part 4A.

**Testimonial capture (formal, on the win):**
> "Would you do a short note, just what you genuinely observed? The responsiveness, the lighter inbox, the calmer buyers. Only what you can stand behind. That honest version is worth more to me than any inflated quote."

*Structure: Hormozi price-after-desire timing (reveal the offer only once the win is verbalized) + Braun's genuine-resource bump shape (lead with the proof, then the small ask). Parents: Hormozi price-late (outreach-proof.md Tactic 8), [Braun bumps](https://joshbraun.com/how-to-write-follow-up-emails/). ## APPENDIX — VOICE & DON'T-LIST (pin this on every call)

**The voice:** operator-to-operator. Calm, specific, a little dry. You've sat in the inbox and they can hear it. You screen as hard as you sell. You'd rather lose the deal than fake the number.

**Don't:**
- ❌ Lead with "AI." Lead with the wound.
- ❌ Quote a refund-reduction %. It doesn't exist. Say so, out loud, every time.
- ❌ Name "$2M" in cold outreach. War story first, the number only in warm, substantiable conversation.
- ❌ Invent case studies, testimonials, ratings, logos, or "trusted by." (The library's weak/self-reported numbers are the operators' own, never restated as ours.)
- ❌ Restate an industry stat as Tideover's own result. Cite the source or don't say it.
- ❌ Make a paid ask without a documented win and an experience testimonial in hand.
- ❌ Oversell capacity. It's 1 to 5 partners, the scarcity is real.
- ❌ Pitch a performance fee in the 90-day window. Deferred until the case study exists.
- ❌ Let AI write the per-prospect hook or follow-up line. A tool pulls the raw signal, a human writes the line. AI/token personalization is now worse than none (outreach-proof.md, Tactic 3).
- ❌ Push an artifact unasked. Offer the Playbook/teardown, then send on the yes (permission-gate, outreach-proof.md Tactics 5 + 10).
- ❌ Answer an objection before you clarify it. One-line clarify first, then the rebuttal (outreach-proof.md, Tactic 9, Miner/Nelson).
- ❌ Open with the "I'm newer / no track record / smaller shop" admission. Lead with the hook and the made-for-them artifact; the honesty lands late, as the reason for the free sample.
- ❌ Use a dead opener or cliche tell: "hope this finds you," "quick question," "just following up / circling back / bumping," or a bare "Not a pitch." sign-off. One earned reassurance per message, max.
- ❌ Drift a follow-up cadence past 5 touches or into a few-day burn. Hold 3 to 5 over ~20 days (outreach-proof.md, Tactic 4).

**The artifact-first upgrade in one line:** *Where every real operator opens with a claim ("I have some ideas," "I help businesses fix that," "I've got a few ideas to close that gap"), we open with the finished made-for-them thing, the free teardown on their own tickets or the day-stage script already written. The artifact replaces the claim, and the no-track-record honesty becomes the reason for the free sample, never the opener.*

**The brand in one line:** *Be the one honest, competent person in a presale founder's inbox, and prove it by hand before you ever charge for it.*
