The long wait

Your customers paid. Now they wait 90 days. That gap is where trust quietly leaks out.

A presale order isn't a one-off ticket — it's a months-long emotional journey. The same buyer who was thrilled at checkout becomes anxious, then suspicious, then a refund request. Here's how the wait usually plays out.

Day 7
Excited, eager

"Can't wait!" They check the order page, re-read the campaign, and tell friends. Easy to delight — if anyone answers.

Day 30
First doubt

The thrill fades. "Any update on shipping?" A generic helpdesk gives a standard shipping answer — for a product that won't ship for two more months.

Day 60
Anxious, impatient

Silence reads as a red flag. WISMO ("where is my order?") tickets pile up. One frustrating reply and they're Googling "refund."

"It doesn't understand pre-orders — it gives standard shipping answers."
Day 89
Ready to bail

No reassurance, no answer. Now it's a chargeback, a 1-star "scam" review, and a customer you'll never win back.

Chargebacks threaten your standing

Refunds and disputes don't just cost the sale — they put your Stripe and PayPal accounts at risk.

WISMO floods drown your inbox

The same repetitive "where is my order?" question, hundreds of times, on three different timelines at once.

"Scam" reviews stick around

An anxious customer left in the dark writes the review — and it shapes how the next backer sees you.

The things founders actually ask

We already have a helpdesk — do I have to switch or install something new?

No, and that's the whole point. Tideover is a bolt-on, not a rip-and-replace. We route only your presale and preorder tickets to us — every other ticket stays in the Gorgias, Tidio, or Intercom you already use, handled exactly as it is today.

There's zero infrastructure change: no migration, no second inbox for your team to learn, nothing to rip out. We work alongside your current helpdesk and add the presale layer it never had — and change nothing else.

How is this different from the AI bot already in my helpdesk?

Generic helpdesks treat a presale order like any other order — so they give a standard shipping answer to a product that won't ship for two months. Tideover knows which orders are preorders, knows each one's real production timeline, and knows what to say at day 7 versus day 60 versus day 89. The reassurance is the whole product.

Will you make delivery promises you can't keep?

Never. A false ship date is the single most damaging thing we could say to an anxious customer. We use honest confidence bands ("currently tracking for [month]," "ETA band X–Y"), never invented exact dates — and every novel reply is approved by a human before it sends. We never auto-fire a promise.

What does the free pilot actually involve?

For one presale cycle, we run your presale support by hand inside your helpdesk. We capture a clean baseline, draft timeline-aware replies in two fixed daily windows (you'll know the cadence up front), build your day-stage script library, and log every "save." You see exactly how it works before there's ever a paid plan on the table.

What can you actually prove, and what can't you yet?

Honestly: in a single cycle we can prove leading indicators — faster first-response time, fewer repetitive WISMO tickets, and logged saves — all measured against your own baseline. A full refund-reduction case study needs a complete 60–120 day presale cycle to finish, so it lands after your first cohort completes. We tell every partner that plainly, and we'd rather under-promise than fabricate a number.
