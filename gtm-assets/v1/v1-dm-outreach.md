## 0. Merge Fields & Pre-Send Gate

These get hand-built per lead. Auto-generate them and you've built a tidy slop machine.

| Field | Rule |
|---|---|
| `{{firstName}}` | The founder or decision-maker, not a generic handle. Missing → suppress. Never "there" or "hey." |
| `{{product}}` | Lowercase, mid-sentence, natural: "your focus mushroom chocolates," never the scraped title "Brainstorm Focus Bites 30ct." A raw title pull is the slop. |
| `{{sampleLink}}` | Hosted MP4 of their First Strike, the rendered ad of THEIR product (`26-prospect-sample-scripts.md` → render → host). Rendered and ready before the first touch; sent only after they say yes. Blank → not ready, don't send. |
| `{{hook}}` | One true, specific observation about their account. The over-run ad, a review theme, the angle carrying everything. **A human writes this line. Always.** A tool pulls the raw signal; you write the hook. |

> **NON-NEGOTIABLE — never let AI write `{{hook}}`.** Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 3 (8 sources): AI/token personalization is now *worse than none*. The rule is absolute: **a tool pulls the raw signal (Meta Ad Library, reviews, their site); a human writes the hook.** Letting AI write the variable line is the single thing that would break the whole system. No exceptions, no batch generation, no "AI-assisted draft." *(The real parents this protects: every library opener earns the read with one hand-found specific — Welsh's funding round, Estefa's "audience of 1,500," Prospeo's "2x the review volume." A tool can find the signal; only a human writes the line that says the quiet part.)*

**The hook bar, all three or kill the send:** (1) **true**, verifiable from their public account; (2) **specific**, names the actual ad or angle or review, not "you're scaling on Meta"; (3) **says the quiet part**, something they half-suspected and didn't want to say out loud.

**Pre-send gate (every box, or don't send):**
- [ ] **Profile converts (hard pre-launch gate, not a step).** Before ANY priority DM ships, the profile + featured section carry one offer and visible proof, verified live by clicking it yourself. The DM only buys a profile click; the profile does the closing. See §6.3. *(Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 6 / Divergence 2, and Daniel Fazio's core rule — "the cold DM is only a traffic mechanism; the magic happens when prospects click your profile, see your offer, and act from there" — `real-scripts-library.md` Tactic 6 source: https://www.linkedin.com/in/coldemailwizard/.)*
- [ ] First Strike rendered, clears the production bar, hosted, link resolves. Ready to send on the "yes."
- [ ] `{{hook}}` is true, specific, says the quiet part, and **a human wrote it**.
- [ ] `{{product}}` reads natural lowercase mid-sentence.
- [ ] Archetype routed right (§7). No Wounded-Veteran grief sent to a winning Volume-Player.
- [ ] No fabricated number, no claimed case study, logo, testimonial, or track record. No library operator's number restated as ours.

## 1. Connection Notes (LinkedIn)

300-char cap. **The rule: no note, OR one ultra-specific note under 180 characters.** *(Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 6 / Divergence 5: 2026 LinkedIn data — naked requests accept at 55–68% vs 28–45% with a generic note; the one exception is a hyper-specific sub-180-char note. A generic note hurts; a sharp specific one can help. Never a flat pitch.)*

> **Structure: Sam McKenna "Show Me You Know Me" — https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samsalesli_smykm-samsales-activity-7082345704769122304-zjgr** — the one exception note follows McKenna's confirmed design: open with a specific, real, them-first observation that "only makes sense to one person," no calendar link, no ask in the first touch. The naked-default follows the 2026 LinkedIn data (Tactic 6) and Jack Reamer's post-connection model where the request itself carries nothing and the warm line comes after accept (https://salesbread.com/cold-linkedin-message-templates/).
> **Artifact-first upgrade:** where McKenna's note humanizes ("I see Roy pop up in your posts all the time") and Reamer's softens ("favorite hiking spots"), our exception note names the made-for-them artifact already built. The specific observation is still them-first; we just point it at the finished ad instead of a hobby.

- **Default: no note.** Highest accept rate. Send the request naked, then permission-ask the sample in the opener after they accept (§2).
- **Exception: one ultra-specific note, under 180 chars,** only when the hook is sharp enough to prove you looked at *them*. No link (links tank accept rate and trip filters). No pitch. The note never asks for anything.

On X you skip this entirely, open DMs or reply-first, then §5 cadence.

If you use a note, pick by what's verifiable. Rotate so no two are identical across a batch. Each is under 180 characters.

**CN1 — Hook-specific (best; use when the hook is short and sharp):**
```
{{firstName}} — {{hook}}. Made a 15-sec ad of {{product}} off your
own reviews to show what I mean. Connect and I'll ask before sending.
```

**CN2 — Vertical-specialist (kills "generic agency"):**
```
{{firstName}} — I only work with functional-consumables brands.
Built a 15-sec ad of {{product}} before reaching out.
```

**CN3 — Shortest (best deliverability):**
```
{{firstName}} — made a 15-sec ad of {{product}} after looking at
your account.
```

**Rules:** no link, no "AI," no price, no "free," no calendar ask. Each under 180 characters. Missing `{{firstName}}` → no note (or suppress). Send in their business hours. Don't message until they accept. When in doubt, no note beats a weak note.

## 2. Openers

**The opener's job:** one true hook, then **ask permission to send the First Strike**. Deliver the link on the yes. One hook, one ask.

> **Structure: Justin Welsh passive cold DM — https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/how-to-find-more-customers-on-linkedin** — Welsh's touch-1 leads with one specific real observation, adds a soft credential, and asks for nothing. We keep his "observation, no pitch, low-pressure" shape and graft on the one ask the artifact earns. The mechanism (4 angles × 24 variations) is held back the way Welsh holds back his offer.
> **Artifact-first upgrade:** Welsh leads with acknowledgment ("Congrats on the recent round... love what you're doing"); Josh Nelson and Estefa lead with a *claim* of results. We lead with the **finished First Strike already built** and permission-ask it. Where they say "I have some ideas," we say "I already made the ad — want me to send it?" The artifact replaces the claim. *(Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 10 + the library's "What this proves" note: this is the stronger version of their move-2, and none of them do it.)*

### 2.0 Permission-ask vs embedded-link (the A/B)

*Per `outreach-proof.md` Divergence 1 + Tactics 5/10. The **permission-ask is the favored default** — every documented Loom/sample case (the 150-brand campaign, the 4%→22% Loom split) converts higher asking "want me to send it?" first. Keep an embedded-link variant ONLY as an explicit A/B, never the assumed winner.*

- **Variant P (permission-ask — DEFAULT):** hook → "want me to send it?" → link on the yes. Use this unless an A/B is actively running.
- **Variant L (embedded-link — A/B only):** hook → link in the same message. The show-immediately proof-first thesis. Run only as a measured split against P; the proof leans toward P.

The openers below are written as **Variant P (permission-ask)**. The embedded-link wording (Variant L) is noted inline where it differs, for the A/B only.

## 2A. Openers — Wounded Veteran (Archetype A)

**Route here when:** 5–10yr operator gutted by the Feb-2025 Andromeda update. Winners died for no reason. Tried every targeting fix. Burned by a slow agency.

**Voice:** recognize the wound, then pivot fast to the production-hands lever they couldn't pull alone. Don't marinate in grief. Never say "volume is the answer." Reference Andromeda as shared context, never sold back as a hook. One hook, one ask.

> **Structure: Justin Welsh observation-first DM (https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/how-to-find-more-customers-on-linkedin) + Nick Abraham's risk-minimizer move (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham_coldemail-prospecting-b2bsales-activity-7119728772051832833-dVJm).** Welsh gives the shape: specific observation, no pitch, soft ask. Abraham's load-bearing `{risk minimizer}` slot ("on a performance basis") is the thing that de-risks the ask — we fill that slot with the already-built artifact instead of a promise. Sam McKenna SMYKM element 6 (pre-empt the objection in the message) lets us name the agency scar before they raise it.
> **Artifact-first upgrade:** Abraham's risk-minimizer is still a *conditional claim* ("If I could generate X"). Ours is unconditional and already done ("So I rebuilt that angle... it's already done"). The render is the risk-minimizer.

First message after accept (LinkedIn) or first DM (X). Pick one, rotate across the batch.

## 3. Follow-Up Ladders (opener + 3 follow-ups, then stop)

DMs run shorter than the email rig: opener plus 3 follow-ups over a ~20-day window, then stop. Reply in-thread. One idea per message. If they reply at any point, drop the ladder and go to §5. Never send a second breakup.

> **Structure of the ladder: Daniel Fazio 3-email sequence (https://medium.com/@danielfazio/the-best-b2b-cold-email-template-ive-ever-used-b28166cf709e) for the spacing and escalation, and Sam Nelson's Agoge sequence (https://blog.contactout.io/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/30MPC-x-Sam-Nelsons-Agoge-Sequence.pdf) for the value-then-breakup arc.** Fazio's three emails escalate: ask → "want to see proof?" → "here's the value even if you don't work with us." Our F1/F2/F3 run the same escalation in a DM thread.
> **The first follow-up is the single most valuable message after the opener** *(per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 4: follow-ups carry ~44% of replies, FU1 alone ~26%)*. So F1 is **not** a "did you see this" bump — it carries the most value-dense content in the ladder.

## 4. Reply-Handlers — the 6 common responses

The moment they reply, stop the ladder. Match to a bucket, respond in-thread. **Clarify the real objection before you answer it**, then concede what's true, then land the distinction. Never defensive, defensiveness reads as hype to a tired buyer. Goal of every handler: book the 15-min call (`18-sales-playbook.md`), where spend decides retainer-vs-pack. Never quote a retainer in DM.

> **Structure of the whole handler set: Marcos Ruiz's $3M DM flow — ask, dig, dig, soft ask (https://www.thebirdhouse.email/p/steal-this-3m-linkedin-dm-script).** Every handler opens with Ruiz's clarifying question ("I'm curious — are you looking to X, or mostly just following along?" → "what do you mean specifically by..."), then lands the distinction, then the soft ask. Never a raw calendar link cold.
> **The clarify-first line: Jeremy Miner / Prospeo (https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/ ; https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script) — "When you say [X], what specifically do you mean?"** *(Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 9 / Divergence 4: never answer an objection before you question it.)* Each handler leads with a one-line clarify so you answer the objection they actually have.

### 4.1 INTERESTED ("nice", "tell me more", "how does it work")

Easiest and most fragile. Don't pitch into the open door. Book the call before momentum cools.
> **Structure: Marcos Ruiz dig-then-soft-ask (https://www.thebirdhouse.email/p/steal-this-3m-linkedin-dm-script).** Ruiz's exact move on a warm reply: qualify before you set up the call ("I can set up a call... who can break down exactly how this would work for you"). We dig on scope first, then offer the call.
```
Appreciate that, {{firstName}}. So I point at the right thing: are
you weighing this for a single SKU, or the whole account?

Either way: that First Strike is one variation of one angle-family.
The way it runs is the full package: 4 angles, 24 variations every
two weeks, launched and tagged in your account, read at day 14,
losers killed, winners doubled.

Fastest way to see if it fits your spend and SKU mix is 15 min, I
diagnose live on your account. [FILL: booking link]. Or drop your
rough monthly Meta + TikTok spend here and I'll tell you straight if
you're a fit.
```

### 4.2 NOT NOW ("not the right time", "circle back Q3", "swamped")

Clarify what "not now" is really about, concede, leave the door open, give a real reason to return. Never push.
> **Structure: Jeremy Miner / Prospeo "think about it" clarify (https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script) + Josh Braun "not interested / not right now" — drop the ask, leave one useful thing (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_josh-a-prospect-responded-to-my-cold-email-activity-7074138982707556352-T9Cm).** Clarify the real "not now" (bandwidth vs budget), concede, leave the offer standing, give a real reason to return without pushing.
```
Fair, {{firstName}}. Is it bandwidth right now, or budget timing?
Changes what I'd send you.

Either way the First Strike offer stays open for whenever the timing
turns. One flag: the only thing with a clock is the founding slots, 2
of 5 left, a full cycle at no cost in exchange for publishing the
before/after. Want me to check back in [FILL: ~3–4 weeks], or leave
it?
```

### 4.3 WHO ARE YOU ("never heard of you", "what's your background", "got case studies")

Clarify what proof they actually want, then the proof-only moment. Don't apologize, don't headline the home-gym business, don't fabricate. Newness is the reason the sample and guarantee exist.
> **Structure: Jed Mahrle "ask for feedback / honest frame" DM (https://www.getmagical.com/templates/jed-mahrles-top-7-email-templates) + Nick Abraham risk-minimizer (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham_coldemail-prospecting-b2bsales-activity-7119728772051832833-dVJm).** Mahrle's whole move is disarming honesty about who you are; we use it as the honest "I'm newer" admission — placed here, late, on demand, never in the opener. Abraham's risk-minimizer becomes the guarantee that puts the risk on us.
> **Artifact-first + LEAD-WITH-STRENGTH:** the honesty ("newer than the 10-year agencies") is the *reason* the free First Strike and guarantee exist, not a confession that leads. The proof shown is real work only — their First Strike, real teardowns, the guarantee. No fabricated case study, ever.
```
Fair question. What would settle it for you, seeing more work, or
knowing how I de-risk it if I'm wrong?

Straight answer either way: I'm newer to AI ad creative than the
10-year agencies, and I won't fake a case study, because faking
exactly that is what clogs your inbox. So I prove it honest ways.

You'd see real work, your First Strike, before I ask for anything. I
publish teardowns of real functional-consumables ads: the hook, where
hold-rate decays, which claim is load-bearing and whether it's
FTC-safe. Here's one: [FILL: teardown link]. And the guarantee puts
the risk on me: beat your control on hold-rate with CPA flat in 30
days, in your own account, or the next cycle's free.

15 min and I'll walk you through it: [FILL: booking link].
```

### 4.4 PRICE ("how much", "what are your rates")

Clarify what they're sizing against, then anchor on the $497 pack publicly. Never quote a retainer in DM. Move to the call to size it.
> **Structure: Prospeo price objection — "depends on what we'd build... let's do 15 min so I can give you a real number" (https://prospeo.io/s/smma-sales-script) + Jeremy Miner clarify-first (https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/).** Clarify what they're comparing against, anchor on the one public number ($497 pack), defer the retainer to the call where it can be sized — never quoted blind. *(Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 8: price after desire, never into a vacuum.)*
```
Happy to. So the number's useful: are you comparing this to an
agency retainer, or to per-spot UGC?

Public price is the starter pack: $497, one-time, 10 videos. About
$50 a video, against $200–500 for one comparable UGC spot. Lowest-risk
way to watch the work land in your own account before we talk retainer.

Retainers I quote live, scaled to your spend and SKU mix, because a
rate card would be guessing. 15 min and I'll size it: [FILL: booking
link]. What's your rough monthly spend?
```

### 4.5 SEND MORE ("got more samples", "show me the portfolio")

Strong buying signal. Deliver immediately, then convert to a call. Real assets only.
> **Structure: Daniel Fazio email 2 — "would you like to see a case study / more work?" delivered on the ask (https://medium.com/@danielfazio/the-best-b2b-cold-email-template-ive-ever-used-b28166cf709e) + Marcos Ruiz qualify-then-soft-ask (https://www.thebirdhouse.email/p/steal-this-3m-linkedin-dm-script).** Fazio's shape is "you asked for proof, here it is"; we deliver real assets immediately, dig on what to lead with, then convert to the call.
> **Artifact-first upgrade:** Fazio offers a case study; we have none, so what we "send more" is real work that exists — the angle reel and real teardowns — never a fabricated result. The First Strike is named as angle 1 of 4.
```
Yeah. Anything specific you want it to handle, a tricky SKU or a
consumption shot? Tells me what to lead with.

Here's the angle reel and a couple of teardowns of real
functional-consumables ads so you see the judgment, not just the
output: [FILL: reel link] · [FILL: teardown link].

On {{brand}}, the {{product}} First Strike is angle 1 of 4. I'd test
[FILL: 1-line angle 2 idea] and [FILL: angle 3 idea] against your
control. Fastest way to see the full package mapped to your account
is 15 min: [FILL: booking link].
```

### 4.6 SKEPTICAL ("AI looks fake", "we tried AI, it was bad", "won't work for my audience")

**Clarify first** — pin down what specifically read as fake before you answer. Then concede the true part (AI alone is slop), then point at the thing they can watch. The sample is the rebuttal. Never repeat the word "slop" back at them.
> **Structure: Jeremy Miner Clarify → Discuss → Diffuse (https://7thlevelhq.com/the-top-52-objections/) + Sam McKenna element 6 pre-empt (https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samsalesli_smykm-samsales-activity-7082345704769122304-zjgr).** Miner's signature: never answer the objection, question it first ("when you say it looked bad, what specifically read as fake?"). Then concede the true part, then point at the artifact as the rebuttal, and pre-empt the next objection (where AI genuinely breaks) before they raise it.
> **Artifact-first upgrade:** the rebuttal isn't an argument, it's the render built from their reviews and product. The sample is the proof; the operator judgment is the thing being sold.
```
Fair. When you say the AI looked bad, what specifically read as fake
to you? The face, the voice sync, the product in-hand, or the whole
thing felt generic?

Because most of it is bad, and usually for one reason: AI with no
brand context renders generic mush. The render I'd send works because
it's built from your reviews and your actual product, with an operator
picking which of 4 angles fits your AOV. The AI is the hands. The
judgment is what you're paying for.

I'll also tell you where AI genuinely breaks: the drink-and-react
consumption shot. So I route around it, hands-and-pour over
face-on-sip. In functional consumables the product is the star and
the avatar just holds it, so the uncanny-valley tax stays low. 15 min
and I'll show you the QC bar every variation clears: [FILL: booking
link].
```

> **Bonus — "just send me a proposal / pricing PDF":** clarify first, then redirect, per Josh Braun's "send me an email" defuse (https://joshbraun.com/send-me-an-email/) — name the polite brush-off honestly, then offer the higher-value alternative. *"Happy to. What would you want it to cover, scope or price? I ask because a blind PDF would guess at your spend and SKU mix, and you'd get exactly the generic agency experience you're avoiding. Give me 15 min, I'll size it to your numbers live, and I'll send a one-pager same day if you still want it. [FILL: booking link]"*

## 5. Cadence

### 5.1 Per-lead sequence (3–5 touches over a ~20-day window)

*Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 4 / Divergence 6: hold at 3–5 touches over ~20 days. Never drift to 8–12 touches or a compressed few-day burn. The first follow-up is the most valuable message — F1 is the value-dense one (§3), not a bump.*

> **Structure: Daniel Fazio 3-email spacing (Day 0 / +3 / +3 — https://medium.com/@danielfazio/the-best-b2b-cold-email-template-ive-ever-used-b28166cf709e) widened to the proven ~20-day window, and the Sam Nelson Agoge arc (opener → value touches → breakup, reply on-thread — https://blog.contactout.io/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/30MPC-x-Sam-Nelsons-Agoge-Sequence.pdf).** Fazio proves the escalating 3-touch shape; the spacing is stretched to the dataset-optimal 20 days from `outreach-proof.md`.

**LinkedIn (connection request first):**

| Step | Day | Message | Advance when |
|---|---|---|---|
| Connection request | Day 0 | §1: no note, or one <180-char ultra-specific note | Accepted |
| Opener | Day of accept | §2 permission-ask opener (Variant P default) | No reply in 3 days |
| Follow-up 1 (value-dense) | +3 days | F1 — the highest-leverage message, re-offer + why-the-sample | No reply in 4 days |
| Follow-up 2 (value) | +7 days | F2 — the mechanism / standing arrangement | No reply in 6 days |
| Follow-up 3 (close) | +9 days | F3 — leave the door open. No second breakup. | Stop. |

Total window ≈ 19–20 days from accept. On a slow accept, the clock starts at the opener.

**X (open DMs or reply-first, no connection step):** opener Day 0, F1 Day 3, F2 Day 10, F3 Day 19. Same ~20-day window, same value-dense F1.

> If their X DMs are closed: reply genuinely to 1–2 of their posts first (teardown-grade insight, not a pitch) to open the DM, or fall back to LinkedIn. Replies out-distribute everything at low follower counts. *(The reply-first-to-open-the-DM move follows the library's Nemo/X-engagement note and `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 6 source 6.)*

**Rules:** one message per step, one idea, one ask. Any reply → drop the ladder → §4. Stop after Follow-up 3, never an 8–12-touch sequence, never a 3-day burn. Send in the prospect's business hours, M–F. No weekends, no late nights. Openers go out in small daily batches so every hook and sample stays hand-built.

### 5.2 Daily volume (sized for first cash)

First cash rides DMs to the top ~10 A-tier leads. Small-n, high-quality, not a spray. The binding constraint is sample-production time (~15–30 min each), not DM throughput.

> **Structure: Sam McKenna's depth-over-volume rule — "spend 80% of your time on 20% of your prospects, start at ~20 messages/week not hundreds"** *(per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 6, matched to our deal size).* The volume table below is McKenna's depth model, not Clifton Sellers' 100-DMs/day volume model, because the ticket size and the hand-built artifact demand depth.

| Window | Daily motion | Why |
|---|---|---|
| First-cash sprint (top 10) | 2–4 permission-ask touches/day until all 10 sent, replies same-day | Only ~10 touches to start landing $497 packs |
| Widen (A-tier 11–24) | 3–5/day once ≥3 of top-10 reply positively | Email rig online in parallel for the full list |
| LinkedIn safety ceiling | ≤20–25 connection requests/day, ramped from ~10–15, never spiked | Aggressive volume gets accounts restricted |
| Reply block | 10 min/day replying to ICP operators' posts with real insight | Warms profiles, opens DMs, compounds distribution |

**Checkpoint (DM-ignition + ~4 days):** ≥2 of top-10 replied positively AND ≥1 call booked. If red, the First Strike or hook is the problem, not volume. Loop back to the §0 bar and hand-rebuild the hook. Do not answer a cold patch with more sends. Fix the input.

### 5.3 Profile-as-funnel (HARD pre-launch gate — verify live before ANY priority DM)

*Per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 6 / Divergence 2: every credible DM source (Welsh, McKenna, Fazio, Sellers) says the DM only buys a profile click and the profile does the closing. This is a gate, not a warming step. Outbound does not go live until all three are verified by clicking your own profile as a prospect would.*

> **Structure: Daniel Fazio's profile-is-the-traffic-mechanism rule (https://www.linkedin.com/in/coldemailwizard/) + Justin Welsh's soft-credential-in-bio model (https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/how-to-find-more-customers-on-linkedin).** Fazio: the DM only buys the click, the profile closes. Welsh: the credential lives on the profile, not in the first touch.
> **Proof-only:** the bio carries one offer and the *free* First Strike, no fake metrics, no borrowed follower counts. The library's numbers stay with their authors — Welsh's pipeline math, Fazio's send counts, none get pinned to an Adwield profile.

- [ ] **Bio carries one offer, proof-only:** "Adwield — AI ad creative for functional-consumables brands. I build your First Strike free, on your own product, before you pay. Fresh winners every two weeks. Capped at 5 brands." No fake metrics, no borrowed follower counts.
- [ ] **Featured / pinned = visible proof:** a teardown or a clean render pinned, so "show me examples" is answered the second they click.
- [ ] **Engage 1–2 of the lead's recent posts** with genuine insight in the days before the request, where natural.

Profile fails any line → fix the profile before a single priority DM ships. The DM is traffic; the profile is the close.

## 6. Archetype-Routing Quick Reference

| Signal | Archetype | Lead with |
|---|---|---|
| Gutted by Feb-'25 Andromeda; "winners died for no reason"; tried every targeting fix; agency-burned; 5–10yr operator | **Wounded Veteran (§2A)** | Recognition, then pivot to the production-hands gap. Never "volume is the answer." |
| Winning, wants leverage; ships 90+/wk; can't produce the variation | **Volume-Player (§2B)** | Capacity math, launched-and-tagged. Zero grief. |
| 2–3 person team juggling agencies, wants to hand off not babysit (the ideal client) | **Lean Team (§2B)** | Offloaded ownership. "I own the family, you don't babysit a third vendor." |
| $100K+/mo, 90+/wk testing | **Out of standalone fit** | Honest: "at that scale I'm one angle-family of leverage, not your creative department." One-family only, or refer. |

**Hard prerequisites before any send:** (1) the profile converts (§5.3, verified live); (2) the First Strike exists, clears the production bar, and is ready to send on the yes; (3) the `{{hook}}` is true/specific/says-the-quiet-part and **a human wrote it**; (4) the proof-only assets (First Strike, teardown link, guarantee) are live. A high hit rate and a scaled winner are the aim, never a claimed result; never fabricate a number, case study, testimonial, logo, or track record, and never restate a library operator's number as ours. Confident and honest, never apologetic. Newness is why the free First Strike and the risk reversal exist.
