Adwield — Daily Prospecting SOP

The operator's daily run sheet. Turns the artifact-first spine (outreach-playbook §3.5–3.7, §5.1) into a checklist you run without re-reading the strategy.

What this is. The repeatable daily motion for Adwield outreach. Strategy lives in outreach-playbook.md; the voice lives in adwield-brand-kit.md; the copy bar is copy-standard.md. This file is the how-many, in-what-order, by-when. Run it every working day.

The one guardrail over all of it (Proof-Only). Every metric is a target to measure, never a claimed result. No fabricated case studies, testimonials, logos, or numbers. No priority touch ships without a rendered First Strike and a hand-written true hook.

Channel order: X and LinkedIn DMs first. Operators live on X, zero warmup. Cold email is not the critical path.

The pre-send filter (every message). True. Specific. Says the quiet part. A competitor can't paste the same line. If you'd be annoyed receiving it 10x this week, don't send.

Do not skip a day. Prospecting has a half-life (playbook §1.12). What you skip today shows up as a dry pipeline 90 days out.

A. THE WATCHLIST (50–100 ICP accounts)

The hand-checked watchlist is the intent-data stack on a $750 budget (playbook §2). Build it once, maintain it daily.

ICP, every account on the list passes all four:

  • [ ] DTC performance-ads operator, $20–100K/mo Meta + TikTok spend.
  • [ ] Functional-consumables vertical (something used up and re-bought).
  • [ ] Active in the Meta Ad Library right now (you can see live ads).
  • [ ] Reachable on X or LinkedIn (a real operator account, not a faceless brand handle).

Tag each by archetype (sets the angle later):

  • [ ] Wounded Veteran — 5–10yr operator, post-Andromeda pain, "tested everything."
  • [ ] Volume-Player — ships 90+ creatives/wk, wants ops leverage.
  • [ ] Lean Team — 2–3 person team, ~$200–400K/mo, already using outside creative agencies. The ideal client; weight the list toward these.

Maintenance (daily, 10 min):

  • [ ] Add 2–5 new accounts to keep the list at 50–100.
  • [ ] Drop any that went dark in the Ad Library (no live ads = no trigger = no value).
  • [ ] Mark the ~10 A-tier leads (best-fit, clearest live trigger). A-tier gets the rendered First Strike first.
  • [ ] One row per account: handle, archetype, spend signal, last touched date, current status.

Warm first. Before any cold touch, check for a 2nd-degree or community path (playbook §2). A warm intro beats cold by a wide margin.

B. THE META AD LIBRARY SCAN

The engine for the {{hook}} (playbook §3.6). One prospect at a time. You're reading their account like an operator, not a tourist.

Open their advertiser page in the Ad Library and read for these tells:

1. The over-run creative (the account's load-bearing ad).

  • [ ] Find the ad that's been live longest and/or shows the most active duplicates.
  • [ ] Same creative re-run across many ad sets = the account is riding one winner. That's the hook: a good ad doing too much work, about to break.

2. The fatigue tells (the Fatigue Wall, ~7 weeks — brand kit §4).

  • [ ] A top creative live 7+ weeks with no fresh challenger behind it.
  • [ ] Minor re-cuts of the same hero ad (new text, same footage) = hand-cutting variations off one angle, no new angle in the pipe.
  • [ ] Thin or stale rotation — few new creatives launched recently relative to spend.
  • [ ] A visible public post about creative fatigue or post-Andromeda pain (X/LinkedIn) — pair it with the Library read.

3. The angle carrying the account.

  • [ ] Sort their live ads by theme. Name the one angle doing the heavy lifting (testimonial, founder-story, problem-agitate, UGC react, etc.).
  • [ ] That angle, named exactly, is the spine of both the hook and the First Strike.

Cross-reference their reviews / Trustpilot (playbook §3.6) for the exact pain language customers use. That phrasing becomes the ad's script. Pull 1–2 real review lines per priority prospect.

Cadence: scan each A-tier prospect the day you touch them; full watchlist sweep once a week. Queue what you find — don't write the hook in the same pass.

C. QUEUE ONE FRESH TRUE TRIGGER PER PROSPECT

One signal per prospect, and it must be fresh (playbook §2). Stacking signals reads like a research report, not a conversation.

  • [ ] Pick exactly ONE trigger per prospect from the Ad Library scan:
    • the over-run / load-bearing ad
    • a fatigue tell on the hero creative
    • the single angle carrying the account
    • a review theme (exact pain language)
    • a visible post about fatigue / post-Andromeda pain
  • [ ] Verifiable only. If you can't see it on their public account, it's not a trigger. Generic = no reply.
  • [ ] Date it. Log when you saw it. A stale signal reads as "didn't research you."
  • [ ] Log raw, not written. One line of the literal observation into the queue (e.g. "hero = the [product] testimonial ad, live ~9 wks, 6 active dupes, no challenger"). The hook gets written in Block D, by hand.

Tool pulls the signal; a human writes the hook (playbook §3.6). Never let AI write the hook — that's the slop the whole motion avoids.

D. BATCH-WRITE THE {{hook}}

Write the day's 10 hooks in one sitting from the queued raw signals. Batching beats one-at-a-time.

The {{hook}} bar — all three, every time (playbook §3.5):

  • [ ] True — verifiable from their public account.
  • [ ] Specific — names the actual ad / angle / review line / timeline. A number, a date, the product name.
  • [ ] Says the quiet part — something they half-suspected and didn't want to say out loud (e.g. "the whole account is riding one ad").

Voice (brand kit §8, copy-standard.md):

  • [ ] Lead with them, not you. Credentials live below the signature.
  • [ ] No pitch in the hook. The retainer, the loadout, the guarantee all wait for the reply.
  • [ ] Specific over adjectives. 7 weeks, 4 angles, 24 variations, the product name — never round-number vagueness.
  • [ ] Never lead with volume, the word "AI," or price (brand kit §8).
  • [ ] Earned empathy, not pity. "The game changed under you," never "I know it's been hard."
  • [ ] Flavor (equip / wield / loadout / crit / hit rate) only where it makes the meaning clearer or stickier. Otherwise plain English. Never cheesy, no emojis, no exclamation selling.

Copy-standard pass before any hook is queued to send:

  • [ ] No em-dash as connector. No triads. No "not X, it's Y." No filler (leverage, unlock, seamless, supercharge, game-changer).
  • [ ] Cut every non-load-bearing word. Target 30–60% cut.
  • [ ] Read it aloud. If it sounds like LinkedIn, redo it.

Hook-iteration (playbook §3.8): hold the body constant, rotate 3 first-lines across each ~30-send batch. Track reply rate, keep the winner, retire the other two, write a new challenger.

Reference exemplars: copy-standard.md → Example Messages #1–4 (Wounded Veteran, Lean Team, Volume-Player, the "AI is slop" reply-handler).

E. PRODUCE THE FIRST STRIKE (one ad, cheap, per prospect)

The First Strike is one battle-ready ad of their own product, rendered from their real site and reviews, before they pay a coin (playbook §5.1; brand kit §5 STEP 1). It carries the credibility the track record can't. No A-tier priority touch ships without one.

Steps to render one (per prospect):

  1. [ ] Pick the product — their hero SKU (the one the over-run ad sells).
  2. [ ] Pull the source assets from their public site: product shots / packaging, the name, the offer. No login, no scraping anything private.
  3. [ ] Pull 1–2 real review lines (their site or Trustpilot) for the script — their customers' exact pain language.
  4. [ ] Pick the angle — rebuild the single angle carrying their account (Block B), or the one fresh angle you'd test against their control.
  5. [ ] Write a ~15-sec script from the review language + that angle. Operator judgment picks which angle fits their AOV — that's the layer being demonstrated.
  6. [ ] Render one variation with the AI UGC tool (the gooseworks ad pipeline). One finished ad, not a pack. Check the mouth-sync and the product accuracy before it leaves your hands.
  7. [ ] Host it, don't attach (playbook §3.7). Link a hosted file. Attachments tank deliverability and trust.
  8. [ ] Sanity check: Is it actually their product, their pain language? Would a skeptical operator watch 15 sec and not wince? If no, re-render or cut.

Proof-Only on the render (brand kit §9):

  • [ ] No claimed result attached to it ("our hit rate is X"). The ad is the proof — let it carry itself.
  • [ ] Sample-host watch analytics are internal only, never quoted as traction.

Optional founder edge — the video/voice weapon (playbook §3.7). For top A-tier leads, screen-record their account/site with face-cam on, 30–45 sec, 3 beats: (1) name the exact thing on their screen, (2) the First Strike you made, (3) one soft ask. Host the file. This is the un-copied edge; competitors skip it because it doesn't scale.

F. THE DAILY 10-TOUCH RUN (X and LinkedIn first)

10 touches/day, each carrying a true hook. A-tier (rendered First Strike) goes out first.

Order of channels:

  1. [ ] X DMs — operators live here, zero warmup. Warm the DM with a reply or quote-tweet on one exact post, then DM: genuine specific praise tied to one post → the hook → the artifact, want me to send it? (copy-standard.md #1.)
  2. [ ] LinkedIn DMs — connection request with no note. On accept, open with the hook + the made-for-them artifact, never a pitch. (copy-standard.md #2.) Limit: 15–25 messages/seat/day.
  3. [ ] r/FacebookAds — intel and soft presence only. Mine voice-of-customer, be the helpful operator, never pitch (playbook §4.4).
  4. [ ] Cold email — warming in parallel, not the critical path. Skip unless the rig is live.

Every send, before it leaves:

  • [ ] One fresh, dated, verifiable trigger attached. No trigger, no send.
  • [ ] The hook leads. The retainer / loadout / guarantee are not mentioned (they wait for the reply).
  • [ ] A-tier touch has its rendered First Strike linked (or the personal video for top leads).
  • [ ] One soft, interest-based close. "Want me to send it?" / "If it holds up, that's the whole pitch." No calendar link in touch one.
  • [ ] Ran the pre-send filter + the copy-standard read-aloud pass.

Log every touch: prospect, channel, trigger used, first-line variant (of the 3), First Strike sent (y/n), date. This feeds the §3.8 iteration loop.

On a reply: stop prospecting that lead, switch to the warm-reply objection tree (copy-standard.md) and the locked objection counters (playbook §5.1): "AI ads are slop" → show don't claim, never repeat the word; "AI does this free" → the judgment layer; "my agency disappointed me" → 24 variations every two weeks, not 4 in a month. The No-Risk Loadout guarantee ("beat your control or the next cycle's free") and the loadout mechanism enter the conversation only now, after they bite.

END-OF-DAY CHECK

  • [ ] 10 touches sent, X + LinkedIn first.
  • [ ] Every A-tier touch carried a rendered First Strike + a hand-written true hook.
  • [ ] Watchlist still at 50–100, dark accounts dropped, A-tier marked.
  • [ ] Tomorrow's hooks queued from today's scan.
  • [ ] Touch log updated; first-line variant performance noted.

Diagnose by symptom (playbook §3.8): silence across all 3 first-lines = the trigger source is too generic, go back to Block B. High reply + low booking = fix the offer/copy, not targeting. Replies but wrong fit = tighten the watchlist (Block A).
