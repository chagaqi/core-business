VSL / Loom Scripts — Sales-Video Pack (v1)

Word-for-word spoken scripts for Adwield. AI-native ad creative for DTC functional-consumables brands.

Brand: Adwield (adwield.com) · Founder: Chaga Chai · Status: ready-to-record, PROOF-ONLY · Revised 2026-06-19 against the Real Scripts Library + outreach-proof.md

Source of truth: adwield-brand-kit.md. Big idea, said plainly in outreach/sales: you don't hire an agency that bills you to be slow, you get a finished ad of your own product before you pay, then a system that ships fresh creative every two weeks. The package: 4 angles × 24 variations every two weeks, launched and tagged in your account. Free offer is the First Strike (one finished ad of their product). The guarantee: month-to-month, beat-your-control-or-the-next-cycle-is-free. Public paths: Knight (DFY) and Forge (DWY) only, no self-serve. These four assets sell the service, not AI-UGC ad scripts. They sit on the locked offer, the cold sequence, the landing page, and the proof-only credibility system.

STRUCTURE-MATCHED TO PROVEN REAL SCRIPTS. Each asset below is built to mirror the spine of a real, published, at-scale operator script (captured verbatim in real-scripts-library.md) or the VSL story arc in outreach-playbook.md. The send-ready spoken copy stays clean; the STRUCTURE NOTE in each asset's rationale layer names the real parent it mirrors and links it. We did not invent these shapes. We run the proven play one notch sharper. The one place we go sharper than every real parent is below.

OUR ONE UPGRADE: ARTIFACT-FIRST. Where the real operators open with a claim (Josh Nelson: "I have some ideas that could work for your business too"; HubSpot: "I have an idea I can explain in 10 minutes"; Berman: "I'm in charge of growing the number of clients agencies like yours get"), Adwield opens with the finished, made-for-them artifact — the rendered First Strike of their own product, or a live teardown of their own account. The artifact replaces the claim. It is the same move-2 in their frameworks (Abraham's {risk minimizer}, Fazio's "here's exactly how you can achieve [benefit]"), in a stronger form: a thing already built beats a promise to build it. None of the real operators do this. It is the only divergence we keep.

GAMING METAPHOR STAYS OUT OF HERE. The MMO/RPG flavor lives in the brand kit and on the website/sales-page, never in outreach or sales video. In these scripts use plain operator language: "the package / the system / 4 angles × 24 variations" (not "loadout"), "scaled winner / breakout" (not "crit"), "run / own" (not "wield"), "we build / a finished ad of your product" (not "equip your weapon / secret weapon / battle-ready"), "creative fatigue around week 7" (not "the Fatigue Wall" as a sold concept), "the guarantee" (not "the No-Risk Loadout"), plain tier names (not "S-Tier / Raid Tier / Starter Loadout"). Keep as proper nouns only: Adwield, First Strike, Knight, Forge.

VOICE LOCK. Operator, not guru. Plain declarative sentences. Specific ($20–100K/mo, 24 variations, 4 angles, 2-week cycles, 7-week fatigue, 5-brand cap). No emojis, no exclamation selling. Volume is never the hero, only how the winner gets found faster. Never lead with "AI." Earned empathy, never pity. Banned: revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge, synergy, death spiral, "got your nights back," "effortless," "near-zero effort." No dead openers: no "hope this finds you," no "quick question," no "just following up / circling back / bumping," no standalone "Not a pitch." sign-off. At most one earned reassurance per script.

PROOF LOCK. Zero ad case studies at launch. No fabricated metrics, testimonials, or logos, ever. The WEAKER-flagged numbers in the library (HubSpot's 57%/21%, Nelson's 41% close, the 34%/22%/30% Loom rates, anyone's revenue) are THEIRS, captured as their published claims, and are never restated as Adwield's results in any spoken line. A winning creative and a scaled winner are the aim, never a claimed result. The home-gym business appears once, lightly, as character ("a real business of my own"), never as ad-proof. Trust is demonstration: the free First Strike, the live teardown, a real self-test number, founding-partner deltas, the guarantee. The guarantee currency is labor (next cycle free), not cash. Prospect is the hero, you are the guide, the villain is creative fatigue around week 7 and the post-Andromeda auction. One soft ask. The artifact carries the credibility. Every [CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER] stays literal until a real delta exists.

The four: (1) Cold-DM Loom (60–90s) walks a priority prospect through the First Strike you built of their product; becomes {{sampleLink}} in the cold sequence, delivered on the permission-yes. (2) Landing-Page VSL (3–5 min) carries the full argument. (3) Discovery-Call Pre-Frame (90s–2min) in the Calendly confirmation. (4) Case-Study-Delta (60s), recorded only after a real pilot produces a real delta.

How to read these

  • SPOKEN: = exact words. Warm the phrasing, never change the claims.
  • [ON-SCREEN] / [ACTION] = what the viewer sees / what you do. (0:00) = running timestamp.
  • STRUCTURE NOTE: = the rationale layer. Names the proven real parent the asset's spine mirrors, with the link, plus where the artifact-first upgrade replaces the parent's claim. This never gets recorded. It is the citation, not the script.
  • Merge fields (keep braces): {{firstName}}, {{brand}}, {{product}}, {{hook}}. Real numbers in Asset 4 are __% / __× placeholders, filled from real pilot data only.
  • {{hook}} is human-written, always. A tool pulls the raw signal (Meta Ad Library, review themes); a human writes the one-line hook. Never let AI write the per-prospect hook. (Per outreach-proof.md Tactic 3: AI/token personalization now converts worse than none, 8 sources. Non-negotiable wherever a script touches personalization.)
  • Recording bar. Webcam or phone, on-brand. Good mic beats good camera. One take with a cut beats a polished read. Plain background, real desk. No music.

BODY PART 1 — The 7-week truth (the Problem + Root cause) (0:18–1:05)

[ON-SCREEN] Face-to-cam.

SPOKEN: "The post-Andromeda auction rewards whoever keeps putting fresh, specific creative in front of the customer. Not better targeting. Fresher creative, faster. And fatigue sets in around week seven. So if you're shipping four tests a month and hoping, the math is against you. You physically can't produce at the rate the auction now demands.

That was never an instinct problem. You know what to test. It's a hands problem. A two-person team can't brief, produce, QC, launch, and read twenty-four variations every two weeks and run the rest of the business. The winner you need is sitting in the variations you don't have time to make."

BODY PART 2 — The mechanism, 4×24 (the Solution / the "we do this by…") (1:05–2:05)

[ON-SCREEN] Optional diagram: "4 angles → 24 variations → 2 weeks → kill losers, double winners → reload." Or stay face-to-cam.

SPOKEN: "The package is four angles, twenty-four variations. And the name isn't the magic. 'Test four angles and iterate' is what good operators already do. The magic is three things: who picks the angles, the production economics that make twenty-four affordable, and the fact that I do the production-and-launch labor your two-person team doesn't have hands for.

Every two weeks you greenlight four angles and the claims you can back. I turn those into twenty-four production-grade UGC variations, launched and tagged in your account, your naming, your ad-set structure. Not twenty-four files dumped in a Drive folder. I read them at day fourteen, kill the losers, double the winners, load the next four angles. A fresh winner is always ready before creative fatigue around week seven breaks the last one.

Volume isn't the answer. It's how the next breakout gets found faster. Each video lands in the two-to-five-hundred-dollar all-in range, which is the only reason fielding twenty-four at a time is affordable."
