What actually broke (and why none of your fixes touched it)

Creative fatigue sets in around 7 weeks. Call it The Fatigue Wall. Your winners were never going to last. A creative crushing today is, on the auction's clock, already aging toward the wall, and the moment it breaks, performance falls off whether or not you touched a thing. An ad strategist on r/advertising put it exactly right: "creative fatigue sets in at 7 weeks… we've been running 26-week campaigns when our end customers are disengaged at week 7."

So the only real defense is fresh, specific variation, faster than fatigue can kill the winner. Not a better ad or a smarter hook, a steady supply of fresh ones. Every targeting fix you tried treated a creative-supply problem as a targeting problem. That's why none of them worked.

The fix: your loadout, the 4×24 Framework

Here's exactly what I do. Your loadout is 4 angles × 24 variations, the weapon you equip every two weeks.

4 hypothesis angles × 24 production-grade AI UGC variations ÷ every 2 weeks = one crit, found on a schedule.

Every two weeks, the loop runs:

  1. You greenlight 4 angles and the specific claims each can make. You approve the direction, not 24 surprises.
  2. I send the 4 hooks plus script directions per angle for sign-off before mass production. The 24 are variations on approved ideas.
  3. I produce all 24: scripted, rendered, run through a pre-launch QC pass for mouth-sync, hands, render quality, brand-safety, claim language. Anything that fails the slop bar never touches your spend.
  4. Launched and tagged in your account, in your naming convention and ad-set structure. Live, ready to read against your metrics. (Or handed off as files to launch yourself. Your call.)
  5. Read at day 14. Kill the losers, double the winners, load the next 4 angles. You get a decision log: what we killed, what we're doubling, head-to-head vs your control.

So instead of four tests a month and praying, you're running 24 production-grade variations every two weeks, keeping a fresh winner equipped before the last one breaks.

Every word defended, because a guarantee from an unknown is worth nothing if it's vague:

  • CPA holds flat or better. This ties the promise to the number you fear. High-hold creative that bleeds cash fails the guarantee.
