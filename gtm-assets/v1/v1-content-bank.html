W7 · THU · P5 Tactical — tutorial

Thread (4). Angle vs variation, worked beverage example.

X (thread):

[1/4]
Your creative testing feels random because you're testing variations while you think you're testing angles. They aren't the same, and confusing them is why you can't reload a winner when it dies.
[2/4]
An ANGLE is a hypothesis about why someone buys.
"People reach for this drink to replace the 3pm energy-drink crash."

A VARIATION is one execution of that angle: a hook, a presenter, a first 3 seconds.
[3/4]
Test 24 variations of ONE angle and you learn which execution wins. You've tested one reason to buy.

Test 4 angles × 6 variations and you learn which reason moves your audience AND which execution lands. A win you understand, you reload at week 7. A win you stumbled into, you lose again.
[4/4]
So the structure is always a small number of real angles, 4 is workable, with many variations each.

Not a volume flex. The only way to learn the why, not just the that. Steal the framework. It works without me.
