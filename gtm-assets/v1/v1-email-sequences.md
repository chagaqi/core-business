# Adwield — Cold Email Sequences (v1)

**Brand: Adwield (adwield.com) · Founder: Dylan · Smartlead-ready · Proof-only**
**Bar: `copy-standard.md`. Spine: `outreach-playbook.md` §3.5 / §4.1. Voice: `adwield-brand-kit.md`.**
**Structure-matched to `real-scripts-library.md`. Each template names the real parent it mirrors in its note layer.**

> **What this rebuild is.** Every template below is structure-matched to a proven, published, real operator script in `real-scripts-library.md` (or the playbook VSL arc). The send-ready copy stays clean; the **rationale note under each template names the real parent + the link**. We didn't invent a sequence. We ran the proven play one notch sharper.
>
> **Our one upgrade — ARTIFACT-FIRST (explicit).** Every real agency operator in the library opens with a *claim*: Fazio "I've helped X get Y," Josh Nelson "I have some ideas," Berman "I'm in charge of growing the number of clients," Abraham "If I could generate X." We open with the **finished, made-for-them ad** instead of the claim. The artifact replaces the promise. That is the whole upgrade, and it's the one thing none of the real parents do (`outreach-proof.md` Tactic 10, the most-corroborated pattern in the file, 9 sources).
>
> **Hard prerequisite.** No priority cold email goes out unless a rendered First Strike already exists for that lead. `{{sampleLink}}` resolves *before* the first send. The sample carries the trust; the copy is the frame.
>
> **Permission-first default (per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 10 / divergence 1).** Email 1 *asks* ("I built you a 15-second ad of {{product}} — want me to send it?") and delivers the link on the "yes." It does not embed the link. Every documented Loom/sample case converts higher asking first than dropping the link cold. An embedded-link variant is kept per sequence, but only as an explicit A/B; permission-first is the favored default. Track reply-then-send-rate, not opens.
>
> **Cadence (§4.1, per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 4).** 3 to 5 emails over a ~20-day window. First touch under 80 words. Each follow-up adds new info, never "just bumping this." **The first follow-up is the single most valuable message after the opener — make it the most value-dense email in the sequence**, never a thin "did it come through." One soft interest-based CTA per email. In-thread, blank subject after touch 1. Stop after the last email. Never drift to 8 to 12 touches or a compressed few-day burn.
>
> **Proof state.** Zero case studies at launch. No fabricated numbers. `[CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER]` marks where a real founding-partner delta goes once cycle 1 ships, never before. The WEAKER-flagged stats in `real-scripts-library.md` and `outreach-proof.md` (HubSpot's 57%/21%, the 34%/22%/30% Loom rates, Josh Nelson's 41% close, anyone's revenue) are **theirs, never restated as ours**. Founding-slot count is the true current number or the clause is cut. The home-gym business never appears in cold copy.
>
> **Merge fields.** `{{firstName}}`, `{{brand}}`, `{{product}}`, `{{sampleLink}}`, `{{hook}}`. The two that make or break the send: a true, specific, says-the-quiet-part `{{hook}}`, and a hand-cleaned lowercase `{{product}}`. **The hook is human-written, always (per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 3 / the non-negotiable): a tool pulls the raw signal, a human writes the hook. AI-written personalization now converts worse than none.** Auto-generate either field and you've built slop.
>
> **Front-end rule.** Public copy names only two paths: hire a Knight (DFY) and build a Forge (DWY). No self-serve tier, ever.

**Shared parents across all four sequences:** Email 2 (send-on-yes + heaviest value) mirrors **Fazio Email 2** (the proof/case-study offer 3 days after the opener) crossed with the **permission-then-sample** pattern (`outreach-proof.md` Tactic 10). Email 3 (the standing arrangement) mirrors the **Lavender Mouse Trap** (signal → "would it be helpful?" → the mechanism). Email 4 (breakup) mirrors **Sam Nelson's Agoge breakup #1** ("I'll close your file / tell me where I went wrong"). Links live in each template's note.

**Routing.** One lead gets one sequence per outreach window. The B/C/D sequences give a different true door for a re-approach and angle variety across a batch. Pick the sequence whose `{{hook}}` you can write true for that lead.

**How each sequence is built.** Email 1 is the **permission-ask** (default): the hook, then "want me to send it?" — no link. Email 2 is the **send-on-yes + value** touch: it delivers `{{sampleLink}}` *and* carries the heaviest single piece of new info, because the first follow-up is the most valuable message in the sequence. Email 3 deepens the mechanism. Email 4 is the useful breakup. Each sequence also lists an **embedded-link A/B variant of Email 1** for the explicit test against permission-first.

# SEQ A — TRACK 1 — the ad past its clock
### Hook: a specific ad of theirs still running long past week-7 fatigue.

**Routing:** `track = 1`. 5 to 10yr operators gutted by the Feb-2025 Andromeda update.
**Use when:** your `{{hook}}` research names a specific stale ad. Same creative dominating their Meta Ad Library, a dated hook, a seasonal angle out of season.
**Voice:** open on the wound, pivot fast to the production lever. No grief marinade.

---

### Email 1 — first touch, permission-ask (under 80 words)
**Send:** Day 0
*Default. No link. Asks first, delivers on the yes (per `outreach-proof.md` divergence 1).*

```
Subject: {{brand}}'s ad has been running a while

{{firstName}}, {{hook}}. That one should've retired around week 7.
The auction stopped rewarding it no matter how good it was.

So I built its replacement. A 15-second UGC ad of {{product}}, pulled
from your own site and reviews. Not a mockup.

I'd rather build it than claim it, so I made the ad first. Want me
to send it over?
```

**A/B subjects:**
1. `{{brand}}'s ad has been running a while`
2. `the {{product}} ad that won't die`
3. `built {{brand}} a replacement`

> **Structure: Daniel Fazio 3-email sequence — https://medium.com/@danielfazio/the-best-b2b-cold-email-template-ive-ever-used-b28166cf709e** (`[VERBATIM]`, 100k+ sends). Fazio's opener = a researched observation about them, then a soft single ask. We hold his shape (researched line → easy ask) and apply the **artifact-first upgrade**: where Fazio's move-2 is the *claim* "I've helped [#] businesses get [benefit]," we replace the claim with the **finished ad we already built for them** and ask permission to send it. The honesty ("I'd rather build it than claim it") sits late, as the reason for the free sample, never as an opener admission.

> **Email 1 — embedded-link A/B variant (test against the permission-ask, not the default).** Replaces the last two lines with the link in-message:
> ```
> I'd rather build it than claim it, so I made the ad first. Watch
> the pour shot: {{sampleLink}}
> ```

### Email 2 — send-on-yes + the value (the most important message in the sequence)
**Send:** Day 3 (or immediately on a "yes" to Email 1)

### The artifact-first upgrade (the one thing every real parent does NOT do)

Every real Email-1 parent opens with a **claim**: Fazio "I've helped [#] businesses get [benefit]," Prospeo "I help [niche] fix that," Abraham "If I could generate [result]," Lavender "our tool helps teams ramp faster." We replace the claim with the **finished, made-for-them ad** and permission-gate it ("want me to send it?"). The artifact is the credential the claim was reaching for, delivered up front. The honest "newer shop" admission never opens a message; it lives late, in the breakup, as the *reason* the First Strike and the founding cycle are free. That single substitution is the upgrade — and per `outreach-proof.md` Tactic 10 (9 sources, the most-corroborated pattern in the file), it's a stronger version of the exact move-2 these operators already run.

### First-line rotation (§3.8)

Hold the body constant. Rotate the 3 A/B subjects across each batch of ~30 sends. Read reply rate, not opens. Keep the winner, retire the rest, write a new challenger. Diagnose by symptom: high reply + low booking = fix copy/offer; high bounce = data hygiene; silence across all three = the trigger source is too generic. Heavy spintax has a detectable signature and torches domains. Don't use it.
