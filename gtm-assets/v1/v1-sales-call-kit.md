> **Non-negotiables.** No ad case studies, no paid-ads history, because there are none. The made-for-them sample is the trust spine. Every case-study reference stays `[CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER]`. The ~$2M home-gym business appears once at most, lightly, as character, never as ad-proof. Public copy names only Knight (DFY) and Forge (DWY). No self-serve tier. Find spend first, then route to one Track. Never lead with "AI." Quote retainers live, never publicly. Never discount the retainer to close. `[FILL: …]` is a real detail you supply before sending. Never invent a number to fill one. The library's weak / self-reported numbers (HubSpot's 57%/21%, anyone's revenue, the 34% Loom rate) are *theirs*, never restated as ours.

> **Lead with strength.** The opener leads with the specific hook and the made-for-them artifact. The "I'm newer than the ten-year agencies" line never opens anything. That honesty lands *late*, as the reason the sample is free, never as a cold admission of no track record.

### 0:00–2:30 — Opener (Clarify, the frame)

> **▸ Structure: Hormozi CLOSER "Clarify" — "What made you take this call?" verbatim opener.** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #3](../real-scripts-library.md). We keep his literal Clarify question; the selectivity + decision-pre-frame wrapping is ours.

> "Thanks for the time. Two things before I say a word about how I work. One: I take five functional-consumables brands total, that's the whole shop, so half my job today is working out whether you're one I should take. If you're not a fit I'll say so and point you somewhere better. Two: you already watched the sample I made of [THEIR PRODUCT], so you know I'd rather build than talk.
>
> Here's how I'd like to run it, if that works for you: I ask about the account, I show you what I'd do, and by the end we'll both know whether this is a fit. I'll ask you straight at the end either way, so you're not stuck deciding later in the dark. And it's just you on the creative-spend call, right, nobody else who'd need to sign off?
>
> So start with you. What made you take this call? What's actually going on in the account?"

*Then stop.* Their unprompted framing is the best Track signal you'll get. Note whether they open on grief ("our winner died and nothing's worked since") or on capacity ("we're winning, we just can't produce fast enough").

*The middle paragraph is the **decision pre-frame**: set who decides and the agenda up front, and tell them you'll ask for a decision at the end. Cheapest anti-ghosting move there is. (per outreach-proof.md Tactic 7: add a decision pre-frame to the agenda.) The "you already watched the sample" line is the **artifact-first upgrade** doing its work: the prospect is warm because the made ad arrived before the call, so Clarify opens on a believer, not a skeptic.*

**If they open by complimenting the sample** → "Glad it landed. We'll come back to it. First I want the account." *(Bank the goodwill, don't pivot to closing.)*

### 2:30–5:00 — SPEND (the branch decider)

> **▸ Structure: Voss "no-oriented" + calibrated question — a low-pressure factual ask that hands the prospect control ("Is now a bad time to talk?" energy, applied to the spend number).** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #2 Voss](../real-scripts-library.md). Asking for the band, not the exact figure, is the calibrated-question move: easy to answer, no pressure, gives you the route.

Ask it plainly and early. Everything downstream changes on it.

> "Easiest place to start. What are you spending a month across Meta and TikTok? Rough is fine, I just need the tier."

**Decide the branch silently. Don't say the tier name.**

| Their number | Silent branch | The call becomes |
|---|---|---|
| **$20–100K/mo** | Tier B, primary | Retainer pitch. Pack is the cautious foot-in-door |
| **$5–20K/mo** | Tier A, downsell | Pack-first. Don't pitch a retainer they can't absorb |
| **$100K+, 90+/wk testing** | Out of fit | Honest disqualify, or one-angle-of-leverage framing |
| **Under $5K or pre-launch** | Out of fit | Point them to fix offer/LP/PMF first |

**Say the matching one:**
- $20–100K → "Good. That's the tier I built this for. Let me find where the pain is, then I'll show you what it'd look like."
- $5–20K → "Got it. You're a notch earlier than my retainer tier, so I wouldn't put you on one even if you asked. I'd start you somewhere lower-risk. First, the account."
- $100K+/90+wk → "Straight answer: at ninety tests a week I'm not your creative department. I'd be one angle-family at most, and I'd rather say that to your face than oversell you. Do you want a single extra lever, or someone built for your scale?"
- Under $5K / pre-launch → "I'll be honest. You're earlier than where my work pays for itself. Making more creative before the offer and the landing page pull their weight wastes money. Let me tell you what I'd fix first, for free, and come back when the spend's there."

**If they dodge the number** → "Fair. I just need the band, so I don't pitch you the wrong thing. Under five a month, five to twenty, twenty to a hundred, or north of that?" *(Still won't give one? Yellow flag. Qualify harder on decision-maker.)*

### 5:00–7:30 — Three hard gates (vertical, decision, claims)

> **▸ Structure: Voss calibrated questions in series ("What is the biggest challenge you face?" form), each one a "how/what" that makes the prospect supply the qualifying fact.** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #2 Voss](../real-scripts-library.md).

> "Quick three, rapid-fire. One: is it functional consumables? Coffee, gummies, beverage, greens, nootropics, that lane? Two: are you the one who says yes on creative spend, or is there someone above you? Three: the claims you run on, can you genuinely substantiate them, or are you flying close to the FTC line and hoping?"

- **Vertical** is locked. Beauty and fashion are an explicit no, synthetic-avatar uncanny valley. Outside the lane, disqualify kindly.
- **Decision-maker** is required. If they're not it: "No problem. Who is, and can they be on the next one? I only walk through numbers with the person who can say go."
- **Claims** previews the compliance gate as a feature: "I ask because claim language is what gets a supplement account banned, not the AI. Gating it is part of what you're paying me for."

### 7:30–14:00 — Pain-dig (Overview: their past attempts and the wound)

> **▸ Structure: Hormozi CLOSER "Overview" ("What have you tried so far? How did that work? Why didn't it work?") fused with Miner NEPQ Problem-Awareness questions ("Can you walk me through the biggest challenges? How long has this been going on? What's the impact on your day-to-day?"), closed by Miner's Consequence question.** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #3 Hormozi Overview](../real-scripts-library.md) + [#1 Miner Problem-Awareness & Consequence](../real-scripts-library.md). Miner's rule governs the whole beat: "Listen 80%, talk 20%. Let the questions do the selling."

Ask, then shut up. Their answers route the pitch. The bracketed probes are for when the first answer is thin.

1. **"How many creative tests are you actually getting *into the account* in a month right now?"**
   *Probe (Miner, how-long/impact):* "And how many are genuinely new *angles* versus the same angle re-cut?" *(The angle-vs-recut split shows whether it's a strategy problem or a throughput one.)*

2. **"When a winner dies on you, does it die slow around the six-or-seven-week mark, or fall off a cliff one day?"**
   *Probe (Hormozi Overview, "how did that work for you"):* "And when it dies, do you have the next one ready, or are you scrambling?" *("Scrambling" means the production gap is real and felt.)*
   **Consequence question (don't skip):** *"And if nothing changes, the next winner dies the same way in a couple months and you're back here scrambling again. What does that actually cost you?"* *(This is Miner's Consequence question verbatim in spirit — "What happens if nothing changes in the next 6-12 months?" Make HIM voice the cost. Don't supply the number, don't hint at it. The dying-winner cost has to come out of his mouth, or the guarantee lands on nothing. per outreach-proof.md Tactic 7.)*

3. **"Who's making the creative today? You at 1am, a freelancer, an agency, your ops people?"** *(Hormozi Overview, "why do you think that didn't work.")*
   *Probe:* "And how long from 'we should test this angle' to it being live?" *(Cycle-time is the throughput wound made concrete. Weeks means the bottleneck is real.)*

4. **"Did mid-February last year do anything to the account?"**
   *Probe only if they light up:* "Walk me through it. What broke, what did you try?" *(The Andromeda tell. Grief routes to Refuge. A shrug means Leverage. Don't manufacture a wound they don't have.)*

5. **"Last one, and it's the fork. If you could wave a wand, is the real problem that you don't *know* what to test, or that you can't physically *produce and launch* enough to find out?"**
   *(Almost always throughput. Strategy-doubt leans Refuge. Pure throughput leans Leverage.)*

### 14:00–15:00 — Track confirmation (Label: say it back BEFORE you pitch)

> **▸ Structure: Hormozi CLOSER "Label" ("So it sounds like… is your goal. Does that sound about right?") run through Voss's tactical-empathy labels ("It sounds like…", "It seems like…").** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #3 Hormozi Label](../real-scripts-library.md) + [#2 Voss labels](../real-scripts-library.md). Load-bearing: it stops you routing a winning skeptic into grief. Reflect what you heard. Let them correct you.

**Heard grief / Andromeda / agency-burn / "nothing works":**
> "Let me say back what I'm hearing, tell me if I've got it wrong. Something broke that wasn't your fault, you've tried every targeting and budget fix, none of it touched the real problem, and you're making maybe four things a month and praying. That right?" → *On the yes, pitch **Refuge**.*

**Heard "we're winning, we just can't produce fast enough":**
> "Let me reflect it so I pitch the right thing. You don't have a strategy problem, you're already winning, the issue is pure throughput: ops is the bottleneck and creative ships slower than you can test. Fair?" → *On the yes, pitch **Leverage**. Don't bring up Andromeda.*

**Genuinely mixed:** default to **Leverage** unless they explicitly grieve. A winner handed grief reads as talking past him.

### 21:00–24:00 — Guarantee, cap, recap (Explain concerns, pre-close)

> **▸ Structure: Hormozi CLOSER "Explain away their concerns" before the price, plus the Label-recap that pre-closes by making them say yes to the summary.** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #3 Hormozi](../real-scripts-library.md). The risk reversal *is* the lever.

Present the guarantee verbatim.

> "Here's the deal. Within 30 days my creative beats your current best ad on hook-rate and hold-rate, the 3-second and the thumbstop, and your CPA holds flat or better, measured against your own control, in your own account, with the head-to-head data on the table. If it doesn't, your next 24-variation cycle is free. Month-to-month, cancel anytime, you keep every video either way."

Then the cap. Real numbers only.

> "The cap is real and published. I take five functional-consumables brands, so you can never become the deprioritized account on somebody's list. I've got [FILL: N] founding slots left, and founding partners get terms I won't repeat later."

Then the recap. It pre-closes by making them say yes to the summary.

> "Let me make sure I've got your situation right before I show you numbers. You're at [their spend] a month, [their pain in their words], and what you'd want from me is [the relevant outcome]. Miss anything?"

*Their "no, that's it" is a micro-commitment. They've confirmed the problem, the stakes, and the outcome in their own words. The ask lands on three yeses.*

### 24:00–27:00 — Buying-temperature read (Reinforce / Commitment)

> **▸ Structure: Miner NEPQ Commitment question ("What would you need to see to feel confident moving forward?") + Voss no-oriented question ("Have you given up on this project?") to surface the real hesitation safely.** — [real-scripts-library.md → Discovery #1 Miner Commitment](../real-scripts-library.md) + [#2 Voss no-oriented](../real-scripts-library.md). Surface the hesitation now, on the call, instead of in the silence afterward.

> "Before I size anything. On a scale where 10 is 'where do I sign' and 1 is 'this isn't for me,' where are you honestly?"

- **8–10:** "Good. Let me show you exactly what it looks like." → the ask.
- **5–7:** "Fair. What's the gap between where you are and a 9? The real one." → *Handle it with §2 before quoting. A price on top of an unspoken objection just gives them a number to say no to. (Miner Commitment: "What would you need to see to feel confident?")*
- **Below 5:** "Honest of you. Sounds like this might not be the moment, which is fine. What would have to be true for this to be a clear yes?" → *Fixable, handle it. Timing or spend, exit warm onto the §6 nurture. (Voss no-oriented: giving them room to say "no" returns control and keeps them talking.)*

### 27:00–30:00 — The single ask (Prospeo gap-close, branched)

> **▸ Structure: Prospeo SMMA gap-creation close ("I've got a few ideas on how to close that gap… can we grab 15 minutes so I can walk you through what's working?").** — [real-scripts-library.md → Sales Call → Close #5 Prospeo](../real-scripts-library.md). The gap is named (their spend vs the winner they can't find fast enough); the ask is a small, low-friction next step.
>
> **▸ Artifact-first upgrade:** Prospeo's close offers to *show them ideas on a future call*. Ours offers to size a real number off the artifact they already saw, or hand over the actual pack now. The ideas aren't a promise of a future walk-through; the proof is already on the table, so the ask is to act on it, not to schedule a pitch.

**Tier B / retainer:**
> "Want me to size what a retainer looks like for your spend and SKU mix? Three minutes, built off your numbers, not a rate card." → **§3, retainer-sizing.**

**Tier A / pack:**
> "I'd start you on the 10-video pack. $497, one-time, lowest-risk way to see the work land in your own account. Want the checkout link now while we're on?" → **§3, pack-close.**

**Always offer the unforced out (locked):**
> "And if this isn't the right time, that's a real answer. I'd rather you come back ready than sign someone who's half-in. Want me to check back in a couple weeks?"

# §2 — Objection Bank (16 net-new, #11–26)

> **These start at #11** so they never collide with doc 18's #1–10 ("AI is slop," "I can do it myself," "agencies disappoint me," "no case studies," "why not a creator," "can one person do volume AND quality," "AI looks fake," "just send a proposal," "too expensive," "let me think").
>
> **▸ Structure: every row runs the Miner NEPQ "Clarify → Discuss → Diffuse" sequence — the Clarifying question fires FIRST, then the scripted answer — paired with the Prospeo agency-specific price/think-about-it rebuttals.** — [real-scripts-library.md → Sales Call → Objections #6 Miner & Prospeo](../real-scripts-library.md). Two rules govern every row. **(1) Clarify before you answer** (Miner's signature move: "When you say [X], what specifically do you mean?"). Ask it, let them answer, *then* deploy the scripted response, so you're answering the objection they actually have. A price objection is usually a belief gap upstream, not a money problem. **(2) Concede what's true first**, then land the real distinction. Never defensive. Proof-only throughout. *(per outreach-proof.md Tactic 9.)*
>
> **▸ Artifact-first upgrade:** where the real rebuttals defer proof to "let me show you on a call" or "my clients see [result]," ours points repeatedly back to the sample they already have. References (#19), proof (#18), "too cheap" (#20) all resolve to the rendered ad on their own product, not a borrowed claim.
