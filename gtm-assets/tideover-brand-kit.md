## 1. The Name + What It Means

**TIDEOVER** — from the idiom **"to tide someone over"**: to support or sustain a person through a difficult, in-between stretch until things get better. You give someone enough to *tide them over* until the real thing arrives.

That is the whole brand in one word. A presale customer has paid, but the product is 60–120 days away. They're stuck in the in-between — anxious, second-guessing, one frustrating silence away from a refund request or a chargeback. **Tideover is what carries them across that gap, calm and aboard, until their order ships.**

- The name is a **promise**, not a feature. It says what we do *for the customer* (carry them through the wait), which is exactly what the merchant is buying.
- It is warm, human, and plain-English — no jargon, no tech-bro suffix, no "AI" bolted on. The word does the emotional work.
- "Tide" also quietly evokes patience and inevitability: the tide always comes in. The order *is* coming. We keep people steady until it does.

**One-liner:** *Tideover keeps your presale buyers calm — and aboard — through the long wait, so they don't bail before their order ships.*

**The bolt-on truth (say it early, everywhere):** *Tideover bolts onto the helpdesk you already run and takes only your presale tickets — everything else stays exactly where it is. Nothing to migrate, nothing to rip out.* This is a **core value proposition**, not a footnote: the merchant adds the presale brain their stack never had and changes nothing else.

## 2. The Big Idea

**Tideover is a presale-specialist support layer for the long wait.**

Presale and preorder brands collect cash today and ship in 60–120 days. That gap is an **emotional liability**: inboxes flood with "where is my order?", customers file chargebacks and call the brand a scam, and generic helpdesks (Gorgias / Tidio / Intercom) have **no idea which orders are preorders** or what to say at day 7 vs. day 60 vs. day 89.

Tideover plugs into the merchant's **existing** helpdesk (Gorgias / Tidio / Intercom — we don't replace it), knows **each order's real production timeline**, and delivers **timeline-aware, emotionally-intelligent reassurance** that keeps anxious buyers from bailing during the wait.

**Tideover is a bolt-on, not a rip-and-replace.** It routes **only** the presale/preorder-related tickets to Tideover; **every other ticket stays in the merchant's current helpdesk, handled exactly as it is today.** There is no infrastructure change, no migration, no new helpdesk for the team to learn — Tideover works *alongside* Gorgias / Tidio / Intercom and touches nothing but the presale wait. This is a **core reason to say yes**: the merchant keeps everything they already have and simply adds the presale brain it never had.

**What makes this defensible (the wedge):**
- The hard part is **not the AI** — LLMs are commoditized. The moat is the **day-by-day reassurance playbooks** (what to say at each stage of the wait) and the **outcome data** (which messages keep a buyer aboard).
- **No funded player owns "presale support."** Incumbents are generalists who treat "where's my order?" as a one-off ticket, not a months-long emotional journey. That gap is the wedge. (Source: idea 7901 traction analysis — no visible funded presale-support player, no search vocabulary, no patents.)
- The category has **no name yet**. Tideover gets to coin and own it.

**What Tideover is NOT:**
- Not a new helpdesk (we bolt onto theirs and take only the presale tickets — nothing else changes).
- Not a generic chatbot (we're presale-specialist, timeline-aware).
- Not a preorder/checkout app (those stop at *taking* the order; we own the wait *after*).
- Not an MMO / gamified / gimmicky product. This is a **trust + empathy brand.**

## 5. The Beachhead — Crowdfunding Graduates

**CHOSEN BEACHHEAD: Kickstarter / Indiegogo / BackerKit graduates moving onto Shopify.** Within that, prioritize **hardware + design + made-to-order creators with 60–120 day fulfillment windows and large backer lists.**

**Why this segment first (the explicit call):**
- **Most acute pain.** They juggle three customer groups at once on different timelines — Kickstarter backers, BackerKit late-pledges, and new Shopify preorders. Verbatim: *"Support is losing their minds trying to figure out who is who and what timeline they're on."* This is a 9/10 pain and the longest waits live here.
- **Clearest outbound list.** Unlike the unnamed "long-wait DTC" segment (no shared vocabulary, no list), crowdfunding graduates are **enumerable** — campaign pages, BackerKit community, r/kickstarter, r/Crowdfunding, "moving to our store" threads. A scout agent can build a named, time-bound prospect list of campaigns mid-fulfillment.
- **Already pay for workflow tools.** BackerKit is paid software; these creators are accustomed to paying for preorder/fulfillment tools. ~30–50% will pay $100–$300/mo.
- **Founder credibility maps 1:1** (see §8) — physical-goods, long-fulfillment, in-house customer comms is exactly this buyer's world.
- **Reusable playbook.** One crowdfunding-graduate playbook resells across the segment; made-to-order luxury and drop/scarcity brands inherit ~80% of it for expansion later.

**Deprioritized for now (not abandoned):** made-to-order luxury (#2 expansion), drop/scarcity brands (#3), micro/solo (too low ARPU), regulated/supplements (timeline-promise liability too high to learn on). **De-prioritized entirely:** building a full helpdesk.

## 6. The Concierge-First Model

**Prove the language and the impact manually before writing any software. Software automates only what's already proven.**

Tideover does **not** lead with an app. It leads with a **concierge pilot**: the founder + AI run a partner's presale support **by hand**, drafting timeline-aware replies *inside the partner's own Gorgias / Intercom / inbox*, capturing the exact reassurance language at each day-in-wait stage. Only once the day-by-day scripts are proven do they get encoded into a thin Shopify embedded app.

**Why concierge-first:**
- **Zero integration risk to start** — no build needed to deliver value or learn.
- **Manufactures the first real proof** — the day-stage playbook and the leading-indicator wins come from real tickets, not assumptions.
- **The moat is built here** — the playbooks and outcome data are authored in the concierge phase; the app just scales them.

**Live-support cadence (the pilot SLA):** replies are approved in **two fixed daily windows (AM + PM)**, told to partners up front. Between windows, only a small, pre-approved, timeline-safe holding template set may auto-send (e.g., *"Got your message — here's your current ETA band, full reply by [next window]"*). **Novel replies are always human-approved** — never auto-sent — to avoid hallucinated timeline promises. This is a load-bearing trust rule: in presale, a false delivery promise is far more damaging than a slow one.

### Leading-indicator framing (what we *can* prove in 90 days)

- **Partner-reported "saves"** — logged instances of an anxious/chargeback-threat customer talked back from the ledge.

Every metric is framed as a **target to measure against a clean baseline**, never a claimed result. The full refund-reduction case study lands **after the first cohort completes its wait** — and we say that plainly to every partner up front. `[CASE STUDY PLACEHOLDER — refund-reduction delta, post first completed cohort]`

### The honest operator war story (the founder's real credibility)
The founder ran a real **~$2M/yr physical-goods company** — warehouse, in-house shipping, fulfillment, **and** the "where's my order?" customer comms during long fulfillment waits. He lived this exact problem *from the merchant side.* That is legitimate, ownable, lived-experience credibility — the strongest asset Tideover has pre-proof.

## 9. Voice & Tone

**The voice of Tideover is the opposite of a cold helpdesk.** It is the steady, reassuring human who tells you your order is fine, here's exactly where it is, and you're in good hands — and means it.

**Five voice pillars:** **Warm. Calm. Reassuring. Human. Steady.**
Confident but **never gimmicky.** We are a trust + empathy brand — every word either builds calm or it doesn't ship.

### DO

- **Be warm and human.** Write like a real person who genuinely cares the customer isn't left in the dark. Contractions, plain words, a steady tone.
- **Be calm and steady.** Never breathless, never hype. The whole point is to lower the customer's pulse. Short, settled sentences.
- **Lead with reassurance + specifics.** Pair empathy ("totally understand the wait feels long") with a concrete, honest detail ("your order is in production; current ETA band is X–Y").
- **Acknowledge the feeling before the fact.** Name the anxiety first ("waiting on something you've already paid for is genuinely stressful"), then resolve it.
- **Use honest confidence bands, never hard promises.** "Currently tracking for [month]" / "ETA band X–Y," never an invented exact ship date.
- **Be plain-spoken.** No jargon, no "leverage / synergy / cutting-edge." If a real merchant wouldn't say it to a customer, we don't write it.
- **Speak to the merchant as a fellow operator** — peer to peer, "I've been in your inbox," not vendor-to-lead.
- **Stay quietly confident.** State what we do clearly and let the calm carry the authority.

### DON'T

- **Don't sound like a cold helpdesk / canned bot.** No "Your ticket has been received," no robotic deflection, no "per our policy."
- **Don't be gimmicky, hypey, or salesy.** No exclamation-point energy, no "🚀 revolutionary," no MMO/gaming theme, no growth-hack tone. This brand never winks at the reader.
- **Don't fabricate or imply proof.** No invented metrics, testimonials, star ratings, logos, or "trusted by thousands." (See §8.)
- **Don't make hard delivery promises** or guarantee exact dates. Confidence bands only — a false promise is the most damaging thing we can say.
- **Don't be cold, clinical, or corporate.** No buzzwords, no passive bureaucratic voice, no "we value your patience."
- **Don't manipulate or guilt the anxious customer.** Empathy is sincere, never a tactic. (Edge case: if production is cancelled, empathy that papers over it reads as manipulative — be honest.)
- **Don't over-claim the founder's background.** War story in cold outreach; the "$2M" number only in warm, substantiable contexts.

### Voice in one swap

> ❌ *"Your order has been received and is being processed. Please allow 60–120 days for fulfillment. Thank you for your patience."*
> ✅ *"Hey — totally get that waiting on something you've already paid for can feel like a long time. Quick reassurance: your order's in production and currently tracking for [month]. I'll keep you posted the moment that changes. You're in good hands here."*
