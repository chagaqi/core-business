## 1. Alex Hormozi — the C.L.O.S.E.R. framework

**Who he is / proof:** Founder of Acquisition.com; built and sold Gym Launch, Prestige Labs, ALAN; portfolio publicly stated at $100M+/yr in revenue. Author of *$100M Offers* and *$100M Leads*. He trained his own closers on this exact sequence (referenced in his "I Taught 115+ Salesmen My Closing Framework" episode).

**Framework (his acronym):** **C**larify · **L**abel · **O**verview · **S**ell the vacation · **E**xplain away concerns · **R**einforce the decision.

**Discovery / question sequence — VERBATIM** (as published in third-party write-ups of his framework; the question wording below is reproduced consistently across the Follow Up and Medium breakdowns):

*Clarify (why are they on the call):*
- "What made you take this call?"
- "What's your goal right now?"
- "Why is that important to you?"

*Label (mirror back the goal):*
- "So it sounds like… [X] is your goal."
- "Does that sound about right?"

*Overview (their past attempts — the high-value step):*
- "What have you tried so far to accomplish this?"
- "How did that work for you?"
- "Why do you think that didn't work?"

> Note: the well-known long-form version of the Overview question circulated from his talks is **"Tell me a little bit about what you've done so far to try and get past this."** I found that line in the framework summaries (search result), not a primary transcript — treat as `PARAPHRASE` of his spoken wording until matched to video.

**Sell / Explain / Reinforce:** Hormozi's published material gives *principles* here, not fixed question scripts — "sell the vacation, not the flight" (sell the outcome, not the mechanism); on Reinforce, the idea that buyers immediately question their decision so you affirm it post-sale (videos, handwritten card). Labeled `PARAPHRASE` because there is no fixed verbatim line.

**Credibility flag:** HIGH on the framework (it's his, he ran closers on it). MEDIUM on exact wording — the cleanest verbatim I could source is via reputable third-party breakdowns, not a Hormozi-hosted script page. The two independent write-ups agree on the question wording, which raises confidence.

## 2. Jeremy Miner — NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning)

**Who he is / proof:** Founder/CEO of 7th Level. His marketing claims $33M+ in personal sales, **351,000+ salespeople trained across 37 countries**, and features in Forbes, Inc., Entrepreneur, USA Today, Yahoo Finance (verbatim from his own NEPQ landing page — see proof link). Treat the revenue figure as self-reported; the training-volume and media features are checkable.

NEPQ phases: **Connection → Situation → Problem Awareness → Solution Awareness → Consequence → Commitment/Qualifying → Transition.**

### Discovery questions — VERBATIM (official 7th Level "NEPQ Sales Secrets Cheat Sheet", direct PDF)

*Problem Awareness:*
- "Can you walk me through the biggest challenges you're dealing with right now?"
- "What's happening in your business that made you start looking for help?"
- "How long has this been going on?"
- "What's been the impact on your day-to-day or your team?"

*Solution Awareness:*
- "What solutions have you considered so far?"
- "Do you feel like this could be the answer for you?"
- "What do you feel has been missing from what you've tried?"
- "What would the ideal solution look like for you?"

*Consequence:*
- "What happens if nothing changes in the next 6-12 months?"
- "How do you see this affecting your growth long-term?"
- "What would it mean for your business if this problem continues?"
- "What's the worst-case scenario if this goes unresolved?"

*Commitment:*
- "What would you need to see to feel confident moving forward?"
- "What could we do together to make this happen for you?"
- "Where do you feel we should go from here?"
- "If we could solve [problem], would that be worth it for you?"

*His best-practice rule (verbatim, same sheet):* "Listen 80%, talk 20%. Let the questions do the selling." / "Make them the hero. NEPQ helps prospects sell themselves on the solution."

### Earlier-phase wording — VERBATIM (third-party NEPQ flashcards/notes, lower trust than the official PDF)
- Connection: "Why are you here?" / "What are you looking for?"
- Situation: "What are you currently doing about [what they said in the connection phase]?"

### Top objection: "I need to think about it" — PARAPHRASE (pattern confirmed across his TikTok + flashcards, exact words not pulled from a clean primary source)
His signature move is to clarify the stall instead of arguing it:
> "When you say [think] about it, what specific parts of what we went over would you be [thinking] about?"

The same template swaps the verb to match the prospect ("…when you say *pray* about it, what specific parts…"). Labeled `PARAPHRASE` — the structure is unmistakably his and repeated verbatim across sources, but I'm not quoting from a single authoritative transcript.

**Credibility flag:** HIGH — the discovery questions come straight from his company's own free cheat-sheet PDF. The objection line is real and his, but I'm flagging the wording as reconstructed. The $33M figure is marketing; the 351k-trained / media-feature claims are on his own page and likely verifiable.

## 3. Chris Voss — tactical empathy & calibrated questions

**Who he is / proof:** 24-year FBI veteran, former **lead international kidnapping negotiator** for the FBI; founder of The Black Swan Group; author of *Never Split the Difference* (NYT bestseller, 2M+ copies). Credentials are public record.

### Calibrated questions — VERBATIM (his published "how/what" questions; functional negotiation lines)
- "How am I supposed to do that?"
- "What is the biggest challenge you face?"
- "What about this is important to you?"
- "How can I help to make this better for us?"
- "How would you like me to proceed?"
- "What is it that brought us into this situation?"
- "How can we solve this problem?"
- "What's the objective?"
- "What are we trying to accomplish here?"
- "How will we know we're on track?"
- "How will we address things if we find we're off track?"

### Labeling (tactical empathy openers) — VERBATIM
- "It seems like…"
- "It sounds like…"
- "It looks like…"

### "No"-oriented questions — VERBATIM (he wants a "no" to give the other side control)
- "Is now a bad time to talk?"
- "Have you given up on this project?"

### Mirroring — PARAPHRASE (technique, not a fixed line)
Repeat the last 1-3 words of what they said, then go silent — often paired with "Please, help me understand."

### Accusation audit / bad-news framing — VERBATIM phrase
- "You're probably not going to like this…" (his preferred pre-frame before delivering something hard; he contrasts it favorably against "Not to be rude…")

**Voice:** the "Late-Night FM DJ Voice" — deep, soft, slow, reassuring — is his recommended default tone. (Technique label, `PARAPHRASE`.)

**Credibility flag:** HIGHEST on pedigree (real FBI lead negotiator, not a guru). The calibrated questions and labels above are his standard published lines, but I sourced them from a well-regarded book-notes page rather than the book text, so I'm capping them at `VERBATIM (functional line)` — they are the literal questions he teaches, widely reproduced identically.

## 4. Cole Gordon — high-ticket discovery & closing

**Who he is / proof:** Founder of Closers.io (Gordon Media/7 Figure Selling Academy); publicly states he **scaled Closers.io past $30M/yr** in coaching sales; built a remote-closing training business (Remote Closing Academy / 30 Day Closer). Ex-high-ticket closer himself. Revenue is self-reported but widely cited.

### Framework — PARAPHRASE (concepts confirmed; I could not pull verbatim lines from his videos — YouTube transcripts weren't machine-readable here)
- **Two discovery syntaxes:** "Problem-First Syntax" and "Goals-First Syntax" — pick based on whether the prospect is moving away from pain or toward a desire.
- **"The gap":** two drivers — *actual pains* and *unfulfilled desires*; sell the gap between **where they are now** and **where they want to be**. Let beliefs get built by questions so "the prospect closes themselves."
- **Belief ladder:** sequence questions so the prospect states the beliefs that make your offer the logical next step, *before* you pitch.

## 5. Real agency (SMMA) discovery + close + objection scripts

**Source / proof:** Prospeo.io "SMMA Sales Script: Copy-Paste Scripts That Close (2026)." Prospeo is an email-finder/sales-data company (a real SaaS), publishing a swipe file. Credibility flag: MEDIUM — it's a vendor content piece, not an individual operator's battle-tested call. Treat as a clean, usable *template* rather than proof one closer ran it to a number. Useful because the wording is agency-specific and directly relevant to the ads agency.

### Discovery questions — VERBATIM (from the article)
- "Most [niche] owners deal with one of three things — inconsistent leads, wasted ad spend, or no time to post consistently. Which one hits closest?"
- "How are you getting new customers right now — referrals or paid campaigns?"
- "What have you tried before that didn't work?"
- "What's a new customer worth over 12 months?"
- "If you could get 10 qualified leads a month, what would that do for revenue?"
- "What happens if nothing changes in 6 months?"

### Cold-call frame (the gap-creation move) — VERBATIM
- Bridge: "Out of curiosity — are you handling your marketing in-house, or do you have someone running it?"
- Gap: "So where would you want to be in terms of leads per month if everything was working?"
- CTA: "I've got a few ideas on how to close that gap for [niche] businesses. Can we grab 15 minutes Thursday so I can walk you through what's working for [similar client]?"

### Objection rebuttals — VERBATIM
- **"I already have a marketing guy."** → "What results are they getting you? Most owners I talk to have someone posting but aren't seeing leads. I'd love to show you what's working in [niche] specifically."
- **"Send me an email."** → "Happy to. What's your biggest marketing challenge right now? That way I send something relevant, not a generic PDF."
- **"How much does it cost?"** → "Depends on what we'd build. My clients in [niche] invest $X-Y/mo and see [result]. Let's do 15 minutes so I can give you a real number."
- **"I've been burned before."** → "I hear that a lot. What happened? [Listen.] That's exactly why I lead with a strategy call — no commitment, just a clear plan."
- **"I'll think about it."** → "Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? I'd rather address it now than have you wondering about something I could answer in 30 seconds."

## Cross-source pattern (for our own call structure)

Three independent sources converge on the same "think about it" move: **clarify the stall, don't fight it** (Miner: "what specific parts…would you be thinking about?"; Prospeo: "what specifically do you want to think through?"). Same with the close: surface the *gap* (Gordon, Prospeo) and let the prospect state the cost of inaction (Miner's Consequence questions, Hormozi's Overview).
