What this is: actual sales/outreach scripts published by people who run or ran done-for-you agencies — the closest match to what Adwield is. Each entry has the operator, a direct link, a one-line proof they're real, and the script **verbatim** (their words, inside the quote block). My notes sit outside the quote block and follow `copy-standard.md`. Where I could only find a paraphrase or a third-party retelling, it's labeled as such — not passed off as verbatim.

Captured 2026-06-19. Credibility ranking is mine, stated honestly at the top of each entry.

## 1. Nick Abraham — Leadbird (cold-email lead-gen agency)

**Proof he's real:** Founder of Leadbird, a cold-email agency that "sends 1 to 1.5 million cold emails per month" with "over 150 active clients" (per the Startup Spells masterclass writeup); positioned as a ~$10M lead-gen agency in a Smartlead interview.
**Direct link (verbatim source):** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham_coldemail-prospecting-b2bsales-activity-7119728772051832833-dVJm
**Format:** LinkedIn post. The post first shows the bad pitch he says NOT to use, then his template, then his agency's live example.

VERBATIM (his post, including the "don't" and the "do"):
> The worst cold email pitch of all time: (I don't know how people still use this) "Are you taking on more clients?" It just sounds so scammy. Any client worth having wouldn't respond. Qualifying the leads would be a nightmare. You're much better off strictly focusing on providing value. Instead of baiting them into saying yes, say: If I could {generate X result} {risk minimizer} would you be interested in speaking? For example, for my lead generation agency, the script would say: If I could generate meeting-ready leads on a performance basis, would you be interested in speaking? Sending emails that bait prospects into replying wastes your time and theirs.

The template, isolated:
> If I could {generate X result} {risk minimizer} would you be interested in speaking?

His own filled-in version:
> If I could generate meeting-ready leads on a performance basis, would you be interested in speaking?

My note: this is the highest-signal entry. The `{risk minimizer}` slot ("on a performance basis") is the load-bearing part — it's the same move Adwield makes by leading with a built artifact instead of a promise. Worth lifting the structure, not the words.

## 2. Alex Berman — X27 / Experiment 27 (B2B lead-gen agency)

**Proof he's real:** Founder of X27 (formerly Experiment 27), a B2B lead-gen agency; co-author of *The Cold Email Manifesto*. The template below is his own case study, claiming the campaign "helped our clients to reach over $10 million worth of business."
**Direct link (verbatim source):** https://x27marketing.com/insights/tutorial-cold-email-template/
**Format:** Blog tutorial. He reproduces the actual email he sent to web/app dev agencies. Result he cites on that page: "~15%" book rate, "way over $10,000 in revenue" from 40 emails.

VERBATIM (the full email, brackets are his own merge fields):
> Subject Line: Question about <company>
>
> Hey <name>!
>
> I came across <company> on >channel> and I'm seriously impressed with what you're doing, congrats on working with <portfolio item>! I run x27 and I'm in charge of growing the number of clients agencies like <company> get.
>
> We do this by putting together a team of specialist marketers with interest and experience in your niche. So in the case of <company>, we'd get the best in design and development to sky-rocket the number of clients you get – even if you don't have a gazillion dollars to spend on marketing.
>
> Our service has been carefully designed and tested rigorously so firms like yours can sky-rocket their inbound leads. To date, we've helped our clients to reach over $10 million worth of business.
>
> I'd love to learn what you're doing right now to find clients- you're structured very closely to our other agency clients- I'm sure we can help you double or triple the amount of client you're getting (Just as we helped Dom & Tom to get McDonald's as a client in less than a month – We're also working with Fueled, Faction, Bluewhale & Maxburst).
>
> If you're ready to get more clients, just get in touch with me directly and I'll set up a quick call 🙂 I'm also more than happy to answer any questions you might have about agency marketing.
>
> Thanks and have an awesome day!
> Alex
>
> p.s. I love your website- it's really slick!

My note: longer and more hype-heavy than `copy-standard.md` would allow ("sky-rocket" twice, "gazillion dollars"). The mechanics that work: the named-client proof line ("Dom & Tom to get McDonald's in less than a month") and the p.s. The body would get cut 40% by our standard. Keep it as a what-real-agencies-send reference, not a model to copy.

> Subject: Your website might be pushing customers away, [First Name]!
> I looked at your website today and ran a few speed and UX/UI tests. You might not see it, but your site could be silently turning visitors away. ## What to take into Adwield's outreach

Every credible operator here lands on the same four moves: (1) one specific, researched observation about THIS prospect, (2) one named result or proof stat, (3) the agency does the work for them, (4) a single ~15-min ask, no deck. The differences are only in how much hype they pad around it — and our `copy-standard.md` already strips the hype. Adwield's edge over all of these: they open with "I have some ideas" / "I help businesses fix that"; Adwield opens with the finished ad already built. The artifact replaces the claim. That's a stronger version of move (2), and none of these operators do it.
