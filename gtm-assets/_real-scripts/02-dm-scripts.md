## 1. Justin Welsh — passive cold DM after a funding round

**Source / proof:** Justin Welsh, solo creator, ex-VP Sales at PatientPop (took it to $0→$100M+ revenue era), $5M+ solo business, 700k+ LinkedIn followers. His own newsletter.
**Direct link:** https://www.justinwelsh.me/newsletter/how-to-find-more-customers-on-linkedin
**Verbatim:**

> Congrats on the recent round, {{FirstName}}. I'm a healthcare technology veteran (ZocDoc/PatientPop) and love what you're doing. Following you all from way over here in Los Angeles.

**Engagement / numbers (his own words, verbatim from same page):**
> Even if I got a 10% response rate, I was putting 50+ new prospects at an average deal size of $25,000 into my pipeline each year.
> That's $1.25M in annual pipeline for spending 15 minutes on Friday mornings.

He adds he "usually got more than a 10% response rate." Mechanic: one Google Alert review every Friday, then 10-12 of these DMs. No pitch, no calendar link. Pure acknowledgment + soft credential.

**Credibility flag:** HIGH. Real operator, real exit, shares the line for free in his own newsletter. The result is self-reported, so treat the $1.25M as his math not an audited number.

## 2. Jed Mahrle — 7 outbound templates (free swipe via Magical)

**Source / proof:** Jed Mahrle, "Practical Prospecting," ex-Head of Outbound Sales at Mailshake, built sales teams, runs a prospecting newsletter/training. Templates hosted free on Magical's library.
**Direct link:** https://www.getmagical.com/templates/jed-mahrles-top-7-email-templates
**Verbatim (placeholders are his):**

**2a. Ask for Feedback**
> Hey [First Name]. Let me preface this by saying I'm not trying to sell you anything or book a meeting. I was hoping to get some feedback from you as someone I would normally sell to. I'm building the sales team at [My Company]. We compete with tools like [Competitors]- I'm sure your team uses one of those. I'm genuinely curious what, if anything, would it take to for you to consider an alternative? Any feedback would be greatly appreciated. Thank you!

**2b. Breakup**
> Hey [first name] - seems like my emails missed the mark for you. No worries! Can you let me know where I wasn't relevant and/or how I might be able to improve in the future? Thanks a ton — [my.fullName]

**2c. Bump with Social Proof**
> [First name] - did you see [resource]? [relevant social proof company] used it and saw [X improvement]. Happy to send it over if you'd like. Thanks,

**2d. Ghost Follow-Up**
> Hey [first name]! Saw you talked to [other reps name] the other week about [problem you solve]. Curious if you had any feedback for us? Helps a lot as our team is still new and growing. Happy to send a gift card for the help. Thanks!

**2e. OOO Reply**
> Hey [first name], wondering if you could help me out while [prospects name] is OOO. Based on [pain point] I thought they might find [solution] relevant. Do you think they'd be open to a conversation or would it make more sense to chat with you first?

**2f. Previous Company Referral**
> Hey [name], no problem. I know it's not your job to help sales people, but do you mind me asking if you think [their previous company] might be a fit considering your experience there?

**2g. Reference Direct Reports**
> [First name] - thought your team might find this helpful given [when they joined / reference of targets]. Typically [direct reports title]'s experience [problem you solve] because [highlight a flaw with the status quo]. We fix this by [solution]. Used by teams like [social proof]. Does this resonate? Thanks, [my.firstName]

**Credibility flag:** HIGH. Working outbound operator, these are the templates he actually runs/teaches, given away free (no course gate). Note: cold-email tone, not LinkedIn-native, but the "ask for feedback" and "breakup" frames port straight to DM.

## 3. Marcos Ruiz — $3M LinkedIn DM flow (qualify-first, no calendar link)

**Source / proof:** Marcos Ruiz, claims $3M+ generated via LinkedIn DMs over 18 months, works with clients doing six-figure months. Published in The Birdhouse newsletter.
**Direct link:** https://www.thebirdhouse.email/p/steal-this-3m-linkedin-dm-script
**Verbatim, in sequence:**

**3a. After connection accepted**
> Hey [Name], really appreciate the connection. I'm curious - are you looking to [desired outcome] or mostly just following along with the content?

**3b. Dig on their reply**
> What do you mean specifically by [their answer]?

**3c. Deeper qualify**
> What part of [their goal] do you feel you need the most guidance with right now?

**3d. Hot inbound reply-handler**
> Hey, thanks for reaching out. Curious what has you interested in [topic]? Also, be sure to check out [free resource]—this will give you a great overview.

**3e. The ask (after 3-4 exchanges)**
> Let me know if this is something you'd be interested in—I can set up a call with my team who can break down exactly how this would work for you.

**Credibility flag:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Real revenue claim but self-reported and the operator is less publicly verifiable than Welsh/Mahrle. The MECHANIC is the gold here: ask → dig → dig → soft ask, never a raw calendar link on a cold thread. That maps directly onto our artifact-first warm-reply tree.

## 4. John Nemo — permission-based LinkedIn opener (two-step)

**Source / proof:** John Nemo, runs Nemo Media Group, LinkedIn lead-gen specialist, author of "LinkedIn Riches," 6+ years selling on the platform. Published on his own LinkedIn article.
**Direct link:** https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/my-best-copy-and-paste-sales-script-john-nemo
**Verbatim:**

**4a. Initial**
> Hey [NAME] - hope you are well! Curious - Are you interested in using LinkedIn to find new clients or customers?

(then offers a free webinar) followed by:
> If you'd like to see how it works, just reply with the word 'YES' [...] If you're not interested, no worries at all.

**4b. After they reply YES**
> Awesome! Here's a link to the free webinar: http://linkedinriches.com/webinar-coaching/ Can't wait to hear your thoughts!

**Credibility flag:** MEDIUM. Long-time LinkedIn operator, but this is "ask permission then drop the funnel link" — works for low-ticket info offers, NOT for our high-touch DFY play. Use only the permission-gate idea ("reply YES"), not the funnel.

**Source / proof:** Sam McKenna, founder/CEO of #samsales Consulting, ex-Head of Enterprise Sales at LinkedIn, ex-VP at On24. SMYKM method cited at ~13x reply rate vs. industry average. The full verbatim email she sent to Amy Volas was published in her own LinkedIn post.

**Engagement on that post:** 140 reactions, 85 comments (visible debate, incl. Nina Jurewicz pushing back on email length — a real skeptical thread, not an echo chamber).

**STATUS: PARAPHRASE of the example structure — I could NOT capture her full email word-for-word from the post.** What's confirmed verbatim is the design checklist she states about that email:

> It's long, It's personal, It shows my personality (hello human connection!), It teaches her, It demonstrates our thinking

And one verbatim humanizing line from the SMYKM example she's published:

Confirmed mechanics of the example (paraphrased, not her exact words): references the prospect's specific recent LinkedIn post ("rules are meant to be broken"), shows research on their prospecting strategy, includes the personal cat line above, and asks for availability WITHOUT dropping a calendar link.

**Credibility flag:** HIGH on the operator (ex-LinkedIn head of enterprise sales is about as battle-tested as it gets). LOW on verbatim — the full email body is gated behind her playbook/masterclass and I only captured the cat line + the checklist exactly. Do NOT present the body as verbatim. ## 6. Jack Reamer / Salesbread — connection-request + cold-message templates (1M+ invites tested)

**Source / proof:** Jack Reamer, CEO of Salesbread (B2B lead-gen agency). Stated results: tested 1M+ invite messages, ~47% connection acceptance, ~36% positive reply rate, ~5,000 qualified leads generated for clients last year, ~1 lead/day per client.
**Direct links:**
- Connection requests: https://salesbread.com/60-linkedin-connection-request-message-templates/
- Cold messages: https://salesbread.com/cold-linkedin-message-templates/

**Verbatim connection notes:**
> Hey [Name], since we're both working in [commercial real estate] here in [San Diego], I'd like to invite you to share my network here on LI.

> [Name], we're looking for a [law firm] like yours to collaborate on an upcoming book.

> Hi [Name]. I noticed we have a common area of interest & would love to connect.

**Verbatim post-connection cold messages:**
> Good to connect [First Name]. By the way, what are your favorite hiking spots these days?

> Good to connect. Now that we are connected, do you mind if I ask about your approach to [XYZ]?

> Good to connect [First Name]. To be transparent, I do [XYZ] and we help with [pain point].

**Credibility flag:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Agency self-reports the numbers (47%/36% over 1M invites) — believable scale, not independently audited. Caveat: the reply-rate stats are aggregate, NOT tied to any single template shown, so don't assume a given line "gets 36%." The "share my network" and "favorite hiking spots" lines are softeners, useful but generic — weaker than Welsh's specificity.
