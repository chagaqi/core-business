## 1. Daniel Fazio (Client Ascension / ListKit) — full 3-email sequence

**Source name:** Daniel Fazio, "Cold Email Wizard." Co-founder, Client Ascension and ListKit.
**Link:** https://medium.com/@danielfazio/the-best-b2b-cold-email-template-ive-ever-used-b28166cf709e
**Proof:** Co-founder of Client Ascension (agency-owner training program) and ListKit (B2B lead-data tool). In the article he states he's "sent well over 100,000 cold emails" for himself and clients and made "hundreds of variations."
**Credibility:** High. Operator publishing his own actual template, not a course teaser. Note: skews to SMB/creator outreach (Instagram angle), so it's a structure to adapt, not lift, for B2B-ops ICPs.

**Email 1**
> Subject: Quick question about @ [[InstagramUsername]]
>
> Hey [[First Name]]. Saw you popping up on my Instagram Explore Page. You're killin it! [[Or some other variation of a compliment that you've done personal research on them]]
>
> Do you need [[Benefit your service provides]]?
>
> I've helped [[# of other businesses in target niche]] get [[benefit your service provides]].
>
> Got time for a quick call this week?

**Email 2** (3 days later)
> Subject: Re: Quick question about @ [[InstagramUsername]]
>
> Hey [[First Name]]. Would you like to see a case study from [[Actual business you provided results for]]?

**Email 3** (3 more days later)
> Subject: Re: Quick question about @ [[InstagramUsername]]
>
> Hey [[First Name]]. Here is exactly how you can achieve [[benefit your service provides]], even If you don't work with us. [Info provided] If you change your mind, let me know if you've got time this week for a quick phone call.

## 3. Nick Abraham 3-layer framework — example email (THIRD-PARTY RECONSTRUCTION, labeled)

**Source name:** ColdSend playbook, reconstructing Nick Abraham's hyper-personalization framework.
**Link:** https://www.coldsend.pro/playbooks/nick-abraham-hyper-personalization-framework
**Proof of the operator:** Same Nick Abraham / Leadbird credentials as #2 (the playbook cites 1.5M+ emails/mo, 40%+ positive-reply targets, ~200 clients).
**Credibility:** Medium. This is ColdSend's *written reconstruction* of Nick's three layers (intent signal + colleague reference + AI personalization line), NOT Nick's own published copy. Use it as a structural example only.

> [!note] LABELED: written by ColdSend as an illustration of Nick's framework, not Nick's verbatim words.
> Subject: Quick question about [company]
>
> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> Noticed you follow Clay on LinkedIn - guessing you're dealing with lead generation challenges like most companies scaling past 50 employees.
>
> Not sure if this should go to you or {{colleague_name}} on your sales team, but figured one of you might find this relevant.
>
> We just helped Zapier increase their qualified leads by 40% using a similar approach to what Clay teaches, but specifically for companies your size.
>
> Worth a quick chat to see if it's relevant for {{company}}?
>
> Best,
> [Your name]
>
> PS: Saw you're hiring SDRs - probably perfect timing for this conversation.

The three layers it demonstrates: (1) intent signal from a LinkedIn follow, (2) colleague-uncertainty line to manufacture "is this even me?" curiosity, (3) a personalization line tied to company size/hiring.

## 4. Jason Bay (Outbound Squad) — "Past Employee / Current Client" referral opener

**Source name:** Jason Bay. Founder & CEO, Outbound Squad (outbound-sales training).
**Link:** Template pack: https://www.getmagical.com/templates/jbays-cold-email-starter-pack — template walk-through and example surfaced via Outbound Squad / 30MPC tactics (https://tactics.30mpc.com/steal-the-cold-email-campaign-that-gets-15-replies).
**Proof:** Founder/CEO of Outbound Squad; trains B2B sales teams; co-ran the 30MPC/Outbound Squad webinar on a drip that gets "15%+ reply rate."
**Credibility:** High operator. The verbatim worked example below comes from his teaching of the "noticed you were [title] at [old company]" referral play.

Template skeleton (verbatim structure):
> Noticed you were [title] at [old company, current client of yours] back in [year]. We're working with them to help [results or problems].

Verbatim worked example he gives:
> Hey Katie, noticed you were Director of Sales at Zoom back in 2018. We're working with Jason Bay over there right now to help them increase the percentage of meetings scheduled from their cold outreach from 1-9%. Thought their learnings could help with the work you're doing at Medallia.

His "Reply Method" structure (verbatim component labels from the 30MPC formula): Hook (lead with prospect priorities/problems) → Social Proof → Offer (give value, don't just ask for time) → CTA. Backed by his cited data: emails under 100 words perform best; personalization ~5x replies; problem-focused language +20%; social proof +41%; compelling offer +28%.
**Link (data + method):** https://www.30mpc.com/newsletter/the-data-backed-cold-email-formula-the-exact-words-length

## 5. Will Allred / Lavender — three framework examples (Mouse Trap, Vanilla Ice Cream, Breakup)

**Source name:** Will Allred, co-founder of Lavender (AI email-coaching tool). Examples published in Lavender's framework compilation.
**Link:** https://www.lavender.ai/blog/sales-email-frameworks
**Proof:** Co-founder of Lavender; the company markets itself on having analyzed "millions" / "billions" of sales emails. Will Allred is a widely-followed (280k+) sales-email voice on LinkedIn.
**Credibility:** High for data-backed structure. Lavender explicitly calls these "frameworks, not templates" with "light personalization," so subject lines aren't paired — these are body openers to adapt.

**The Mouse Trap with Context** (verbatim body):
> Will, looks like you're hiring reps. Would it be helpful to get a more granular look at how they're ramping on email? Our in-inbox email coach, Lavender, helps teams ramp faster.

**Vanilla Ice Cream** (verbatim body):

**The Breakup** (verbatim body):
> Hey George, I've reached out a few times because you're hiring so many new reps. I thought our email coach could help. I'm going to chalk it up to my timing being off...

## 6. Josh Braun — "Poke the Bear" (cold email + cold call)

**Source name:** Josh Braun. Founder, Braun Sales Academy; ex-Basecamp/Jellyvision sales leader; well-known "ditch the pitch" voice.
**Link (email principles + generator):** https://joshbraun.com/poke-generator/ and https://joshbraun.com/poke-the-bear-cold-call-script/
**Proof:** Runs Braun Sales Academy; ~284k LinkedIn followers; long track record teaching B2B outbound.
**Credibility:** High operator-educator. The verbatim lines below are his own published examples.

Verbatim "poke the bear" email line (a neutral question that illuminates a problem):
> We know that around 50% of cold emails land in spam. How are you reducing the risk of emails landing in spam today?

Verbatim cold-call opener (same framework, transferable to email opener logic):
> Hi Pete, Beth Jones with CaptivateIQ. We've never spoken before. And I know you weren't expecting my call, but I was hoping you could help me out for a moment.

Verbatim poke line from the call:
> I often hear that for orgs your size, it takes a few days to calculate and send statements to reps. How does that compare with your experience?

Core principle (his words): "Ditch the pitch. Poke the bear." Ask a neutral question that gets the prospect to question their status quo instead of leading them into a pitch.

## 7. HubSpot library — the "100 best customers" template (documented results)

**Source name:** HubSpot Sales Blog (public library). The template is the well-circulated Bryan Kreuzberger / Breakthrough Email script.
**Link:** https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/the-cold-email-template-that-won-16-new-b2b-customers-1
**Proof:** HubSpot is a public CRM vendor with a heavily-trafficked sales blog; this specific template is documented with results on the page.
**Credibility:** Medium-high. Vendor library, but with attached hard numbers and a long real-world track record.

Verbatim template:
> Hello [Prospect Name],
>
> I have an idea that I can explain in 10 minutes that can get [company] its next 100 best customers.
>
> I recently used this idea to help our client [SaaS company/competitor] almost triple their monthly run rate.
>
> [First name], let's schedule a quick 10-minute call so I can share the idea with you. When works best for you?
>
> - [Your Name]

**Documented results on the page:** 57% open rate, 21% response rate, 16 new customers acquired.
