# Agency-Scale Client Acquisition — operator systems + scale proof

How owners who actually built outbound + sales engines did it. For Adwield (AI-native performance-ads agency selling DTC operators). Goal: separate verifiable operators from course-sellers, extract the real method, flag where 2026 breaks the old playbook.

**The one distinction that matters.** Almost everyone in this space sells a course. The question is whether they built a real client-services business *first* and at *what scale*, or whether the "agency" is mostly the marketing wrapper for the course. Ranking by hardness of proof:

1. **Hormozi** — verifiable at the top. Gym Launch sold for $46.2M, Acquisition.com is a $200M+/yr portfolio. Hardest evidence in the set.
2. **Berman / Experiment 27** — real agency, public numbers, documented method. (Relevant because he is the co-founder tie behind Robert Indries' strongest credential.)
3. **Sam Ovens** — real operator history (SnapInspect, sold; a $35K/mo consultancy) before Consulting.com. Course is now the main business but the foundation was real.
4. **Platten, Morgan, Kaplan, Indries** — real-ish operator histories, but the *scale* claims are self-reported and the course is clearly the main event. Use the method, discount the numbers.

A recurring weak-proof signal runs through tier 4: the ClickFunnels **"Two Comma Club" award is self-attested gross revenue, not profit, not audited.** A screenshot of $1M through a funnel earns it. Treat it as "ran money through a funnel once," not "built a durable business." Flagged per-operator below.

## 1. Alex Hormozi — $100M Leads (the methodology spine) — VERIFIED SCALE

**Proof of scale (strongest in the set).** Founded Gym Launch, sold a 66% stake to American Pacific Group in 2021, took ~$42M in distributions, headline figure $46.2M. Runs Acquisition.com, a 16-company portfolio at $200M+/yr as of 2023. Also founded ALAN (software) and Prestige Labs (supplements), both divested. Co-owns Skool with Sam Ovens. This is an operator who built and exited real businesses, then wrote the book. Multiple independent sources concur (Celebrity Net Worth, Hampton, startup press).

**Important caveat for us.** His scale came from *licensing* a service model (Gym Launch packaged sales letters + ad creative + SOPs to gym owners), not from running a cold-outbound agency. So $100M Leads is the best free framework available, but it is written for info-products and broad SMB lead-gen, not specifically for selling 5-figure agency retainers to DTC operators. Use it as the lead-gen taxonomy; use Dan Englander (doc 02 in the sibling folder) for the actual high-ticket-outbound motion.

### The Core Four (the lead-gen taxonomy)
Only four ways to get a stranger to become a lead. Everything else is a variant:
1. **Warm outreach** — people who know you.
2. **Post free content** — one-to-many to a built audience.
3. **Cold outreach** — strangers, one-to-one.
4. **Paid ads** — strangers, one-to-many.

### Warm outreach — the ACA framework
For people who already know you. Don't pitch. **A.C.A.: Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask.** Strike up a genuine conversation, listen, then ask if they (or anyone they know) has the problem you solve. Frame around the *problem*, not the offer. This is the zero-cost first channel.

### Cold outreach — the numbers
- **Personalize via research before first contact.**
- **Follow up at least 2–3 times** if no reply on message 1 (lighter than Hormozi's spoken "8–12 touches" advice elsewhere; sources vary, flag).
- Benchmark he cites: roughly a **1/20 conversion** from cold email effort.

### The Rule of 100 (the activity engine)
**Do 100 primary marketing actions every day for 100 consecutive days.** Pick one: 100 cold outreaches/day, OR 100 minutes of content/day, OR $100/day on ads. This is the anti-fragile habit layer. Maps 1:1 to Englander's "habits over outcomes." For a solo operator in a 90-day sprint, this is the single most directly applicable Hormozi mechanic.

### Lead magnet — the 7-step formula
The free thing that converts a stranger to a lead. Steps: (1) pick a narrow problem, (2) solve it completely, (3) choose the delivery format, (4) name it well, (5) make it easy to consume, (6) make it over-deliver on perceived value, (7) add a clear CTA into the paid offer. Core principle: the **Problem-Solution Cycle** ("salty pretzel" metaphor). Solve a small problem free; that reveals the next, bigger problem, which is your paid offer. The free pretzel creates the thirst you sell the drink for.

**For Adwield:** the free 15-sec ad built from the prospect's own reviews *is* the lead magnet. It solves "I have no fresh creative" completely, reveals "now I need a system that ships these every two weeks" (the paid offer). This is textbook pretzel. Already in the playbook; Hormozi names why it works.

### Lead Getters — the scale layer (later, not now)
Four people who run the Core Four *for* you: customers (referrals), employees, affiliates, agencies. "More, Better, New" = the three ways to grow any channel (do more of it, do it better, or add a new one). This is the scale-past-solo layer. Gated for Chaga until first cash; note it exists.

**Verdict:** Real scale, free book, foundational taxonomy. Not a high-ticket-outbound playbook on its own. Spine for *thinking*, not for *exact outbound execution*.

## 2. Alex Berman — Experiment 27 / X27 (cold-email-built agency) — VERIFIED, HIGH-FIT

Berman is here because he is the **most method-transferable real operator in the set** and the credibility behind Robert Indries (they co-founded Experiment 27). His agency was *built by* the exact motion Adwield needs.

**Proof of scale.** Built Experiment 27 to **$400K ARR in 30 days** using cold email. Claims **$12M+ in B2B sales for his own agencies** and **$100M+ in leads generated for clients.** Author of *The Cold Email Manifesto*. Numbers are self-reported but the agency, the client roster, and the method are public and consistent across years of his content and the book. Stronger than tier 4, softer than Hormozi.

### The cold email structure (verbatim components)
A winning Experiment 27 cold email has five parts:
1. **Subject line** — personalized, references the prospect's company name.
2. **Compliment** — one specific, genuine line about their work.
3. **Case study** — the proof. The documented example: *"we helped [a developer] bring on McDonald's and close an extra $1,000,000 in 6 months."*
4. **Call to action** — single, low-friction ask for a short call.
5. **Signature.**

### The 3C framework (why it works)
Every cold email must answer the three questions a prospect has on open:
- **Why me?** (personalization / the compliment)
- **Why should I believe you?** (the case study)
- **What do you want?** (the single CTA)

Keep it short. The whole email earns the reply, not the sale.

### Documented performance
- **17.5% meeting-book rate** from one campaign of 40 leads (small n, flag it, but directionally real and consistent with laser-pointer math).
- The "send to a tight, researched list" approach, not blast.

**For Adwield:** this is the cleanest verbatim cold-email skeleton in the brief and it maps directly onto our artifact-first motion. Our **artifact replaces the case study** in slot 3. We don't have a McDonald's story; we have a finished ad built from their reviews. Same job (Berman's "why should I believe you"), stronger because it's about *them*. Subject = personalized to their account, compliment = the specific ad/angle tell, CTA = "watch this, if it holds up that's the conversation."

**2026 flag:** the 17.5% book rate is from a low-AI-saturation era. See section 7. The *structure* survives; the *response rate* has been cut by more than half industry-wide since then.

## 8. Where 2026 breaks the old playbook (the most important section)

Most of the above was built in a 2018–2022 window of cheap, working cold outreach. That window closed. Hard, multi-source data:

- **Cold email reply rates collapsed: ~8.5% (2019) → ~3.43% (2026).** More than halved.
- **40%+ of all cold email is now AI-generated.** Buyers developed a "delete reflex." **73% of professionals delete templated messages on sight.**
- **Mass/untargeted outreach is dead.** Campaigns to 500+ recipients average ~2.1% reply. Blasting 10,000 generic emails *actively damages* sender reputation. Joel Kaplan's "100,000 AI messages/month" is the textbook anti-pattern.
- **Deliverability is now the #1 variable, ahead of subject line and copy.** Google/Yahoo DMARC enforcement, lower per-inbox volume, stricter spam filtering.
- **The category's own builder agrees:** Jordan Platten, who created the SMMA-course category, now says "SMMA is dying."

**What still works in 2026 (the consensus):** precision targeting (small, researched lists), multi-channel sequencing, technically sound deliverability infrastructure, and personalization that doesn't read as AI. This is **exactly the laser-pointer model** from Englander (sibling doc 02), now mandatory rather than optional.

**The synthesis for Adwield.** Every operator above teaches some version of *volume + light personalization*. 2026 killed the volume half. The surviving half is *high-personalization, low-volume, artifact-led*. Adwield's whole motion (a finished personalized ad as the opener) is the *natively-2026* version of all of this. We're not doing a worse version of the old SMMA playbook; we're doing the thing the old playbook is now being forced to become. The artifact does what the "Two Comma case study" and the "personalized Loom" were trying to do, except it's undeniable proof *and* it survives the AI-slop delete reflex because it's a real deliverable, not a templated message.
