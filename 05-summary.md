# The Six Ideas — Plain English Summary

**Updated:** 2026-05-20

A quick gist of each so you can scan and reorient. The deep writeups live in `01-shortlist.md`.

---

## 1. Presale Customer Service Agent

A Shopify app that handles customer support specifically for brands running presales, preorders, drops, and made-to-order products. When a brand collects payment 60-120 days before shipping, customer anxiety builds and support volume explodes 5-10x — generic bots like Gorgias make it worse because they sound robotic about an emotional "where is my order" question. This agent is the opposite: domain-specific empathy, production-timeline knowledge, and reassurance language tuned to each vertical (sneaker drops vs. crowdfunded hardware vs. bourbon allocations sound very different).

The sales pitch is that you only pay if the agent saves you money — base subscription plus a percentage of refunds prevented. Distribution is the Shopify App Store and direct outreach to BackerKit alumni (Kickstarter graduates moving to Shopify). The moat is that Chaga has shipped presale product himself and knows the exact emotional arc at day 45, 60, and 89 — that experience is the prompt-engineering edge. Generic competitors won't build this because their mid-market customer doesn't run presales. Most defensible idea on the list for his specific background.

## 2. Local Makers Corporate Gifts

A B2B marketplace that aggregates hyperlocal artisans (jam, honey, ceramics, soap, candles, coffee roasters) into curated city-themed gift boxes — "Taste of Austin," "Best of Denver" — sold to HR teams, real estate agents, and sales teams as recurring corporate gifts. The arbitrage is that three markets don't talk to each other: HR teams need authentic gifts but have no time to source, artisans want predictable B2B contracts instead of farmers-market gambling, and nobody operates the aggregation layer because logistics across 20+ small makers is hard.

The pitch is "stop giving forgettable logo-stamped mugs" — boxes ship at $80-150 retail with $30-50 margin per box, and the long-term play is quarterly subscription programs. Chaga's moat is operational: he ran a $2M ecom warehouse with in-house shipping, which is the exact stack required to coordinate inventory from 20 makers. Ideabrowser flagged this as capital-intensive (inventory risk), so the entire business hinges on negotiating consignment terms with artisans before committing budget.

## 3. ChurnDetective for Indie SaaS

An AI agent that watches customer behavior, support chats, and product usage to flag who's about to cancel and suggest retention plays in real time. The wedge is the indie / micro-SaaS segment ($5K-$50K MRR) that's currently underserved — enterprise tools like Gainsight cost $20-100K/year, while indie founders use nothing and watch LTV leak. The 2024-2025 wave of indie builders just hit the retention wall: they can acquire customers but their first 50 customers all churned within 4 months and they don't know why.

Pricing is $79-$199/mo for the indie tier, $499+ for boutique SaaS portfolios. Distribution is Indie Hackers, the X founder community, and MicroAcquire. This was the highest Hormozi value-score in the completed research (8/10) because it combines real-time intervention with recurring revenue and low buyer effort. But it's also the most crowded competitive landscape — Retention.ai, ChurnBuster, ExitInsight, Totango all play here. The indie niche-down is the defense. Architect-friendly because it's machine-from-day-one; no service component required.

## 4. First100 — Productized Customer Acquisition Agency

A productized agency that guarantees a startup its first 100 qualified leads in a 2-week sprint. Most early-stage startups die at the customer acquisition step, not the product step — they have cash from savings or pre-seed but no playbook and no time. Existing demand-gen agencies sell only to Series A+ companies at $10-25K/mo retainers, leaving the pre-seed segment underserved. The product is a repeatable vertical playbook (start with B2B SaaS post-MVP, pre-Series A) plus AI-driven outreach plus manual hustle.

Pricing is three-layer: $7,500 service fee (Chaga's margin) + pass-through ad budget the client owns directly + performance bonus per lead over baseline. Sales cycle is 2-6 weeks per client; 2-3 sprints per month gets to $20K/mo. The watch-out from Chaga's archetype profile is real: this is a service business and Architects under revenue pressure default to doing all the delivery themselves. By month 3 he'd need an operator hire to avoid building a job instead of a machine. Fastest of the five ideas to revenue.

## 5. AI-Native Performance Ads Agency

An ads agency for ecom and DTC brands that produces AI-generated UGC-style video creatives — synthetic avatars, AI voiceovers, prompt-engineered hooks — at a fraction of the cost and 10-20x the velocity of traditional UGC. Where a traditional UGC shop charges $200-500 per video with a 2-week turnaround for one creator, this agency produces 20 variations across 20 synthetic creators in 48 hours. Top-performing ad creatives on Meta and TikTok in 2026 are increasingly AI-generated, so the arbitrage is real but closing fast.

The foot-in-the-door pitch is the killer wedge: "Give us 10% of your existing ad spend. We'll match or beat your current creatives' KPIs with AI UGC. If we don't, you owe us nothing." Zero friction to the yes. Once proven, it scales to a management retainer + creative-only tier + performance bonus. Chaga's long-term play is partnering with a creative strategist (revenue share, no cash) who layers brand storytelling onto the AI engine, doubling the perceived value per engagement. Risk: synthetic UGC has an uncanny-valley problem in authenticity-dependent verticals like beauty and fashion — pick verticals where it works (consumer tech, supplements, productivity).

## 6. AI Code Assistant for GTM Engineers (Nimbalyst-inspired)

A SaaS product with a GUI-driven AI code assistant built specifically for GTM engineers and technical marketers — the emerging role that combines marketing strategy with the technical chops to build modern outbound and revenue-ops automation stacks (Clay, n8n, Apollo, Cargo, Smartlead, Make, custom Python). Generic AI coding tools like Cursor and Copilot don't understand the GTM stack semantically — they don't know what Clay does versus n8n versus Cargo, can't generate a working enrichment pipeline from a prompt, and don't have the templates that 90% of GTM work draws from. This product is the purpose-built alternative.

The product offers AI-assisted workflow generation, semantic knowledge of GTM tools, and one-click deploys for common patterns: lead enrichment pipelines, signal-based outbound triggers, lead scoring models, ABM list builds, CRM hygiene, attribution stitching. Target audience is GTM engineers at $1M-$50M ARR B2B SaaS companies, growth agency operators, and RevOps teams who want to automate without hiring developers. Pricing $99-$499/mo per seat. Distribution: the rapidly growing GTM engineer Twitter and LinkedIn community (Adam Robinson, Mark Kosoglow, Pocus, Common Room, Default, Clay community, RevOps subreddits). Founder fit: Chaga's marketing and sales chops handle the GTM domain, but this needs a strong technical co-builder for the engineering side — Architect's classic partnership play.
